### 1.1 The Cell of Origin

```
Claudin-low breast cancer originates from
one of two cell populations, and the distinction
matters for the therapeutic logic:

ORIGIN A — MAMMARY STEM CELL (direct origin):
  A cell that has never committed to a
  luminal or basal identity.
  It has never executed the somatic
  epigenetic programme that accompanies
  differentiation.
  It retains the methylation pattern of
  a primitive cell — germline-adjacent.
  When it becomes cancerous, it carries
  that primitive epigenetic state with it.

  This is Pommier subgroup 1 (2020).
  This is the MEMORY-LOW subgroup.
  This is the deepest attractor.
  This is the TYPE 4 root lock in its
  purest form.

ORIGIN B — EPITHELIAL-MESENCHYMAL TRANSITION:
  A luminal or basal progenitor that
  underwent EMT — lost its epithelial
  identity programme and acquired
  mesenchymal/stem-like features.
  The cell was committed.
  Something pushed it backward and
  into the mesenchymal state.

  This is Pommier subgroups 2 and 3.
  This is the MEMORY-HIGH subgroup.
  These cells retain partial FOXA1/GATA3
  expression — residual memory of
  the identity they held before EMT.
  This is a shallower attractor than
  Origin A, even though the surface
  phenotype is similar.

THE DIAGNOSTIC CONSEQUENCE:
  Both origins produce tumours that are:
    ER-negative
    PR-negative
    HER2-negative (triple-negative)
    Claudin-low (CLDN3/4/7 absent)
    CD44-high, CD24-low
    Mesenchymal (VIM, FN1, SNAI1 elevated)

  from the surface.

  They are geometrically distinct.

    FOXA1 / GATA3 / SPDEF expression
    (the lineage memory TFs)
    CT antigen expression
    (GAGE family, CT45, STRA8, DPPA2)
    Treg infiltration
    (FOXP3/CD8A ratio)
    Attractor depth score

  ORIGIN A (memory-low):
    FOXA1 / GATA3 / SPDEF absent
    CT antigen elevated
    FOXP3/CD8A ratio high
    Depth score high

  ORIGIN B (memory-high):
    FOXA1 / GATA3 / SPDEF partially retained
    CT antigen lower
    FOXP3/CD8A ratio lower
    Depth score lower

  The distinction is the most important
  thing to establish for the individual patient.
  It determines the therapeutic logic.

### 1.3 The Depth Score — What It Measures and What It Means

```

  POSITIVE AXIS (deeper = more of these):
    VIM   — mesenchymal identity
    CD44  — stemness
    SNAI1 — EMT master regulator
    ZEB1  — EMT master regulator
    FN1   — extracellular matrix remodelling

  NEGATIVE AXIS (deeper = less of these):
    CLDN3 — luminal epithelial tight junction
    CLDN4 — luminal epithelial tight junction
    CLDN7 — luminal epithelial tight junction
    CDH1  — E-cadherin (epithelial adhesion)
    ESR1  — oestrogen receptor (luminal identity)

  The depth score = mean(positive z-scores)
                   minus mean(negative z-scores)
  Normalised within the CL population.

WHAT THE SCORE MEANS GEOMETRICALLY:

  A high depth score means:
    The mesenchymal/stem programme is fully engaged.
    The luminal programme is completely absent.
    The cell is at the floor of the primitive attractor.
    The distance to the nearest commitment checkpoint
    is maximal.

  A low depth score means:
    The mesenchymal programme is present but partial.
    Some luminal markers are partially retained.
    The cell is closer to the commitment boundary.
    The distance to nearest commitment checkpoint
    is smaller.

  Confirmed across three independent cohorts:

    HR = 2.098, p = 0.025

  The convergent HR range across three cohorts

### 2.2 Axis 2 — Memory TF / CT Antigen (Structural/Mechanistic)

```
Established by Script 3 (BRCA-S7h).
Confirmed externally in METABRIC (BRCA-S7l, EXT-P6).

The memory axis runs from:
  MEMORY-HIGH: FOXA1/GATA3/SPDEF partially retained
               CT antigen lower
               Pommier subgroups 2/3 (EMT origin)
  ↓
  MEMORY-LOW: FOXA1/GATA3/SPDEF absent
              CT antigen elevated
              Pommier subgroup 1 (direct stem origin)

THIS AXIS DOES NOT INDEPENDENTLY PREDICT OS
IN UNTREATED PRE-CHECKPOINT PATIENTS.

Memory-low OS = memory-high OS in both TCGA
(p=0.878) and METABRIC (p=0.533, EXT-P7).

This null is not a failure of the axis.
It is a biological signal.

The immune cancellation interpretation
(established in BRCA-S7h, confirmed by EXT-P7):
  Memory-low cells have more CT antigen.
  More CT antigen recruits more immune activity.
  More immune activity recruits more Tregs.
  More Tregs suppress the immune activity.
  Net OS effect: zero.
  The pro- and anti-immune forces cancel.

Remove the Treg block and the CT antigen
recognition is unleashed.
Memory-low cells become the highest-priority
immunotherapy responders — precisely because
they have the most antigen and the most
suppressible immune block.

The null OS in untreated patients is the
mechanism by which memory-low patients
become the highest anti-TIGIT benefit group.
The null is not a dead end.
It is the signpost to the therapeutic prediction.
```
