## SECTION I — THE BIOLOGICAL GROUNDING

```
THE NORMAL LUMINAL CELL:
  The cell type this cancer should be.
  Mature ductal epithelial cell.
  Hormone-responsive.
  Slow cycling.
  Estrogen-dependent for survival.
  Expressing ESR1, FOXA1, GATA3
  at high levels.
  Expressing PGR (PR) downstream of ESR1.
  Expressing CCND1 (cyclin D1)
  as an estrogen-responsive gene.
  Genomically stable.
  Dependent on ESR1 axis for identity
  and for survival.

THE LUMINAL A FALSE ATTRACTOR:
  This is what needs to be reconceptualized.
  Luminal A cells are not stuck BELOW
  the luminal threshold.
  They are stuck AT the luminal threshold —
  unable to exit through normal senescence.
  They continue cycling in the correct
  luminal identity.

  The false attractor in Luminal A is:
    The sustained activation of the
    ESR1-driven proliferative programme
    in the absence of normal cell cycle exit.

  The gate that is broken is not the
  luminal identity gate.
  It is the SENESCENCE and CELL CYCLE
  EXIT gate.

  This changes the geometry completely.

THE DEPTH AXIS IN LUMINAL A:
  If the switch genes (FOXA1/GATA3/ESR1)
  are retained and even amplified,
  depth in Luminal A cannot be measured
  by switch gene suppression.

  Depth in Luminal A measures:
    How far has the cell moved from
    normal regulated luminal cycling
    toward uncontrolled luminal proliferation?

  The depth axis is a WITHIN-LUMINAL
  axis — not a luminal-to-basal axis.

  High depth Luminal A:
    Strong ESR1 activity.
    Strong CCND1/CDK4 activation.
    Absent or weak RB1 (retinoblastoma).
    Absent or weak CDKN2A (p16/ARF).
    Sustained cell cycle entry.
    Possibly PIK3CA-mutant (PI3K/AKT
    pathway activation).

  Low depth Luminal A:
    ESR1 active but regulated.
    Normal RB1 function.
    Normal p16 expression.
    Cell cycle regulated by estrogen
    but still responsive to
    anti-proliferative signals.
    May be treatable by endocrine
    therapy alone.
```

### PREDICTION 1 — SWITCH GENES: RETAINED OR ELEVATED

```
PREDICTION:
  In Cancer LumA SC vs Mature Luminal,
  the canonical luminal switch genes
  will NOT be suppressed.
  They will be retained or elevated.

  This is already suggested by the
  GSE176078 secondary comparison.
  The TCGA-BRCA PAM50 Luminal A cohort
  must confirm this independently.

SPECIFIC PREDICTIONS:

  FOXA1:
    PREDICTION: Retained or elevated
    vs adjacent normal.
    Expected direction: FLAT or UP.
    If DOWN: prediction wrong.
    Threshold for "retained": <20% reduction.

  GATA3:
    PREDICTION: Retained or elevated.
    Expected direction: FLAT or UP.
    Reasoning: GATA3 is THE luminal A
    identity TF. It is the most mutated
    gene in ER+ breast cancer — but
    mutations are missense mutations that
    often GAIN function, not loss of
    function. GATA3 should be AT or ABOVE
    normal luminal levels in Luminal A.

  ESR1:
    PREDICTION: Retained — near normal
    or slightly elevated in some cases.
    Expected direction: FLAT.
    Reasoning: ESR1 is the definition of
    ER+ breast cancer. If ESR1 were
    suppressed, it would not be ER+.
    ESR1 will be at or near normal
    luminal levels in Luminal A cancer.

  PGR (Progesterone Receptor):
    PREDICTION: Elevated or retained.
    PGR is directly transcribed by ESR1.
    It is a readout of active ESR1 signaling.
    In Luminal A, ESR1 is active and
    driving its downstream programme.
    PGR should be AT or ABOVE normal levels.
    This is the estrogen-responsive gene
    most directly controlled by ESR1.

WHAT THESE PREDICTIONS MEAN:
  If confirmed — Luminal A is structurally
  unlike every other cancer in this series.
  Every prior cancer had suppressed switch
  genes. Luminal A does not.
  This is the first cancer in the series
  where the depth axis is not defined by
  switch gene loss.
  That is a finding, not a failure.
```

### PREDICTION 2 — THE DEPTH AXIS IS PROLIFERATIVE, NOT DIFFERENTIATION-BASED

```
PREDICTION:
  The depth score in Luminal A will
  correlate with PROLIFERATIVE genes,
  not with luminal identity loss.

SPECIFIC GENE PREDICTIONS:

  MKI67 (Ki-67):
    PREDICTION: ELEVATED in Luminal A
    vs normal luminal.
    Expected change: +50% to +300%.
    Reasoning: Ki-67 is the clinical
    standard for Luminal A vs Luminal B
    separation. It is elevated in all
    malignant cells. Even in Luminal A
    (low Ki-67 by clinical threshold
    of <14%), it will be elevated vs
    truly quiescent mature luminal cells.
    Prediction: positive and significant.

  CCND1 (Cyclin D1):
    PREDICTION: ELEVATED in Luminal A.
    Expected change: +50% to +200%.
    Reasoning: CCND1 is directly regulated
    by ESR1 — it is the bridge between
    estrogen signaling and cell cycle entry.
    In Luminal A, the ESR1 programme is
    driving sustained CCND1 expression.
    CCND1 amplification is present in
    ~15% of Luminal A. Even without
    amplification, expression will be
    elevated vs mature luminal.

  CDK4:
    PREDICTION: ELEVATED in Luminal A.
    CDK4 partners with CCND1 to
    phosphorylate RB1 and drive
    S-phase entry. It is the partner
    of CCND1 and will co-elevate.
    This is the CDK4/6 inhibitor target —
    the most important drug class in
    Luminal A metastatic disease.

  MYC:
    PREDICTION: ELEVATED in Luminal A.
    Not a scaffold oncogene here —
    or is it? In the TNBC analysis MYC
    was flat (scaffold behaviour).
    In Luminal A, MYC amplification
    is present in ~16%. MYC elevation
    in Luminal A may track with depth.
    Prediction: moderately elevated.
    This is uncertain — flagged for
    honest accounting after data loads.

THE DEPTH SCORE HYPOTHESIS:
  Within the Luminal A population,
  depth = degree of proliferative
  drive uncoupled from normal
  estrogen-regulated cell cycle control.

  Depth formula (predicted):
    High MKI67 + High CCND1 + Low RB1
    → High depth score
    → These cells are furthest from
      normal regulated luminal cycling
    → These are the cells most likely
      to recur late
    → These are the cells that need
      CDK4/6 inhibition in addition
      to endocrine therapy
```

### PREDICTION 3 — THE EPIGENETIC LOCK IS DIFFERENT FROM TNBC

```
PREDICTION:
  The epigenetic lock in Luminal A is NOT
  the EZH2/PRC2 mechanism that locks TNBC.

  EZH2:
    PREDICTION: NOT significantly elevated
    in Luminal A vs normal luminal.
    Expected direction: FLAT or slightly up.
    Reasoning: EZH2 is the PRC2 enzyme
    that silences developmental genes.
    In Luminal A, the developmental genes
    (FOXA1/GATA3/ESR1) are NOT silenced.
    Therefore EZH2 is not the relevant
    epigenetic lock in Luminal A.
    If EZH2 is flat in Luminal A while
    being +269.7% in TNBC — that
    confirms the subtype-specificity
    of the lock mechanism.
    This is the most direct test of
    the lock specificity hypothesis.

  WHAT IS THE LOCK IN LUMINAL A?
    Not epigenetic silencing of identity.
    The lock in Luminal A is:
    Constitutive activation of the
    cell cycle through the ESR1-CCND1-
    CDK4/6-RB1 axis.

    The cancer cell has:
      ESR1 constitutively active
      (estrogen-independent in some cases)
      CCND1 constitutively elevated
      CDK4 active
      RB1 phosphorylated = inactivated
    The cell cycle is permanently
    open in the G1/S direction.
    The cell cannot exit G1.
    It keeps entering S phase.
    It keeps dividing in the luminal
    identity programme.

    This is the Luminal A false attractor
    in mechanistic terms:
    The cell is stuck in a loop of
    luminal identity + continuous
    cell cycle entry.
    The exit gate (RB1 active → G0)
    is permanently shut.

  RB1:
    PREDICTION: SUPPRESSED in Luminal A
    vs normal luminal.
    Expected direction: DOWN.
    Reasoning: RB1 is the master cell
    cycle exit switch. When RB1 is active
    (unphosphorylated), it prevents
    S phase entry. In Luminal A, RB1
    is constitutively phosphorylated
    by CCND1-CDK4 → functionally
    inactivated → but not transcriptionally
    suppressed necessarily.
    This prediction is the most uncertain
    in this set — RB1 loss may be
    post-translational rather than
    transcriptional.
    Flagged: may not appear as
    transcriptional suppression.
    If RB1 mRNA is NOT suppressed —
    that is correct biology and an
    analyst assumption error.

  CDKN2A (p16):
    PREDICTION: SUPPRESSED in Luminal A.
    p16 inhibits CDK4/6.
    If p16 is lost, CCND1-CDK4 runs
    without brake.
    CDKN2A loss is known in some
    Luminal A cases.
    Prediction: moderately suppressed.
    Uncertain — flagged.
```

### PREDICTION 4 — THE PIK3CA AXIS

```
PIK3CA mutation is present in ~45%
of Luminal A. It is the most common
driver mutation.

PIK3CA activates the PI3K/AKT/mTOR
pathway — a growth and survival
signalling axis.

PREDICTION:
  Genes in the PI3K/AKT downstream
  programme will be elevated in
  Luminal A cancer vs normal luminal.

SPECIFIC PREDICTIONS:

  AKT1/AKT2/AKT3:
    PREDICTION: Moderately elevated
    in Luminal A.
    AKT is downstream of PIK3CA.
    PIK3CA mutation → AKT activation.
    The protein is activated by
    phosphorylation — not necessarily
    by transcriptional elevation.
    This prediction is uncertain.
    May not appear at mRNA level.
    Flagged as uncertain.

  MTOR:
    PREDICTION: Flat or slightly elevated.
    Same caveat — post-translational
    activation is not detectable
    by mRNA alone.
    The downstream readout (S6K, 4EBP1)
    is not measurable by bulk RNA-seq.

  NOTE:
    The PIK3CA axis predictions are
    the most uncertain in this set.
    PIK3CA is activated by mutation —
    a gain-of-function point mutation —
    not by transcriptional elevation.
    mRNA levels of PIK3CA do not
    reliably indicate mutation status.
    This will likely be an analyst
    assumption error for the
    post-translational predictions.
    Stated here honestly before data loads.
```

### PREDICTION 5 — THE DEPTH SCORE PREDICTS LATE RECURRENCE RISK

```
THIS IS THE PRIMARY CLINICAL PREDICTION.
It is the most important prediction
this analysis can make.

BACKGROUND:
  Luminal A breast cancer has late
  recurrence — disease that returns
  10 to 20 years after initial diagnosis
  and treatment.
  This is unique among breast cancer subtypes.
  TNBC recurs early (3-5 years) or not at all.
  Luminal A recurs late — or not at all.
  The 20-year recurrence rate in Luminal A
  is approximately 10-20% despite
  endocrine therapy.

THE PREDICTION:
  The attractor depth score in Luminal A
  will correlate with late recurrence risk.

  High depth (high MKI67 + high CCND1 +
  high CCND1:CDK4 expression):
    → These tumours are more deeply
      embedded in the proliferative
      programme
    → Higher late recurrence risk
    → These are the patients who need
      extended endocrine therapy
      (5 to 10 years rather than 5)
    → These are the patients who may
      benefit from CDK4/6 inhibitors
      as adjuvant therapy

  Low depth (moderate MKI67, normal
  CCND1, intact RB1 expression):
    → These tumours are near the
      regulated luminal state
    → Lower late recurrence risk
    → Endocrine therapy alone is likely
      sufficient
    → These patients may safely de-escalate

WHAT THIS REQUIRES:
  The TCGA-BRCA dataset has survival
  data. The analysis will correlate
  depth score with:
    Disease-free survival (DFS)
    Distant recurrence-free survival (DRFS)
    Overall survival (OS)

  If depth score predicts DFS/DRFS
  in the Luminal A PAM50 subset —
  this is the clinical output.

  This is what the Oncotype DX and
  Prosigna tests do with different methods.
  The attractor depth score may correlate
  with or extend their predictive value.
  This is a testable comparison.

NOTE ON PRE-REGISTRATION:
  This prediction is stated before
  any survival analysis is run.
  The correlation direction is:
  higher depth = worse outcome.
  This is the pre-registered direction.
  It cannot be changed.
```

### PREDICTION 7 — THE CROSS-SUBTYPE COMPARISON PREDICTION

```
PREDICTION:
  The depth axis in Luminal A
  (proliferative drive) will be
  orthogonal to the depth axis in TNBC
  (differentiation block depth).

  This means:
    A tumour can be deeply displaced
    in the TNBC false attractor
    (high EZH2, low FOXA1) AND
    simultaneously have low proliferative
    depth (low MKI67, low CCND1).

    OR a tumour can be deeply displaced
    in the Luminal A proliferative axis
    (high CCND1, high MKI67) AND
    retain full luminal identity (high FOXA1,
    high GATA3, high ESR1).

  These are two distinct geometric axes
  in the breast Waddington landscape.
  They are not the same axis expressed
  at different magnitudes.
  They are fundamentally different
  directions in expression space.

  The cross-subtype analysis after
  Luminal A and TNBC are complete will
  test whether depth scores on these
  two axes are correlated or independent.

  PREDICTION: They are largely independent.
  A deep TNBC (high EZH2) is not
  necessarily a high-MKI67 tumour.
  A high-proliferative Luminal A is not
  necessarily at risk of becoming TNBC.
  The two axes are orthogonal.

  This is a pre-registered prediction
  for the cross-subtype analysis.
  Stated here before the Luminal A
  analysis runs.
```

## SECTION III — WHAT WOULD FALSIFY THESE PREDICTIONS

```
WHAT WOULD FALSIFY PREDICTION 1
(switch genes retained):
  FOXA1 suppressed >30% in LumA vs normal.
  GATA3 suppressed >30% in LumA vs normal.
  ESR1 suppressed >50% in LumA vs normal.
  If any of these occurs:
    Either the TCGA-BRCA PAM50 LumA
    population includes HER2-enriched or
    Luminal B contamination.
    Or the GSE176078 finding was
    dataset-specific.
    Or the framework needs revision.
    All three possibilities documented.

WHAT WOULD FALSIFY PREDICTION 2
(depth axis is proliferative):
  MKI67 flat in LumA vs normal.
  CCND1 flat in LumA vs normal.
  If both are flat:
    The depth axis in LumA is not
    proliferative.
    Alternative: the depth axis is
    something else entirely.
    Data decides.

WHAT WOULD FALSIFY PREDICTION 3
(EZH2 not the lock):
  EZH2 significantly elevated in LumA
  (comparable to TNBC +269.7%).
  If this occurs:
    EZH2 is relevant in Luminal A too.
    The lock mechanism is shared
    between subtypes.
    This would be a significant finding —
    and would require rethinking
    the EZH2 specificity to TNBC.

WHAT WOULD FALSIFY PREDICTION 5
(depth predicts late recurrence):
  Depth score uncorrelated with
  DFS/DRFS in TCGA-BRCA Luminal A.
  If the correlation is absent:
    The proliferative depth score
    does not add prognostic information
    beyond standard clinical factors.
    Document honestly.
    Pursue why.

## SECTION V — THE STRUCTURAL QUESTION THIS ANALYSIS ANSWERS

```
Every prior cancer analysis in this
series answered the same structural
question:
  What is the false attractor?
  What are the switch genes?
  What is the lock?
  What is the drug?

The Luminal A analysis answers a
different and harder question:

  WHAT DOES DEPTH MEAN IN A CANCER
  THAT HAS NOT LOST ITS IDENTITY?

Luminal A cells have retained luminal
identity. They are not stuck below
the luminal threshold. They are stuck
at the luminal threshold, cycling
continuously without exit.

In this geometry, depth is not
measured by identity loss.
Depth is measured by the degree of
proliferative dysregulation within
the retained identity.

This is structurally novel.
It has not been asked before in this
series.
The answer — whatever the data produces —
will determine how the framework handles
the large class of well-differentiated
cancers that retain lineage identity.

Not only Luminal A.
ILC. Low-grade serous ovarian.
Well-differentiated thyroid cancer.
Low-grade prostate cancer.
The whole class of cancers that
look like their normal counterpart
but will not stop dividing.

The Luminal A analysis is the first
case for this entire class.

What depth means in a cancer that
kept its identity.

Data decides.
Predictions locked.
Script 1 runs next.
