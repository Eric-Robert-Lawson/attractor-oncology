\section{Purpose and Scope}
% ══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

This document provides the complete technical
specification for running the FOXA1/EZH2 dual
IHC ratio assay on formalin-fixed,
paraffin-embedded (FFPE) breast cancer tissue,
in both whole-section and tissue microarray
(TMA) formats. It is intended for:

\begin{itemize}
    \item Pathologists and histotechnologists
    implementing the assay in a clinical or
    research setting
    \item Researchers designing the IHC
    concordance study described in CS-LIT-22
    \item Institutions evaluating the assay
    for inclusion in a breast cancer diagnostic
    panel
    \item Institutions whose archival tissue
    is held in TMA format
\end{itemize}

This document has been revised to incorporate
all relevant findings and limitations from the
OrganismCore master computational artifact
(FOXA1-EZH2-RATIO-MASTER-RA, 2026-03-06),
which records the complete four-script
validation across ${\sim}7{,}500$ patients
in seven independent datasets. Three critical
points from that artifact drive this protocol:

\begin{enumerate}
    \item The protein signal is confirmed at
    the protein level by CPTAC mass spectrometry
    ($r=-0.492$, $n=122$). The MS kappa failure
    was a dynamic range artefact, not a biology
    failure. IHC has 800$\times$ more dynamic
    range than iTRAQ MS.
    \item The claudin-low stromal contamination
    artefact observed in bulk RNA must be
    prevented in IHC by disciplined cancer-cell-only
    scoring.
    \item IHC H-score cut-points do not yet
    exist. RNA-space values must not be used
    as IHC thresholds.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Why IHC Is Expected to Outperform
Mass Spectrometry: The Dynamic Range Argument}
\label{sec:ihcrationale}
% ══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

\subsection{What CPTAC mass spectrometry showed}

The CPTAC-BRCA iTRAQ proteomics dataset
($n=122$) confirmed:
\begin{itemize}
    \item Correct protein-level subtype ordering:
    LumA $>$ LumB $>$ HER2 $>$ TNBC
    \item Strong inverse correlation:
    Spearman $r=-0.492$ between FOXA1 and
    EZH2 protein across all 122 patients
    ($p<0.0001$)
    \item Kappa-based subtype classification:
    \textbf{failed}
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Why the kappa failed and why this
is irrelevant to IHC}

\begin{mdframed}[backgroundcolor=dynamicbox,
                 linecolor=green!60!black,
                 linewidth=1pt]
\textbf{THE DYNAMIC RANGE ARGUMENT:}

iTRAQ MS compressed all four breast cancer
subtypes into \textbf{0.38 log$_2$ units}
total range (LumA: $-0.320$, TNBC: $-0.733$).
Measurement noise at this scale exceeds subtype
signal. Threshold-based classification is
mathematically impossible at 0.38 log$_2$
units of separation.

IHC H-scores span \textbf{0--300} ---
providing approximately \textbf{800$\times$}
more dynamic range. A LumA tumour with strong
FOXA1 (H-score $\approx 240$) and low EZH2
(H-score $\approx 30$) gives a ratio of
$\approx 8.0$. A TNBC tumour with absent
FOXA1 (H-score $\approx 10$) and high EZH2
(H-score $\approx 210$) gives a ratio of
$\approx 0.05$. The separation that is
invisible at 0.38 MS units is large and
classifiable at IHC scale.

\textbf{The MS kappa failure is a reason
to be more confident in IHC, not less.}
The protein signal exists and is directionally
correct. The instrument used to measure it
(MS) lacked the range to classify. IHC has
the range.
\end{mdframed}

\section{Tissue Section Preparation ---
Whole Section and TMA}
% ══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

\subsection{Whole-section FFPE specimens}

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Section thickness:}
    3--4~$\mu$m
    \item \textbf{Sections required per case:}
    Two serial or near-serial sections
    (one for FOXA1, one for EZH2), within
    10 levels to ensure the same tumour
    area is represented in both stains
    \item \textbf{Mounting:}
    Charged or poly-L-lysine coated slides
    \item \textbf{Drying:}
    $60^\circ$C oven, minimum 60 minutes
    \item \textbf{Tumour content:}
    Select sections with $\geq 20\%$
    invasive tumour cells. A pathologist
    must mark the invasive tumour area on
    the H\&E before IHC scoring.
    \item \textbf{FFPE block age:}
    Any age is suitable. IHC does not
    require RNA integrity --- a key
    advantage over PAM50, which requires
    adequate RNA and is unreliable on
    archival or poorly fixed tissue.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Tissue microarray (TMA) specimens}
\label{sec:tma}

\begin{mdframed}[backgroundcolor=tmabox,
                 linecolor=blue!50!black,
                 linewidth=1pt]
\textbf{TMA COMPATIBILITY:}

This protocol applies without modification to
tissue microarray format. TMAs are the standard
infrastructure for retrospective IHC validation
studies at scale in major cancer centres.
Institutions whose archival breast cancer tissue
is held in TMA format are directly compatible
with this protocol. No format conversion is
required.

\textbf{TMA-specific requirements:}
\begin{itemize}[noitemsep]
    \item \textbf{Minimum core diameter:}
    1.0~mm. Cores of 1.5--2.0~mm are
    preferred for adequate tumour cell
    count.
    \item \textbf{Minimum cores per case:}
    Two cores per case per stain are
    recommended. Cases with only one
    evaluable core per stain should be
    flagged and reported separately in
    the analysis.
    \item \textbf{Scoring on TMA cores:}
    Apply the same H-score method
    (Section~\ref{sec:scoring}) to the
    evaluable tumour area within each
    core. Score invasive cancer cell
    nuclei only; exclude stroma,
    lymphocytes, and normal epithelium
    within the core.
    \item \textbf{Two-core averaging:}
    Where two cores are available for a
    case, compute the H-score ratio for
    each core independently and report
    the mean ratio as the case value.
    If the two core ratios differ by
    more than 0.5 log units, flag for
    pathologist review (may indicate
    tumour heterogeneity or core
    placement in stroma rather than
    tumour).
    \item \textbf{CL cores specifically:}
    The claudin-low stromal contamination
    warning (Section~\ref{sec:clwarning})
    applies with equal force to TMA cores.
    A 1.0~mm core punched into a
    mesenchymal-rich CL tumour may have
    low cancer cell density. If $<50$
    invasive cancer cell nuclei are
    identifiable in the core, exclude
    the case from the primary analysis
    and report it as unevaluable.
    \item \textbf{Tissue loss:}
    TMA cores are susceptible to section
    loss on sequential cutting. For each
    TMA block, cut at minimum three
    sequential sections at the start
    of the run to ensure paired FOXA1
    and EZH2 sections are available
    for each core.
\end{itemize}

\textbf{TMA advantage for this study:}
A TMA containing 400 cases requires a single
pair of slides per staining run rather than
400 individual slides. This reduces reagent
cost, staining variability between cases
(all cases processed under identical conditions
simultaneously), and pathologist scoring time
(one slide field rather than 400 individual
section scans). For institutions with archival
TMA infrastructure, this is the recommended
format for the calibration study.
\end{mdframed}

\section{Scoring Method}
\label{sec:scoring}
% ══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

\subsection{What to score}

\begin{mdframed}[backgroundcolor=scorebox,
                 linecolor=purple!50!black,
                 linewidth=1.5pt]
\textbf{SCORE INVASIVE CANCER CELL NUCLEI ONLY.}

Exclude without exception:
\begin{itemize}[noitemsep]
    \item Normal ductal and lobular epithelium
    \item Stromal fibroblasts and endothelial
    cells
    \item Tumour-infiltrating lymphocytes
    \item Myoepithelial cells
    \item Necrotic areas
    \item Any non-cancer nuclear staining
\end{itemize}

A pathologist must mark the invasive tumour
area on the H\&E before IHC scoring.
Score the marked area only.

\textbf{This instruction applies to all
subtypes. It is especially critical for
claudin-low. See
Section~\ref{sec:clwarning}.}
\end{mdframed}

\subsection{H-score calculation}

\begin{equation}
\text{H-score} =
(1 \times p_1) + (2 \times p_2) +
(3 \times p_3)
\end{equation}

where $p_1$, $p_2$, $p_3$ are the percentages
of invasive cancer cell nuclei with weak
(1$+$), moderate (2$+$), and strong (3$+$)
staining respectively. Estimate to the nearest
5\%. \textbf{H-score range: 0--300.}

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{p{2.5cm}p{5cm}p{5.5cm}}
\toprule
\textbf{Intensity} &
\textbf{Visual description} &
\textbf{Practical example} \\
\midrule
0 (negative) &
No nuclear staining above
background &
FOXA1 in claudin-low \\[0.3em]
1$+$ (weak) &
Light brown nuclear tinge;
faint but identifiable;
above background &
FOXA1 in TNBC \\[0.3em]
2$+$ (moderate) &
Clearly brown; unambiguous;
not intense &
FOXA1 in LumB; EZH2 in LumA \\[0.3em]
3$+$ (strong) &
Intense brown; visible at
low power &
FOXA1 in LumA; EZH2 in TNBC \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\subsection{Ratio calculation}

\begin{equation}
\text{FOXA1/EZH2 ratio} =
\frac{\text{FOXA1 H-score}}
     {\text{EZH2 H-score}}
\end{equation}

\textbf{Division by zero:} If EZH2 H-score
$= 0$, record ratio as undefined and classify
as claudin-low by default. Document these
cases separately in the calibration dataset.

\subsection{Worked example}

TNBC section:
\begin{itemize}[noitemsep]
    \item FOXA1: 5\% at 1$+$, 2\% at 2$+$,
    0\% at 3$+$ $\Rightarrow$
    H-score $= 5 + 4 + 0 = 9$
    \item EZH2: 20\% at 1$+$, 45\% at 2$+$,
    30\% at 3$+$ $\Rightarrow$
    H-score $= 20 + 90 + 90 = 200$
    \item Ratio $= 9 \div 200 = 0.045$
\end{itemize}

This falls in the low-ratio TNBC/CL zone,
consistent with the framework.

\subsection{Minimum cell count}

Score a minimum of \textbf{200 invasive cancer
cell nuclei} per stain on whole sections.
For TMA cores, score all evaluable invasive
nuclei; flag cores with $<50$ evaluable
nuclei as unevaluable.

\subsection{Inter-observer assessment}

Each slide must be independently scored by
two pathologists, blinded to PAM50 subtype
and to each other's score. Report:
\begin{itemize}[noitemsep]
    \item ICC for continuous H-score values;
    target $>0.80$
    \item Cohen's $\kappa$ for categorical
    subtype assignment; target $>0.70$
\end{itemize}

\section{Claudin-Low Stromal Contamination
Warning}
\label{sec:clwarning}
% ══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

\begin{mdframed}[backgroundcolor=criticalbox,
                 linecolor=red!70!black,
                 linewidth=2pt]
\textbf{\large CRITICAL SCORING WARNING:
CLAUDIN-LOW TUMOURS}

\bigskip
\textbf{This is the single most consequential
scoring error possible in this assay.}

\bigskip
\textbf{The problem:}

Claudin-low (CL) breast tumours are highly
mesenchymal, with abundant stromal content
relative to cancer cell density. That stroma
consists predominantly of EZH2-low mesenchymal
cells --- fibroblasts, myofibroblasts, vascular
endothelium. These stromal cells are EZH2-negative
or EZH2-low by IHC.

If stromal cell nuclei are included in the
EZH2 count, the EZH2 H-score is artificially
lowered. This falsely elevates the FOXA1/EZH2
ratio, causing a CL tumour that should score
at the lowest ratio to appear in the
luminal range --- the exact opposite of the
correct classification.

\bigskip
\textbf{This artefact was directly observed
and documented.}

In bulk RNA sequencing data (which averages
cancer cell signal together with stromal
signal), claudin-low tumours appeared
\textbf{above LumA} in the ratio ordering
--- placing CL as the most luminal-appearing
subtype, when it is biologically the least
luminal. The mechanism: bulk RNA captures
EZH2-low mesenchymal stroma, artificially
lowering the denominator and inflating the
ratio. This is recorded in the OrganismCore
master computational artifact (result R4-C,
CL ratio $= 1.4888$ in METABRIC bulk RNA
vs CL ratio $= 0.10$ in scRNA-seq cancer
cells only).

IHC avoids this artefact \textbf{only if}
the scorer counts cancer cell nuclei
exclusively.

\bigskip
\textbf{The instruction:}

For every case, and especially for suspected
CL or mesenchymal tumours:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item Score only nuclei within the
    marked invasive tumour area
    \item Exclude all stromal nuclei, even
    those immediately adjacent to or
    interspersed with cancer cells
    \item Exclude all TIL nuclei within
    tumour nests
    \item When uncertain whether a nucleus
    belongs to a cancer cell or a stromal
    cell, exclude it
    \item Flag all cases scored in a
    predominantly mesenchymal background
    for independent pathologist review
    before classification
\end{enumerate}

\bigskip
\textbf{The self-check for correct CL scoring:}

In a correctly scored CL case:
\begin{itemize}[noitemsep]
    \item FOXA1 H-score should be very low
    to absent (CL FOXA1 is $-97.8\%$ below
    normal LumA in single-cell data)
    \item EZH2 H-score should be moderate,
    not zero and not high (CL EZH2 is
    elevated $+67\%$ above normal but not
    at the TNBC level of $+189\%$)
    \item The ratio should therefore be
    very low: low numerator divided by
    moderate denominator
\end{itemize}

If a scored CL case gives a ratio in the
luminal range, stromal contamination of the
EZH2 count is the most likely cause.
Re-score cancer cell nuclei exclusively.

\bigskip
\textbf{TMA note:} A 1.0~mm TMA core punched
into a mesenchymal-rich CL tumour may have
low cancer cell density. If $<50$ invasive
cancer cell nuclei are identifiable, exclude
the case from the primary analysis and report
as unevaluable.
\end{mdframed}

\section{Provisional Ratio Ranges and Their
Correct Interpretation}
\label{sec:cutpoints}
% ══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

\begin{mdframed}[backgroundcolor=criticalbox,
                 linecolor=red!60!black,
                 linewidth=1.5pt]
\textbf{CRITICAL: THESE ARE NOT IHC CUT-POINTS.}

The values below are \textbf{single-cell
RNA-seq ratio values} in mean expression
units. They are not IHC H-score values.
There is no formula to convert between
RNA-space and IHC H-score space. They
depend on entirely different measurement
principles, instruments, and calibration
conditions.

\textbf{Do not use these values as starting-
point thresholds for the calibration study.}

These values indicate three things only:
\begin{enumerate}[noitemsep]
    \item \textbf{Rank order:} LumA highest,
    CL lowest. This rank order is expected
    to hold in IHC.
    \item \textbf{Span:} Two orders of
    magnitude from LumA (9.38) to CL (0.10).
    This suggests IHC will also show large
    separation, even if absolute values differ.
    \item \textbf{Direction:} High ratio
    $\rightarrow$ luminal. Low ratio
    $\rightarrow$ undifferentiated/epigenetically
    locked.
\end{enumerate}

\textbf{IHC H-score cut-points must be
determined in the calibration study using
Youden J index optimisation from ROC analysis
against PAM50 ground truth. See
Section~\ref{sec:calibration}.}

\section{Calibration Study Design}
\label{sec:calibration}
% ══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

\subsection{Purpose}

The calibration study must determine IHC
H-score cut-points from scratch, using
actual IHC H-scores against PAM50 ground
truth. It must not anchor on RNA-space
values.

\subsection{Study type}

Retrospective IHC concordance study.
No new patient recruitment. No treatment
changes. Observational only. Compatible
with both whole-section and TMA formats.

\subsection{Study population}

Archived FFPE breast cancer tissue with:
\begin{itemize}[noitemsep]
    \item Available PAM50/Prosigna result
    \item Confirmed invasive breast carcinoma
    \item Adequate tumour content
    ($\geq 20\%$ invasive tumour cells on
    whole section; $\geq 50$ evaluable
    cancer cell nuclei on TMA core)
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Target sample size:}

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{lcc}
\toprule
\textbf{PAM50 subtype} &
\textbf{Target $n$} &
\textbf{Minimum} \\
\midrule
Luminal A & 80--100 & 50 \\
Luminal B & 80--100 & 50 \\
HER2-enriched & 50--60 & 30 \\
Basal/TNBC & 60--80 & 40 \\
Claudin-low & 30--40 & 20 \\
\textbf{Total} &
\textbf{300--380} & \textbf{190} \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

Include a minimum of 30 ILC cases
(CDH1-absent) as a separate analysis arm.

\subsection{Primary endpoint}

Agreement between FOXA1/EZH2 IHC ratio
classification and PAM50:
\begin{itemize}[noitemsep]
    \item Cohen's $\kappa \geq 0.70$
    \item Overall concordance $\geq 75\%$
    \item AUC for LumA vs Basal and LumA
    vs LumB $\geq 0.75$
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Cut-point determination method}

\begin{mdframed}[backgroundcolor=protocolbox,
                 linecolor=green!50!black,
                 linewidth=1pt]
\textbf{REQUIRED METHOD:}
\begin{enumerate}
    \item Split cohort into training (70\%)
    and test (30\%) sets, stratified by
    PAM50 subtype.
    \item In training set: run ROC analysis
    for each binary task (LumA vs LumB,
    LumA vs Basal, TNBC vs CL). Determine
    optimal H-score ratio threshold per
    boundary using Youden J index
    ($\max(\text{sensitivity} +
    \text{specificity} - 1)$).
    \item Validate cut-points in held-out
    test set. Report sensitivity, specificity,
    PPV, NPV, $\kappa$.
    \item \textbf{Do not use RNA-space values
    (9.38, 8.10, 3.34, 0.52, 0.10) as anchors
    at any step.}
    \item Report cut-points with 95\% CI
    from bootstrapping ($\geq 1{,}000$
    iterations).
\end{enumerate}
\end{mdframed}

\subsection{Secondary endpoints}

\begin{itemize}[noitemsep]
    \item Inter-observer reliability (ICC
    and $\kappa$ between two pathologists)
    \item Correlation between IHC ratio and
    clinical outcomes where available
    (exploratory)
    \item Confirmation that CL scores below
    TNBC in IHC (pre-specified hypothesis;
    CL stromal contamination check as
    sensitivity analysis)
    \item TMA vs whole-section concordance
    (where both formats are available for
    the same cases)
\end{itemize}
