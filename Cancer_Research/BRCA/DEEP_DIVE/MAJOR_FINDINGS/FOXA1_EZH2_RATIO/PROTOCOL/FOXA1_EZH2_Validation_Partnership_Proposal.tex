\textbf{In brief:}

A ratio of two standard IHC proteins (FOXA1 $\div$
EZH2) was derived from first principles using
Waddington attractor geometry applied to 19,542 single
cancer cells. It correctly orders all six major breast
cancer subtypes and maps each to its therapeutic
treatment logic. Validated computationally across
${\sim}$7,500 patients in seven independent datasets
(TCGA, METABRIC, CPTAC, GSE25066, and three others).

\medskip
The test costs \$50--\$100 per patient. It uses
equipment present in any pathology laboratory. It
produces results the same day as the biopsy workup.
It requires no RNA, no proprietary platform, and no
shipping to a reference laboratory.

\medskip
One study is needed before clinical deployment: a
retrospective IHC concordance study against PAM50 in
$n=300$--$400$ archived FFPE cases with known subtype
classification. No new patients. No treatment changes.
Estimated cost: \$5,000--\$15,000 in consumables.

\medskip
\textbf{We are seeking one or more pathology/oncology
partners to run this study.}
\end{mdframed}

\section{The Problem: Molecular Subtyping Is
Inaccessible to Most Breast Cancer Patients}
% ══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

per year globally. The majority of these deaths
occur in low- and middle-income countries, where

Without molecular subtyping, ER$+$ patients (Luminal

academic --- it affects treatment choices that
determine outcomes.

\section{The Finding: The FOXA1/EZH2 Ratio}
% ══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

\subsection{What it is and how it was derived}

FOXA1 is the pioneer transcription factor that opens
chromatin for the luminal identity programme. EZH2 is
the epigenetic repressor (PRC2 catalytic subunit) that
silences that same programme. Their ratio measures the
balance between identity maintenance and identity
suppression --- the biological axis that determines
both subtype and therapeutic vulnerability.

The ratio was derived from Waddington landscape
attractor geometry applied to 19,542 single cancer
cells from 26 patients (GSE176078, Wu et al.\ 2021,
\textit{Nature Genetics}). The predicted ordering
was locked before any confirmatory analysis was run.
It was confirmed exactly.

\subsection{The computational evidence base}

\begin{mdframed}[backgroundcolor=statsbox,
                 linecolor=purple!40!black,
                 linewidth=1pt]
\begin{itemize}[noitemsep]
    \item \textbf{Ordering confirmed:}
    TCGA-BRCA $n=837$ PAM50-labelled patients,
    Kruskal-Wallis $p = 2.87 \times 10^{-103}$
    \item \textbf{Survival stratification:}
    TCGA-BRCA KM $p=0.0031$ ($n=1{,}218$);
    METABRIC KM $p<0.0001$ ($n=1{,}980$) ---
    two independent cohorts, two platforms
    \item \textbf{ROC classification:}
    LumA vs Basal: AUC $0.828$--$0.901$;
    LumA vs LumB: AUC $0.796$--$0.873$
    (replicated in METABRIC and TCGA independently)
    \item \textbf{Protein-level confirmation:}
    CPTAC-BRCA iTRAQ mass spectrometry,
    $n=122$, Spearman $r=-0.492$,
    $p<0.0001$
    \item \textbf{Treatment response:}
    GSE25066 $n=508$, chemotherapy response
    prediction $p < 0.0001$; endocrine therapy
    benefit in METABRIC HR$+$ patients
    $p=0.0027$, $\Delta = 18.7$ months RFS
    \item \textbf{Seven independent datasets.
    Four measurement platforms. Zero biological
    contradictions.}
\end{itemize}
\end{mdframed}

\section{The One Remaining Step}
% ══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

The computational validation is complete across
seven datasets. The biological mechanism is confirmed
by two independent published papers (Schade et al.\
\textit{Nature} 2024; Toska et al.\ \textit{Nature
Medicine} 2017).

\textbf{The remaining gap is IHC calibration.}
The scRNA-seq ratio values (9.38, 8.10, 3.34, 0.52,
0.10) must be translated into IHC H-score cut-points.
This requires running the two-stain IHC protocol on
FFPE tissue where the PAM50 subtype is already known,
and determining the H-score thresholds that correctly
separate subtypes.

This is not a conceptual gap. It is instrument
calibration --- the same step applied to ER, PR,
HER2, and Ki67 before their routine clinical adoption.

\section{The Partnership Ask}
% ═════════════════════════════════════════════════���════════════

\begin{mdframed}[backgroundcolor=askbox,
                 linecolor=blue!50!black,
                 linewidth=1.5pt]
\textbf{What we are asking for:}

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Access to archived FFPE breast cancer
    tissue} from $n=300$--$400$ cases where PAM50
    subtype classification is known (from prior
    research or clinical assay)
    \item \textbf{FOXA1 and EZH2 IHC} run on those
    sections using the protocol specification provided
    (see attached Protocol Document)
    \item \textbf{H-score scoring} by one or two
    pathologists, scored blinded to PAM50 subtype
    \item \textbf{Concordance analysis} comparing
    ratio-based classification to PAM50 classification
\end{enumerate}

\textbf{What we provide:}

\begin{enumerate}
    \item Complete antibody specification (clones,
    catalogue numbers, dilutions, antigen retrieval
    conditions, controls) --- see attached Protocol
    Document
    \item Scoring guide (H-score method, what to
    score, how to calculate the ratio)
    \item Analysis framework and statistical plan
    (concordance, ROC, cut-point optimisation,
    inter-observer kappa)
    \item Full computational validation dataset and
    scripts (publicly available in the repository)
    \item Co-authorship on the resulting publication
\end{enumerate}

\textbf{What this study does NOT require:}

\begin{itemize}[noitemsep]
    \item No new patient recruitment
    \item No changes to any patient's treatment
    \item No new laboratory equipment beyond
    standard IHC capability
    \item No RNA extraction, no bioinformatics
    \item Minimal regulatory burden (retrospective
    tissue research with no patient contact)
\end{itemize}
\end{mdframed}

\section{Why This Matters Beyond Your Institution}

\begin{mdframed}[backgroundcolor=equitybox,
                 linecolor=orange!60!black,
                 linewidth=1pt]
A validated FOXA1/EZH2 IHC protocol would be
immediately deployable in every pathology laboratory
worldwide that currently runs breast cancer IHC
workups --- including settings where PAM50 is
completely inaccessible.

Approximately 1.2--1.5 million breast cancer patients
per year currently receive no molecular subtype
information because PAM50 is unavailable or
unaffordable in their healthcare system. For these

further molecular refinement.

A validated two-stain IHC ratio test would change
that for every one of those patients, at a cost
