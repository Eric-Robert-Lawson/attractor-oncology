### Finding 5: Fiscal Sponsorship —
### The 501(c)(3) Bypass

You do not need to form a 501(c)(3)
to get institutional standing.
Fiscal sponsorship gives you that
standing without the administrative
process.

**What fiscal sponsorship is:**
An existing nonprofit extends its
tax-exempt status to your project.
You receive grants and donations
under their umbrella. They take
a management fee (typically 7–15%).
You retain scientific ownership.

**The relevant option for your
situation:**

*Fractured Atlas*
`fracturedatlas.org`
Accepts science, health, and
technology projects as well as arts.
Online application. No minimum budget.
Management fee: 8% of revenue.
No up-front cost to apply.

*Community Initiatives*
`communityinitiatives.com`
Specifically for research and social
impact projects.
Application online.
Management fee: 9%.

*National Network of Fiscal Sponsors*
Directory of all fiscal sponsors:
`fiscalsponsors.org/seeking-fiscal-sponsorship`

**Why this matters for your situation:**
Fiscal sponsorship does not require
6–10 weeks of IRS processing.
It requires filling out an application
form. If accepted, you have institutional
standing within days to weeks.

**What it unlocks immediately:**
- CST reagent donation requests go to
  a nonprofit umbrella, not an individual
- Experiment.com campaign (if launched
  later) is credentialed
- Donors can give tax-deductibly

**The constraint:**
Tides Center requires $100,000 minimum
project budget. Skip them.
Fractured Atlas and Community Initiatives
have no minimums.

**This is not an immediate action.**
It is a week-2 action if the immediate
outreach generates interest but no
institutional yes yet. It is the
lightweight alternative to the 501(c)(3)
that does not require a lawyer or
6–10 weeks of IRS processing.

## Part 4: The Strategic Principles
## (Unchanged)

### Principle 1: The one-person bridge

Paradigm shifts do not happen through
mass persuasion. They happen through
one person who has the right gap at
the right moment.

Every visit and email is a search for
that person. Success is finding one.
Not ten.

### Principle 2: The document does the work

Your job: one sentence, hand the
document, let them read.
The document does the scientific
persuasion.

### Principle 3: No is navigation

Every no contains information.
A referral from a pathologist to a
colleague is worth more than ten
cold contacts. Ask for the referral
every time.

### Principle 4: Parallel motion

Do not wait for one door to close
before opening another. All viable
contacts move simultaneously.
The first yes wins.

### Principle 5: The framing is fixed

You are a mathematician. The geometry
told you what to look for before you
looked. This framing does not change
regardless of the institution,
the person, or the conversation.

## Part 5: The Personal Execution
## Framework

### The only thing to hold in memory

**"The geometry told me what to look
for before I looked."**

One sentence. Everything navigates
from what they say in response.

### The three anchors

**Anchor 1: The geometry told me first.**
Use when the conversation is about
how you found it.

**Anchor 2: The prediction was locked
before the data.**
Use when the conversation is about
data dredging.

**Anchor 3: I am not asking you to
trust me. I am asking you to look.**
Use when the conversation is about
credentials.

### The silence rule

Their eyes coming up from the page
is the signal. Not silence duration.
Not internal sense of how long it
has been.

Until their eyes come up: wait.
When their eyes come up: answer only
what was asked. Then stop.

When silence becomes uncomfortable:
look at the document in your hand.
Not at them. At the page.
This is patience made visible.

### Answer what was asked

Only what they asked.
Not the question behind it.
Not the question they might ask next.
The question they asked.
Then stop.
If they need more they will ask.
