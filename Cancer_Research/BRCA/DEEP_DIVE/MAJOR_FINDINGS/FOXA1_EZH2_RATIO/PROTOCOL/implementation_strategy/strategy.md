## Part 1: The Core Problem Being Solved

The computational case is complete.
Seven independent datasets. ~7,500 patients.
Four measurement platforms. Zero biological
contradictions. Timestamped prediction that
precedes all confirmatory analysis.

One step remains: IHC calibration.

This means running FOXA1 (CST D4E2 #53528)
and EZH2 (CST D2H1 #5246) stains on
~300–400 archived FFPE breast cancer cases
with known PAM50 subtype, scoring H-scores
blinded to subtype, and deriving cut-points
using Youden J optimisation against PAM50
ground truth.

This step requires:
- A pathology laboratory with IHC capability
- Archived FFPE breast cancer tissue with
  PAM50 annotation
- One or two pathologists willing to score
- IRB approval (retrospective, minimal risk,
  standard pathway)

It does not require:
- New patient recruitment
- New equipment
- RNA extraction
- Bioinformatics
- Funding beyond ~$5,000–$15,000 consumables

The problem is institutional access,
not scientific readiness.

## Part 2: The Strategic Principles

### Principle 1: The one-person bridge

Paradigm shifts do not happen through mass
persuasion. They happen through one person
who has the right gap at the right moment.

Every visit and email is a search for that
person. Not a campaign to convince the
institution. A search for the individual
whose existing problem your framework solves.

Success is finding one person. Not ten.

### Principle 2: The document does the work

The one-pager is not a leave-behind.
It is the primary instrument of
communication. Your job in the room is
to get them to read it. The document
does the scientific persuasion.

Your job: one sentence, hand the document,
let them read.

### Principle 3: No is navigation, not failure

Every no contains information.
Every person who says no but suggests
someone else is a step forward, not back.
A referral from a pathologist to a colleague
is worth more than ten cold contacts.

Track every no carefully. The pattern
across multiple nos will tell you which
framing works and which type of institution
is most likely to engage.

### Principle 4: Parallel motion, not sequential

Do not wait for one door to close before
opening another. All viable contacts move
simultaneously. The first yes wins.
The others become backup.

### Principle 5: The framing is fixed

You are a mathematician. The geometry told
you what to look for before you looked.
This framing does not change regardless of
the institution, the person, or the
conversation. It is not a pitch. It is
the truth of what happened.

## Part 5: The Personal Execution Framework

### The only thing to hold in memory
going into any room

**"The geometry told me what to look for
before I looked."**

One sentence. Everything else navigates
from what they say in response.

### The three anchors

If the thread is lost mid-conversation,
find the nearest anchor and use it.

**Anchor 1 — How you found it:**
The geometry told me first.

**Anchor 2 — Why it is not data dredging:**
The prediction was locked before the data.

**Anchor 3 — Why they should engage:**
I am not asking you to trust me.
I am asking you to look.

### The silence rule

Their eyes coming up from the page is the
signal. Not silence duration. Not internal
sense of how long it has been.

Until their eyes come up: wait.
When their eyes come up: answer only
what was asked. Then stop.

### After every visit — same day

Write immediately:
- Full name and role of everyone spoken to
- What they asked — verbatim if possible
- What made them pause
- Whether they kept the documents
- Whether they suggested anyone else

This record is the navigation map.
Patterns across visits tell you where the
one-person bridge is most likely to be.

## Part 10: The Honest Risk Assessment

**Risk 1: All local institutions say no.**
Mitigation: remote contacts (Munster/SWOG,
future international outreach). The
geography is convenient but not necessary.
Any institution with FFPE breast cancer
tissue and IHC capability anywhere in
the world is a viable partner.

**Risk 2: The in-person framing fails
consistently in the first 30 seconds.**
Mitigation: the warm-up visits at
Advocate Lutheran and RFU are specifically
designed to generate real feedback before
the higher-stakes visits. If the opening
is not landing, the feedback from early
visits tells you why.

**Risk 3: IRB process stalls the timeline.**
Mitigation: retrospective tissue research
is the minimal-risk pathway in most
jurisdictions. Standard IRB review, not
full board. Partner institution handles
their own IRB — you do not need one as
an independent researcher because the
tissue never leaves their institution.

**Risk 4: Working memory failure in a
high-stakes visit.**
Mitigation: one sentence to hold.
Three anchors. The document does the
work. Your job is the opening and the
navigation — not the complete argument
delivery. If the thread is lost, anchor
and continue.
