### SECTION 1 — THE CORE ARCHITECTURE (3 items)

These are the foundational structural claims that the cross-subtype scripts derived independently and that need to be assessed as a unified whole — not per-subtype individually.

**CS-LIT-1: The FOXA1/EZH2 ratio as a single continuous ordering axis across all breast cancer subtypes**
- The claim: a ratio of two routine IHC proteins (FOXA1 ÷ EZH2) correctly orders LumA → LumB → HER2 → TNBC → CL in therapeutic priority sequence.
- Values derived: 9.38, 8.10, 3.34, 0.52, 0.10.
- What to check: has any published paper used this specific ratio, or any two-protein ratio, to order breast cancer subtypes on a treatment axis? Is the FOXA1/EZH2 relationship described as a gradient across subtypes anywhere in the literature?

**CS-LIT-2: The six lock type classification (kinase / chromatin / amplicon / epigenetic / structural / root)**
- The claim: breast cancer subtypes are mechanistically distinguished by what is *holding* the cell in the wrong attractor state, not by what receptors are present or absent.
- What to check: has any published framework, review, or classification system described breast cancer using a "mechanism of arrest" taxonomy rather than a receptor-expression taxonomy? Any convergent classification attempts?

**CS-LIT-3: CL as the deepest subtype when measured correctly (EZH2-free PCA)**
- The claim: when EZH2 is removed from the PCA panel, CL moves from behind TNBC to in front of it (6.572 vs 6.063 distance from normal). CL is the deepest subtype; TNBC's apparent depth is an EZH2 measurement artefact.
- What to check: has any paper measured CL vs TNBC distance from normal breast in multi-gene PCA space? Has EZH2 confounding of TYPE 4 vs TYPE 2 comparisons been described?

### SECTION 2 — FRAMEWORK OBSERVATIONS (9 items)

Derived data that emerged from the three scripts and needs to be placed against the literature for the first time at the cross-subtype level.

**CS-LIT-4: EZH2 graded elevation across subtypes (confirmed CS-2)**
- TNBC +189%, TNBC > HER2 > LumB > LumA in single-cell data.
- What to check: is EZH2 graded elevation across breast cancer subtypes established in bulk or single-cell data? Has any paper shown this gradient quantitatively?

**CS-LIT-5: FOXA1 as continuous depth axis within subtypes vs binary identity marker across subtypes (confirmed CS-9)**
- The claim: FOXA1 encodes depth *within* a subtype differently than it encodes identity *across* subtypes. Within LumA, FOXA1 is uniform (r=+0.084 with depth). Across subtypes, FOXA1 is the ordering axis.
- What to check: has the within-vs-across distinction for FOXA1 been described? Any paper treating FOXA1 as a continuous within-subtype variable?

**CS-LIT-6: AR as a continuous depth axis within TNBC (r=−0.378, p=6.23×10⁻¹⁴⁷, n=4,312 cells)**
- The claim: AR anti-correlates with TNBC depth in single-cell data with high significance. AR is not just a LAR subtype binary — it is a continuous depth encoder.
- What to check: is AR used as a continuous variable within TNBC (not just as a binary LAR/non-LAR classifier)? Any continuous AR scoring in TNBC single-cell data?

**CS-LIT-7: ILC as the geometric inverse of TNBC (structural lock vs epigenetic lock)**
- TNBC: FOXA1 absent, CDH1 present; ILC: FOXA1 hyperactivated, CDH1 absent.
- What to check: has the ILC/TNBC geometric inversion been stated formally as a structural principle in the literature? Any paper framing these two subtypes as mechanistic opposites on the same identity axis?

**CS-LIT-8: LumB DNMT3A/HDAC2 co-expression coupling (r=+0.267 vs r=+0.071 in LumA, p=5.68×10⁻⁵⁶)**
- The claim: DNMT3A and HDAC2 are co-expressed at 3.8× the LumA level in LumB single-cell data. This co-expression is the mechanism of the chromatin lock.
- What to check: has DNMT3A/HDAC2 co-expression been described specifically in LumB? Any paper showing this coupling in ER+ breast cancer?

**CS-LIT-9: LumB TFF1/ESR1 decoupling — replicated in METABRIC (p=0.0019)**
- The cross-subtype replication: first found in single-cell, directional in TCGA bulk (p=0.066), now confirmed in METABRIC bulk microarray (p=0.0019).
- What to check: has the TFF1/ESR1 ratio as an ER output efficiency measure been described in LumB vs LumA in any published bulk array or IHC study? Any METABRIC-based analysis of ER output genes by subtype?

**CS-LIT-10: EZH2 paradox — both arms confirmed in GSE25066**
- Short window: EZH2-high TNBC has better OS (TCGA HR=0.424, p=0.024).
- Long window: EZH2-high TNBC has worse DRFS (GSE25066 HR=1.363, p=0.0047).
- EZH2 higher in pCR=1 vs pCR=0 (p<0.0001).
- What to check: has the dual behaviour of EZH2 in TNBC — chemo sensitivity at short window, late relapse driver at long window — been described as a single unified mechanism? Any paper showing both arms? Any paper showing EZH2 predicts pCR AND worse DRFS in the same cohort?

**CS-LIT-11: TNBC depth score predicts DRFS in GSE25066 (HR=1.509, p=0.0001)**
- A geometry-derived composite score (EZH2+SOX10+MKI67 minus AR+FOXA1+CDKN1A) validated externally.
- What to check: has any externally validated composite depth or attractor score been published for TNBC using these gene combinations? Any prior composite score in GSE25066 using these specific genes?

**CS-LIT-12: Treatment-context dependence of AR prognosis in TNBC (G-1 failure explanation)**
- AR-high (LAR) has *lower* pCR with taxane-anthracycline but better natural history in untreated patients.
- What to check: is the LAR lower-pCR finding with taxane-anthracycline established? (Lehmann 2011, Jiang 2018 Clin Cancer Res were referenced — confirm directly.) Does any paper describe the inversion of AR prognosis by treatment context?
