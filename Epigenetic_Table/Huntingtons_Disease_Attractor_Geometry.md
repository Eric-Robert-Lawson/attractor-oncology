### THE CELL OF ORIGIN

```
Medium spiny neurons (MSNs).

Specifically:
  D1-type MSNs (direct pathway)
    — express DRD1, dynorphin, substance P
    — project to substantia nigra pars
       reticulata and internal globus pallidus
    — facilitate voluntary movement
    — degenerate in the caudate early in HD

  D2-type MSNs (indirect pathway)
    — express DRD2, enkephalin (PENK)
    — project to external globus pallidus
    — suppress unwanted movement
    — degenerate FIRST in HD
    — the first neurons lost are the
       indirect pathway MSNs

  Indirect pathway MSNs have lower

  the shallowest attractor yields

### THE IDENTITY ANCHOR (A)

```
The MSN Identity Anchor is the
transcriptional programme that
constitutes what a medium spiny
neuron is.

At the deepest level:
  BCL11B (CTIP2) — the master
  transcription factor for MSN
  identity specification.
  BCL11B controls differentiation
  of MSN progenitors in the lateral
  ganglionic eminence.
  BCL11B governs the expression of
  all downstream MSN-selective genes.
  BCL11B represses alternative neuronal
  fates.
  BCL11B loss in experimental models
  recapitulates core HD features:
    DARPP-32 expression lost
    MSN identity markers lost
    Striatal connectivity disrupted
  (Arlotta et al., 2008;
   Desplats et al., 2008;
   Tang et al., 2012)

  BCL11B is the deep Identity Anchor.
  It is the gene whose activity most
  directly constitutes MSN identity.

At the operational level:
  DARPP-32 — the functional identity
    marker of the mature MSN.
    Phosphoprotein that integrates
    dopamine and glutamate signals.
    Lost early in HD.
    Its loss is a direct readout of
    Identity Anchor collapse.

  BDNF/TrkB — the trophic Identity
    Anchor signal.
    BDNF is produced in the cortex
    and transported anterograde to
    the striatum.
    TrkB on MSNs is the receptor.
    BDNF-TrkB signalling is not just
    a survival signal — it is an
    IDENTITY MAINTENANCE signal.
    Without BDNF-TrkB, MSNs lose
    the trophic support that keeps
    their identity programme active.
    Loss of BDNF-TrkB signalling in
    iSPNs triggers GSTO2 upregulation
    → aberrant glutathione metabolism
    → elevated striatal dopamine
    → dopaminergic neurotoxicity to MSNs
    → early motor symptoms.
    (Oxford/Nature 2024:
    "Discovery of key mechanism in
    Huntington's Disease could pave the
    way for early detection and treatment")
    BDNF-TrkB loss is one of the EARLIEST
    events in HD — preceding clinical onset.
    It is the first domino in Identity
    Anchor collapse.

The Identity Anchor (A) in the ratio
R = A/H is therefore operationally
measured as:

  A = CBP histone acetyltransferase
      activity at MSN identity loci
    = H3K27ac and H3K9ac levels at
      BCL11B, DARPP-32, BDNF, and
      PGC-1α promoter and enhancer regions

This is measurable.
It is a number.
It can be the biomarker for the
intervention protocol.

      46% reduction in CSF mHTT at week 24
