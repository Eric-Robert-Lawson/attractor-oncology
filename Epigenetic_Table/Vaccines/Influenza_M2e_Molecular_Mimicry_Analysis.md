fitness and are outcompeted.

mechanism is what's killing 80% of
its competitors.

confirmed under CURRENT immune pressure
levels (where only a minority of the
population has significant anti-M2e titre).
It has NOT been empirically confirmed
under GLOBAL HIGH-TITRE anti-M2e
pressure (which has never existed).
The functional constraints on M2e
make escape highly costly.
But the exhaustiveness principle applies:
Whatever can happen will happen.
Given enough selection pressure,
enough viral generations, and enough
population size:
A partially escape-competent M2e variant
will eventually emerge.
The question is whether it is viable
enough to persist.
The functional constraint evidence
says: probably not viable enough.
But "probably not" is not the same as "never."

MITIGATION:
The inclusion of the NP CD8+ T-cell
component provides a second kill
mechanism that is independent of M2e.
If an M2e-escape variant arises, it
still faces NP-specific cytotoxic
T cells.
The virus would need to escape BOTH
M2e antibody AND NP T-cell killing
simultaneously.
The probability of simultaneous escape
from two orthogonal conserved targets

escape from either alone.
This is why the two-component design

merely additive.
It is a GEOMETRIC REDUNDANCY against
