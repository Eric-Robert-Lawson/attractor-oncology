## II. WHAT THIS PRACTICE IS

```
This practice is:

  COMPUTATIONAL GEOMETRIC ANALYSIS
  of individual cancer biopsy data
  using the OrganismCore attractor
  framework.

Three layers. Precisely distinguished.

─────────────────────────────────────────

LAYER 1 — THE MEASUREMENT

  Gene expression data is received
  from a patient's biopsy.

  Mathematical computation is applied.

  The output is:
    A depth score.
    A switch gene suppression profile.
    An epigenetic lock profile.
    An attractor classification.
    A geometric trajectory over time
    if serial data exists.

  This is pure mathematics applied
  to biological data.

  No clinical judgment is involved.
  No medical training is required.
  The same computation on the same data
  produces the same numbers every time.

  The geometry was in the data.
  The computation reveals it.

─────────────────────────────────────────

LAYER 2 — THE INTERPRETATION

  What does the geometry suggest?
  Where does this patient sit
  on the established attractor landscape?
  What connections to published literature
  are relevant to this geometric position?
  What questions does this raise for
  clinical assessment?

  This is mathematical interpretation
  informed by literature.

  The connections are explicit.
  The literature sources are cited.
  The uncertainty is stated.
  The interpretation is the output
  of the framework applied to data.

  It is not clinical opinion.
  It is not medical judgment.
  It is geometric interpretation.

─────────────────────────────────────────

LAYER 3 — THE CLINICAL DECISION

  What treatment does this patient receive?
  Does the geometric finding change
  the treatment plan?
  How does the geometry integrate with
  the full clinical picture?

  This is medicine.
  This requires a license.
  This requires clinical training.
  This requires knowing the patient's
  complete history, organ function,
  performance status, prior treatments,
  comorbidities, and preferences.

  This layer is not performed here.
  This layer belongs to the
  licensed medical professional.

─────────────────────────────────────────

THE LINE:

  Layer 1 and Layer 2 are this practice.
  Layer 3 is not.

  This line does not move.
  It is not negotiable.
  It is not situational.
  It does not change based on how
  desperate a patient is or how
  certain the geometry appears.

  The line is the practice.
```

## III. WHAT THIS PRACTICE IS NOT

```
THIS PRACTICE IS NOT:

Practicing medicine.

  No diagnosis is made.
  No treatment is prescribed.
  No clinical judgment about
  a specific patient's care is rendered.
  No act requiring a medical license
  is performed.

  Ever.

─────────────────────────────────────────

Replacing the clinical team.

  The geometric report is additional
  information for the clinical team.
  It is not a replacement for them.
  It does not override their expertise.
  It does not supersede their judgment.
  It adds one dimension — geometric
  position in the attractor landscape —
  that standard clinical instruments
  do not currently measure.

  The clinical team remains essential.
  The geometric report is useful
  only in the presence of a clinical team.
  A patient who intends to use this
  report without any clinical oversight
  will not receive a report from
  this practice.

─────────────────────────────────────────

Providing certainty.

  The framework provides geometric
  measurements and principled
  interpretations derived from those
  measurements.

  It does not provide certainty.

  Some predictions will be wrong.
  Some geometric signals will not
  correlate with clinical outcomes.
  Some patients whose geometry looks
  favourable will progress.
  Some patients whose geometry looks
  concerning will respond unexpectedly.

  The framework is a compass.
  A compass tells you which direction
  is north.
  It does not tell you what you will
  find when you walk north.
  It does not guarantee you will
  reach your destination.

  Precision about what is known.
  Honesty about what is not.
  Every time.

─────────────────────────────────────────

Promising outcomes.

  No outcome is promised.
  Not survival.
  Not response.
  Not remission.
  Not quality of life improvement.

  The geometric report is a measurement.
  What happens clinically is determined
  by biology, treatment, the specific
  clinical decisions made, and factors
  the framework cannot measure.

  Anyone who promises outcomes
  from a geometric report is lying.
  This practice does not lie.
```

## IV. THE NATURE OF THE MONITORING VECTOR

```
The most important thing this practice
provides is not a single measurement.

It is a trajectory.

Position. Direction. Rate of change.

A vector in the mathematical sense.

THE CLINICAL VALUE:

  Standard clinical monitoring asks:
    Is the tumour shrinking?
    Are the markers falling?
    Has the patient progressed?

  These are scalar measurements.
  They answer: where are you now?
  They do not answer: where are you going?

  The geometric vector answers:
    Is the attractor dissolving?
    Is it reconstituting?
    Is the rate of change accelerating?
    Has the dominant attractor basin
    shifted — signalling clonal evolution
    toward resistance?

  These are leading indicators.
  Clinical measurements are lagging.

  The geometry changes before
  the tumour volume changes.
  Before the blood markers shift.
  Before clinical progression is registered.

  This is the window that matters.

  The window between geometric signal
  and clinical detection is where
  intervention is most effective.

  The monitoring vector opens that window.

─────────────────────────────────────────

WHAT THE VECTOR INFORMS:

  The geometric trajectory informs
  how to approach treatment.

  It does not prescribe treatment.

  The distinction is precise:

  INFORMING APPROACH:
    "The depth score is rising
    despite treatment.
    The attractor is reconstituting.
    The geometry suggests the epigenetic
    lock is re-establishing.
    The clinical question this raises:
    is there an epigenetic agent
    that could be added or substituted?"

    This is a geometric observation
    and the question it generates.
    The clinician answers the question.

  PRESCRIBING TREATMENT:
    "Add this drug at this dose."

    This is medicine.
    This is not performed here.

  The geometric vector generates
  the clinical question.
  The clinician answers the question.
  The answer becomes the treatment.
```

## V. THE DRUG CONNECTION STATEMENT

```
Every geometric report contains
connections between the patient's
attractor position and published
literature on drug targets.

These connections must always be
presented in the following way.
No exceptions.

─────────────────────────────────────────

WHAT THEY ARE:

  Literature-derived associations
  between geometric position in the
  attractor landscape and pharmacological
  targets established in published
  clinical research.

  Derived from population-level analysis.
  Validated retrospectively against
  published pharmacology and clinical trials.
  Presented as geometric observations
  with their literature source cited.

─────────────────────────────────────────

WHAT THEY ARE NOT:

  Treatment recommendations.
  Prescriptions.
  Clinical opinions.
  Guarantees that a specific drug
  will work for a specific patient.

─────────────────────────────────────────

HOW THEY MUST ALWAYS BE PRESENTED:

  "The geometry at this attractor
  position is associated in the
  population-level analysis with
  [target/pathway].
  In the published literature this
  connection is established by
  [citation].
  Whether this association is relevant
  to this patient's specific clinical
  situation requires assessment by
  a medical professional who knows
  the patient's complete clinical picture."

  Every drug connection in every report.
  Every time.
  Without exception.

─────────────────────────────────────────

THE REASON THIS MATTERS:

  The patient is not the population.

  The drug map is derived from
  hundreds of patients.
  It describes what is true
  on average.

  This patient's geometry places them
  at a position on that map.
  That position is associated with
  certain drug targets.

  But this patient also has:
    A complete medical history.
    Other medications.
    Organ function constraints.
    Prior treatments.
    Comorbidities.
    Drug access and tolerance.

  None of these are visible to
  the geometric analysis.

  The clinical professional sees all of it.

  The drug connection is a starting
  point for clinical conversation.
  Not an endpoint.
  Not a recommendation.
  A question to bring to the person
  who can answer it properly.
```

## VI. THE PATIENT RELATIONSHIP

```
THE PATIENT COMES FIRST.

Not the science.
Not the evidence accumulation.
Not the framework validation.
Not the reputation of the practice.

The patient.

Every decision in this practice —
what to report, how to report it,
when to stop, what to charge —
is made by asking one question first:

  What is in the best interest
  of the patient in front of me?

─────────────────────────────────────────

INFORMED CONSENT IS NOT FINE PRINT.

  The informed consent document is
  not a legal protection.
  It is a communication.

  It tells the patient exactly what
  they are receiving.
  What the framework is.
  What it is not.
  What is promised.
  What is not promised.
  What the validation status is.
  What the limitations are.

  A patient who signs the informed
  consent and then receives a report
  that surprises them with its
  limitations has not been properly
  consented.

  The consent conversation is as
  important as the consent document.

─────────────────────────────────────────

THE PATIENT'S DATA BELONGS TO THEM.

  Gene expression data from a patient's
  biopsy was generated from their cells.
  It describes their biology.
  It belongs to them.

  It is used only for their analysis.
  It is stored securely.
  It is identified only by an
  anonymous patient ID.
  It is never shared without explicit
  written consent.
  It is deleted immediately and
  completely upon request.
  No copies are retained after deletion.

─────────────────────────────────────────

VULNERABILITY REQUIRES EXTRA CARE.

  The people who come to this practice
  are often in the most frightening
  period of their lives.

  Vulnerability does not reduce
  ethical obligation.
  It increases it.

  The more frightened a patient is,
  the more carefully the results must
  be communicated.
  The more clearly the limitations
  must be stated.
  The more precisely the distinction
  between what is known and what is
  uncertain must be drawn.

  Comfort is not the goal.
  Clarity is.

  A clear picture — even a difficult one —
  serves the patient better than
  a comfortable picture that is imprecise.
```

## VII. HONEST UNCERTAINTY IS MANDATORY

```
Every report contains an honest
uncertainty section.

This is not optional.
It is not supplementary.
It is not added at the end
as a disclaimer.

It is a required structural component
of every report.

─────────────────────────────────────────

WHAT IT CONTAINS:

  What the framework is confident about
  in this specific analysis.
  Named specifically.
  With the data that supports it.

  What the framework is less certain about
  in this specific analysis.
  Named specifically.
  With the reason for the uncertainty.

  What the analysis cannot determine.
  Named explicitly.
  Not hidden.

  Data quality notes.
  Any genes missing from the panel.
  Any flags raised during verification.
  How these affect the confidence
  of specific findings.

─────────────────────────────────────────

WHY THIS IS MANDATORY:

  A report that presents all findings
  with equal confidence is lying.

  Some findings are strongly supported.
  Some are tentative.
  Some are genuinely uncertain.
  The patient and their clinical team
  deserve to know which is which.

  Presenting uncertain findings
  as certain is the thing this
  practice most needs to avoid.

  It is the form of dishonesty
  that causes the most harm
  to the most vulnerable people.

  Honest uncertainty is not weakness.
  It is precision.

  Precision is what this practice
  offers instead of false comfort.
```

## VIII. THE HARD CASES

```
These are the situations that test
the founding principle most severely.

─────────────────────────────────────────

HARD CASE 1:
THE PATIENT WHO WANTS CERTAINTY

  A patient is diagnosed with a
  cancer that has a poor prognosis.
  They receive the geometric report.
  They want the report to tell them
  they will be okay.
  They want certainty.

  The response:
    Give them what the geometry shows.
    Not what they want it to show.
    State the uncertainty where it exists.
    Do not soften findings that are
    concerning to spare their feelings.
    Do not amplify findings that are
    favourable beyond what the data supports.

  The founding principle applied:
    Promising certainty that does not
    exist is promising bullshit.
    It does not matter how much
    the patient wants it.
    It does not matter how much
    it would comfort them.
    The practice does not do it.

─────────────────────────────────────────

HARD CASE 2:
THE PATIENT WHO WANTS TO REPLACE
THEIR CLINICAL TEAM

  A patient is distrustful of the
  medical system.
  They want to use the geometric report
  as a substitute for all clinical care.
  They want to make treatment decisions
  based on the report alone.

  The response:
    Do not produce the report
    under these conditions.
    Communicate directly:
    "This report is designed to work
    alongside your clinical team.
    It is not designed to replace it.
    The drug connections in this report
    require clinical assessment that
    I am not qualified to provide.
    If you engage with a medical
    professional who can review these
    findings, I will produce the report.
    Without that clinical engagement,
    I cannot in good conscience
    provide it."

  The founding principle applied:
    Providing a report to a patient
    who intends to use it without
    clinical oversight is providing
    a tool they will misuse.
    The tool will fail them
    in ways the geometric report
    cannot prevent.
    That is promising more than
    the practice can deliver.
    The practice does not do it.

─────────────────────────────────────────

HARD CASE 3:
THE GEOMETRY THAT CARRIES BAD NEWS

  The geometric trajectory shows
  the attractor is reconstituting.
  The depth score is rising.
  The switch genes are being
  re-suppressed.
  The geometry is signalling
  early relapse before the
  clinical measurements have
  detected it.

  The response:
    Report it.
    Clearly.
    Specifically.
    With the data.
    As a priority signal for
    the clinical team.

    Do not soften it.
    Do not delay reporting it.
    Do not wait for the clinical
    measurement to confirm it
    before telling the patient.

    The whole value of the
    geometric monitoring vector
    is early detection of exactly
    this signal.
    Suppressing it defeats the
    entire purpose of the practice.

  The founding principle applied:
    The patient came for geometric
    truth about their cancer.
    They did not come for a
    comfortable version of it.
    Withholding a geometric signal
    because it is bad news is
    a form of promising bullshit
    by omission.
    The practice does not do it.

─────────────────────────────────────────

HARD CASE 4:
THE CASE WHERE THE FRAMEWORK IS WRONG

  A patient's geometric report predicted
  a favourable geometry.
  The patient progressed clinically
  anyway.
  The framework was wrong.

  The response:
    Acknowledge it directly.
    Document it completely.
    Investigate why.
    Update the reference geometry
    if the divergence reveals a
    gap in the framework.
    Tell the patient honestly
    that the geometric prediction
    did not match the clinical outcome
    and that this is being documented
    and investigated.

  The founding principle applied:
    A practice that hides its failures
    cannot be trusted.
    Wrong predictions are information.
    They improve the framework.
    They are documented alongside
    correct predictions.
    The same standard that applies
    to the population analyses applies
    to individual patient cases.
    Honest accounting of failures
    is not a weakness.
    It is the foundation of credibility.
```

## IX. WHAT IS CHARGED AND WHY

```
This practice charges for the work.

Not for the outcome.
Not for the hope.
Not for the framework's potential.

For the work.

─────────────────────────────────────────

WHAT THE CHARGE REFLECTS:

  The time required to:
    Receive and verify the data.
    Extract the reference geometry.
    Run the computation.
    Produce the geometric report.
    Explain the findings.
    Answer questions about the report.

  The expertise required to:
    Apply the framework correctly.
    Interpret the geometric findings
    with the precision they require.
    State uncertainty honestly.
    Communicate findings clearly.

  The responsibility of:
    Handling sensitive patient data
    with appropriate care.
    Producing a document that will
    be used in a clinical context.
    Standing behind every number
    in the report.

─────────────────────────────────────────

WHAT THE CHARGE DOES NOT REFLECT:

  The desperation of the patient.
  The severity of their diagnosis.
  The scarcity of other options.
  The hope that the report
  will change their outcome.

  These factors are never used
  to increase the charge.
  Ever.

─────────────────────────────────────────

THE SLIDING SCALE:

  Financial hardship is not a reason
  to be denied geometric analysis
  of your own cancer.

  Patients who cannot afford the
  standard charge will be assessed
  individually.
  Some analyses will be provided
  at reduced cost.
  Some will be provided at no cost.

  The financial capacity of the patient
  does not determine whether their
  cancer geometry is worth measuring.

  All cancer geometry is worth measuring.
  Financial hardship does not change that.

─────────────────────────────────────────

THE RULE:

  The charge must be something that,
  if published alongside the report,
  would cause no embarrassment.

  If it would cause embarrassment —
  it is too high.
  Reduce it.
```

## X. THE LEGAL POSITION

```
This is stated precisely because
precision here protects patients
and the practice both.

─────────────────────────────────────────

WHAT IS PERFORMED:

  Computational analysis of gene
  expression data provided voluntarily
  by a patient.

  The computation produces a geometric
  measurement of the patient's cancer
  attractor state.

  The measurement is returned to the
  patient as a structured report.

  The report contains:
    Mathematical outputs.
    Literature-derived associations
    presented as questions for
    clinical review.
    Explicit statements of what
    is and is not established.
    An honest uncertainty section.
    A reminder that all clinical
    decisions belong to the
    licensed medical team.

─────────────────────────────────────────

WHAT IS NOT PERFORMED:

  No diagnosis.
  No prescription.
  No clinical opinion on individual
  treatment decisions.
  No act requiring a medical license
  under any jurisdiction's definition
  of the practice of medicine.

─────────────────────────────────────────

THE ANALOGY THAT CLARIFIES:

  Computing a depth score from
  gene expression data is mathematics.

  It does not require a medical license
  any more than computing a patient's
  BMI from height and weight requires
  a medical license.

  The geometric report is the output
  of that computation.

  The clinical interpretation and
  treatment application of that output
  is the responsibility of the
  licensed medical professional.

  That is where the practice ends.
  That is where medicine begins.
  The line is clear.

─────────────────────────────────────────

DOCUMENTATION FOR LEGAL CLARITY:

  Every engagement is documented.
  Informed consent is signed and filed.
  Every report contains the explicit
  statement that it is not medical advice.
  Every drug connection is presented
  as a literature association requiring
  clinical assessment.
  The case record preserves the
  complete chain of what was done,
  when, and with what data.

  This documentation is not defensive.
  It is the natural product of
  doing the work honestly.
  A practice that documents everything
  has nothing to hide.
  This practice documents everything.
```

## XI. AS THE PRACTICE GROWS

```
This practice may train others.

Every person who learns this framework
and applies it to patient data is bound
by this document.

Not by the technical protocol.
Not by the scientific framework.
By this document.

─────────────────────────────────────────

THE STANDARD FOR ANYONE WHO
DOES THIS WORK:

  They must understand the founding
  principle and mean it.

  Not recite it.
  Mean it.

  They must be able to hold the line
  between Layer 2 and Layer 3
  under pressure.

  When a patient is desperate.
  When the geometry looks clear.
  When the drug connection seems obvious.

  The line still holds.
  Layer 3 still belongs to the clinician.
  The report still ends with questions
  for the clinical team.
  The uncertainty section is still honest.

  Anyone who cannot hold that line
  should not do this work.

─────────────────────────────────────────

THE THING THAT CANNOT BE SCALED AWAY:

  As this practice grows —
  more analysts, more patients,
  more cancer types, more data —
  the one thing that cannot be
  scaled away is this:

  Every patient whose data is analysed
  is a person.
  Not a data point.
  Not a case number.
  Not evidence for the framework.

  A person.
  Frightened.
  Seeking clarity about what is
  happening in their own cells.

  That person deserves the same
  precision, honesty, and care
  as the first patient.
  And the hundredth.
  And the thousandth.

  The scale does not change the
  obligation.
  The obligation is per person.
  It does not average out.

─────────────────────────────────────────

IF THIS DOCUMENT IS EVER VIOLATED:

  If anyone doing this work —
  including the author of this framework —
  produces a report that overstates
  certainty, understates uncertainty,
  crosses into clinical prescription,
  or exploits patient vulnerability
  for financial gain:

  That violation must be acknowledged.
  The patient must be informed.
  The record must be corrected.
  The practice must return to
  this document and account for
  what happened.

  The founding principle is not
  a statement made once in 2026
  and then forgotten.

  It governs every report.
  Every engagement.
  Every patient.

  As long as this practice exists.
```
