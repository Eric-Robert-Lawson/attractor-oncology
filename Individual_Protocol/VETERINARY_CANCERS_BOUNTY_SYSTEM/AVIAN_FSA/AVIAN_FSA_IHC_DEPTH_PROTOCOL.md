## STATUS: ACTIVE — REQUIRED BEFORE VERTEPORFIN OR TAZEMETOSTAT

```
This test has three functions:

FUNCTION 1 — CONFIRM THE TWO-LAYER
ATTRACTOR:
  Confirm that YAP1 is constitutively
  nuclear in the tumour cells
  (Layer 1 active) and that EZH2
  is elevated (Layer 2 active).
  Both layers must be confirmed
  for the full protocol to apply.
  If only one layer is active,
  the single corresponding drug
  is indicated.

FUNCTION 2 — DETERMINE WHICH LAYER
IS DOMINANT:
  If YAP1 nuclear is high and EZH2
  is intermediate: Layer 1 is
  dominant. Verteporfin is the
  priority drug and tazemetostat
  is the reinforcing drug.
  If EZH2 is high and YAP1 nuclear
  is intermediate: Layer 2 is
  entrenched. Tazemetostat is the
  priority drug and verteporfin
  addresses the residual driver.
  If both are high: both layers
  equally active — full two-drug
  protocol is indicated.

FUNCTION 3 — DEPTH SCORE:
  Establish the depth of the false
  attractor to predict response
  timeline.

MELOXICAM CAN START BEFORE THIS TEST.
  Anti-inflammatory supportive care
  starts at diagnosis. The IHC test
  runs in parallel. Verteporfin and
  tazemetostat are held until the
  IHC result is available.

TISSUE ACQUISITION IN AVIAN FSA:
  This is the most accessible biopsy
  in the entire avian bounty system.
  The mass is subcutaneous and
  externally visible. A punch biopsy
  under local anaesthesia + sedation
  is the standard approach. A 3–4mm
  punch biopsy from the viable,
  non-necrotic margin of the mass
  provides sufficient material for
  FFPE IHC panel.
  Fixation: 10% NBF standard.
  Processing: FFPE block.
  Sections: 4-micron for each stain.

FOUR PROTEINS ARE ASSESSED:
  YAP1  — the Identity Anchor
          equivalent. Nuclear vs
          cytoplasmic localisation
          is the critical read-out.
  EZH2  — the primary Hub.
          Standard H-score.
  LATS1 — the Hippo pathway kinase
          that phosphorylates and
          sequesters YAP1.
          Confirmatory marker for
          the upstream Hippo pathway
          constraint status.
  DNMT3A — secondary Hub.

NOTE ON YAP1 SCORING:
  For YAP1, standard H-score is
  insufficient. The critical
  measurement is the ratio of
  nuclear to cytoplasmic staining.
  Score YAP1 nuclear H-score AND
  YAP1 cytoplasmic H-score separately.
  The nuclear/cytoplasmic ratio
  (NCR) is the key value:
  NCR > 2: constitutive nuclear
    activity confirmed.
    Layer 1 of false attractor
    active. Verteporfin indicated.
  NCR 1–2: intermediate.
    Partial nuclear activity.
  NCR < 1: YAP1 predominantly
    cytoplasmic. Hippo constraint
    partially active. YAP1 is not
    dominant Layer 1 driver in
    this case. Check EZH2 for
    standalone Layer 2 mechanism.
```

## WHAT IS BEING MEASURED

```
PROTEIN 1 — YAP1
(Yes-Associated Protein 1)
  The Identity Anchor equivalent.
  Score nuclear AND cytoplasmic
  separately. Calculate NCR.

  NCR > 2 AND EZH2 High:
    Full two-layer attractor
    confirmed. Full protocol:
    verteporfin + tazemetostat.
    Best predicted response.

  NCR > 2 AND EZH2 Low:
    Layer 1 dominant. Verteporfin
    is the primary drug. Check
    DNMT3A — if high, add azacitidine
    as Layer 2 alternative.

  NCR < 1 AND EZH2 High:
    Layer 2 dominant. EZH2 lock
    is active but not being driven
    by YAP1 currently. Tazemetostat
    is the primary drug. Verteporfin
    may not be needed.
    Possible alternative upstream
    driver — document and report.

  NCR < 1 AND EZH2 Low:
    Neither layer confirmed.
    Do not escalate. Seek further
    pathological review.
    Contact OrganismCore@proton.me.

PROTEIN 2 — EZH2
(Enhancer of Zeste Homolog 2)
  The primary Hub.
  Nuclear staining.
  Tumour cells only.

  High H-score (> 150):
    Layer 2 confirmed and active.
    EZH2 lock on quiescence genes
    is established.
    Tazemetostat indicated.

  Intermediate (80–150):
    EZH2 active. Check DNMT3A.
    If also elevated: dual epigenetic
    lock. Tazemetostat first.

  Low (< 80):
    EZH2 not dominant hub in
    this case. Check DNMT3A.

PROTEIN 3 — LATS1
(Large Tumour Suppressor Kinase 1)
  The Hippo pathway kinase.
  Confirmatory marker for the
  upstream Hippo constraint status.
  Nuclear and cytoplasmic staining.
  Tumour cells compared to
  stromal cells in the same section.

  LATS1 Low in tumour:
    Hippo pathway suppressed.
    YAP1 is not being phosphorylated
    and retained in the cytoplasm.
    This confirms the upstream
    mechanism of YAP1 nuclear
    accumulation — the Hippo
    constraint is broken at the
    LATS1 level.
    Confirms Layer 1 mechanism.

  LATS1 High in tumour:
    Hippo pathway partially intact.
    YAP1 nuclear activity may be
    driven by an alternative
    mechanism (LATS1-independent
    YAP1 activation or direct
    epigenetic bypass).
    Document. Still proceed with
    verteporfin if YAP1 NCR > 2.

  NOTE: LATS1 antibody cross-
  reactivity in avian tissue
  must be confirmed with mammalian
  positive control. If cross-
  reactivity fails, omit LATS1
  and note in report. The YAP1
  NCR + EZH2 H-score are sufficient
  for treatment decision.

PROTEIN 4 — DNMT3A
(DNA Methyltransferase 3A)
  Secondary Hub.
  Nuclear staining.
  Tumour cells only.

  High (> 150) with EZH2 also high:
    Dual epigenetic lock. Tazemetostat
    first. Reassess at week 8.
    Add azacitidine if plateau.

  High with EZH2 low:
    DNMT3A is primary epigenetic hub.
    Azacitidine is Layer 2 drug.
    Verteporfin still indicated
    if YAP1 NCR > 2.

  Low:
    Not a dominant hub.
    EZH2 assessment is sufficient.

THE COMPOSITE DEPTH SCORE:
  SHALLOW ATTRACTOR:
    YAP1 NCR: 2–3
    EZH2: 100–200
    LATS1: Low
    → Both layers active but not
      deeply entrenched.
    → Verteporfin + tazemetostat.
    → Mass reduction expected
      at 4–6 weeks.

  INTERMEDIATE ATTRACTOR:
    YAP1 NCR: 3–5
    EZH2: 150–250
    LATS1: Very low
    → Both layers deeply active.
    → Full protocol.
    → Mass reduction expected
      at 6–8 weeks.

  DEEP ATTRACTOR:
    YAP1 NCR: > 5
    EZH2: > 250
    LATS1: Undetectable
    → Both layers maximally active.
    → Full protocol.
    → Commit to 12-week minimum.
    → Consider DNMT3A extension
      from the outset if dual-hub.
```

## THE TEST — STEP BY STEP

```
STEP 1 — TISSUE ACQUISITION:
  Punch biopsy under local
  anaesthesia + sedation.
  3–4mm punch from the viable,
  non-necrotic margin of the
  subcutaneous mass.
  Fixation: 10% NBF.
  Processing: FFPE block.
  Sections: 4-micron per stain.

STEP 2 — IHC PANEL:
  Stain 1: H&E (confirm fibrosarcoma
    morphology — spindle cell,
    collagenous stroma, infiltrative
    margin)
  Stain 2: Anti-YAP1
    (score nuclear AND cytoplasmic
    separately)
  Stain 3: Anti-EZH2
  Stain 4: Anti-LATS1
    (confirmatory — omit if cross-
    reactivity fails)
  Stain 5: Anti-DNMT3A

STEP 3 — SCORING:
  YAP1: Score nuclear H-score and
    cytoplasmic H-score separately.
    Calculate NCR = nuclear /
    cytoplasmic.
  All others: standard H-score
    from tumour cells.
    Score minimum 100 cells per
    stain from viable areas.
    Two high-power fields (40×).
    Report mean.

STEP 4 — CALCULATE DEPTH SCORE:
  YAP1 NCR: ________
  EZH2 H-score: ________
  LATS1 (relative to stroma): ____
  DNMT3A H-score: ________
  Dominant layer: ☐ Layer 1
                  ☐ Layer 2
                  ☐ Both equal
  Depth: ☐ Shallow ☐ Intermediate
         ☐ Deep

STEP 5 — CLASSIFY AND RECOMMEND:
  Apply decision logic above.
  Document YAP1 NCR, EZH2 H-score,
  dominant layer, depth, recommended
  drugs, predicted response timeline,
  and photosensitivity protocol
  for verteporfin if indicated.

STEP 6 — REPORT TO CLINICIAN:
  Complete the IHC Reporting
  Template below.
```
