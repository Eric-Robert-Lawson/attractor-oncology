## WHAT IS BEING MEASURED

```
PROTEIN 1 — BCL6
(B-cell lymphoma 6)
  The Identity Anchor.
  Nuclear staining in tumour cells.
  The critical question: is BCL6
  aberrantly retained at high
  levels consistent with a GC-locked
  programme?

  High H-score (> 150):
    BCL6 aberrant retention
    confirmed. GC lock active.
    BCL6 is currently suppressing
    BLIMP1/IRF4. Tazemetostat
    will open the window for
    differentiation by removing
    the EZH2/H3K27me3 maintenance
    of the BCL6-initiated lock.
    Fastest predicted response
    — BCL6 is driving EZH2, so
    removing EZH2 immediately
    derepresses BLIMP1/IRF4.

  Intermediate H-score (80–150):
    Partial GC lock. EZH2 may
    be acting more autonomously.
    Still respond to tazemetostat.
    Check R ratio.

  Low H-score (< 80):
    GC programme not dominant.
    May be a more advanced or
    plasmablastic stage. Check
    PAX5 and DNMT3A.
    If EZH2 still high: the
    EZH2 lock is maintained
    independently of BCL6 —
    tazemetostat still applicable.
    Document this pattern.

PROTEIN 2 — EZH2
(Enhancer of Zeste Homolog 2)
  The primary Hub.
  Nuclear staining in tumour cells.

  FRAMEWORK PREDICTION:
  EZH2 will be elevated in avian
  B-cell lymphoma tumour cells
  regardless of EZH2 mutation
  status — because the GC programme
  requires EZH2 activity to maintain
  itself by the conserved biology
  of the germinal centre reaction.
  This IHC result TESTS this
  framework prediction directly.
  If EZH2 H-score is high in a
  confirmed avian B-cell lymphoma:
  the framework's EZH2-by-mechanism
  claim is confirmed for this case.
  If EZH2 H-score is low in a
  confirmed GC-type avian B-cell
  lymphoma: this is an important
  negative finding that refines the
  framework.

  High H-score (> 150):
    EZH2 lock confirmed and active.
    Tazemetostat indicated.
    Framework prediction confirmed
    for this case.

  Intermediate (80–150):
    EZH2 active but not dominant.
    Tazemetostat still indicated.
    Check DNMT3A for co-hub.

  Low (< 80) with BCL6 high:
    EZH2 is unexpectedly low despite
    active GC programme.
    This is an important anomaly.
    Document carefully and report
    to OrganismCore@proton.me.
    Tazemetostat may not address
    the hub in this case. Check
    DNMT3A.

PROTEIN 3 — PAX5
(Paired Box 5)
  B-cell identity marker.
  Nuclear staining.
  Confirms B-cell lineage.

  PAX5 high:
    B-cell lymphoma confirmed.
    Proceed with full BCL6/EZH2
    protocol.

  PAX5 low with BCL6 low:
    Possible plasmablastic
    differentiation stage — the
    lymphoma may have partially
    exited the GC programme already
    (rare in avian lymphoma).
    Document. The EZH2 hub may
    still be active. Check EZH2
    independently.

  PAX5 low with CD3 high:
    T-cell lymphoma — not B-cell.
    This protocol (BCL6/EZH2 GC
    lock) does not apply.
    Do NOT use this protocol for
    T-cell lymphoma without separate
    framework derivation.
    Contact OrganismCore@proton.me.
    Document fully.

  NOTE ON CD3:
    Add CD3 to the panel as a
    lineage discriminator alongside
    PAX5. CD3-positive / PAX5-
    negative = T-cell lymphoma.
    CD3-negative / PAX5-positive
    = B-cell lymphoma confirmed.

PROTEIN 4 — DNMT3A
(DNA Methyltransferase 3A)
  Secondary Hub.
  Nuclear staining.
  Tumour cells only.

  High with EZH2 also high:
    Dual epigenetic lock.
    Both H3K27me3 (EZH2) and
    DNA methylation (DNMT3A) locks
    active on differentiation gene
    promoters. Tazemetostat first.
    Reassess at week 8. If plateau:
    add azacitidine.

  High with EZH2 low:
    DNMT3A is the primary epigenetic
    hub in this case. Azacitidine
    is the escalation drug.
    Tazemetostat may still be added
    but DNMT3A is the dominant
    lock. Atypical — document.

  Low:
    DNMT3A not a dominant hub.
    EZH2/BCL6 assessment sufficient.

THE COMPOSITE ASSESSMENT:

  IDEAL CASE FOR THIS PROTOCOL:
    PAX5: High (B-cell confirmed)
    BCL6: High (GC lock active)
    EZH2: High (hub confirmed)
    DNMT3A: Low to intermediate
    R (BCL6/EZH2): > 0.5
    → Full protocol: chlorambucil
      + prednisolone + tazemetostat.
    → EZH2-by-mechanism framework
      prediction confirmed for
      this case.
    → Expected enhanced remission
      duration vs SOC alone.

  EZH2 HIGH, BCL6 LOW CASE:
    BCL6: Low (< 80)
    EZH2: High (> 150)
    → EZH2 lock is entrenched and
      now partially BCL6-independent.
    → Deeper false attractor.
    → Tazemetostat still indicated.
    → Slower response timeline.
    → Check DNMT3A for co-hub.

  T-CELL CASE:
    CD3: High / PAX5: Low
    → STOP. This protocol does
      not apply.
    → Contact OrganismCore@proton.me
      for T-cell derivation guidance.
    → Document and report.
```

## THE TEST — STEP BY STEP

```
STEP 1 — TISSUE ACQUISITION:
  Cutaneous form: punch biopsy.
  Visceral form: ultrasound-guided
    FNA with cell block.
  Leukaemic form: buffy coat +
    FNA of most accessible
    visceral mass.
  Post-mortem: fresh tissue
    immediate FFPE fixation.

STEP 2 — IHC PANEL:
  Stain 1: H&E (confirm lymphoma
    morphology)
  Stain 2: Anti-PAX5
    (B-cell identity confirmation)
  Stain 3: Anti-CD3
    (T-cell lineage exclusion)
  Stain 4: Anti-BCL6
    (Identity Anchor)
  Stain 5: Anti-EZH2
    (primary Hub)
  Stain 6: Anti-DNMT3A
    (secondary Hub)

STEP 3 — SCORING:
  Standard H-score from tumour
  cells for all stains.
  Minimum 100 cells per stain
  from viable, non-necrotic areas.
  Two HPF (40×). Report mean.
  PAX5 and CD3: report as
  positive/negative with H-score.

STEP 4 — LINEAGE CONFIRMATION:
  PAX5+ / CD3-: B-cell confirmed.
    Proceed to Step 5.
  PAX5- / CD3+: T-cell lymphoma.
    STOP. Do not proceed with
    this protocol.
    Contact OrganismCore@proton.me.
  PAX5+ / CD3+: mixed or double-
    positive — rare. Document.
    Contact OrganismCore@proton.me.
  PAX5- / CD3-: non-lymphoid
    or null cell. Reassess diagnosis.

STEP 5 — CALCULATE R:
  R = BCL6 H-score / EZH2 H-score

  R > 1.0: BCL6 dominant.
    GC programme actively driving
    EZH2. Fast predicted response.

  R 0.3–1.0: Balanced.
    BCL6 and EZH2 co-dominant.
    Standard timeline.

  R < 0.3: EZH2 dominant.
    Lock is entrenched. BCL6 may
    have been downregulated but
    EZH2/H3K27me3 persists.
    Slower response timeline.
    Commit to 12-week minimum.

STEP 6 — CLASSIFY AND RECOMMEND:
  Apply decision logic above.
  Document B-cell lineage confirmed,
  BCL6 status, EZH2 status, R ratio,
  depth, recommended escalation
  drugs, and predicted response
  timeline. Note explicitly whether
  EZH2 mutation testing was or
  was not performed — and confirm
  that the framework prediction
  does NOT require EZH2 mutation
  positivity for tazemetostat
  indication in this protocol.

STEP 7 — REPORT TO CLINICIAN:
  Complete the IHC Reporting
  Template below.
```
