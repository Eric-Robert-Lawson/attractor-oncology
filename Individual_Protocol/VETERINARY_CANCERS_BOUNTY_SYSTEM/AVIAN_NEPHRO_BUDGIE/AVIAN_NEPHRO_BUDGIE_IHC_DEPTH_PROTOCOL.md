```
This test has three functions:

FUNCTION 1 — CONFIRM BLASTEMAL
IDENTITY:
  Confirm the tumour is nephroblastoma
  (blastemal cell morphology) and
  that WT1 is absent or aberrant —
  i.e., the Identity Anchor failure
  is confirmed. PAX2 confirms
  renal progenitor programme
  engagement (partial or absent).

FUNCTION 2 — CONFIRM EZH2 HUB
ACTIVITY:
  Confirm EZH2 is elevated in
  the blastemal tumour cells —
  i.e., that the epigenetic lock
  on the MET programme is active
  and that tazemetostat will
  address the hub.

  The framework predicts EZH2
  will be elevated in avian
  nephroblastoma by conservation
  of the developmental biology:
  blastemal cells require EZH2
  to suppress the MET programme
  and maintain the undifferentiated
  false attractor. This IHC test
  directly tests that prediction.

FUNCTION 3 — DEPTH SCORE:
  Establish how deeply the false
  attractor is locked via the R
  ratio (WT1/EZH2). Predict
  response timeline and whether
  DNMT3A co-hub extension will
  be needed.

TISSUE ACQUISITION IN AVIAN
NEPHROBLASTOMA:
  Avian nephroblastoma presents
  as a retroperitoneal renal mass.
  Biopsy options are limited by
  the fixed, deep anatomical
  location.

  Ultrasound-guided FNA:
    The most accessible approach
    in a living bird. An avian
    specialist or exotic vet with
    ultrasound capability can
    perform FNA of a renal mass.
    Cell block from FNA provides
    adequate material for IHC if
    blastemal cellularity is
    sufficient (> 200 cells on
    the cell block). The blastemal
    morphology is distinctive —
    small, round, densely packed
    blue cells — and can be
    confirmed on cytology before
    committing to full IHC.

  Endoscopic biopsy:
    Avian endoscopy is performed
    under general anaesthesia
    through the left lateral
    approach to the coelomic cavity.
    An experienced avian vet can
    obtain a small tissue core from
    a coelomic mass under endoscopic
    guidance. This is FFPE
    processable.

  Post-mortem tissue:
    Where the bird dies or is
    euthanised at or after
    presentation: fresh kidney
    tissue immediate FFPE fixation
    provides the definitive sample.
    Post-mortem tissue is acceptable
    for the IHC test. The bounty
    result template records post-
    mortem outcomes as valid case
    data.

  NOTE — small size:
    In a 40g budgerigar, tissue
    samples are very small. An avian
    reference pathology laboratory
    with experience in psittacine
    histopathology is recommended
    for processing and staining.
    Minimum 100 tumour cells per
    stain for H-score validity.

FOUR PROTEINS ARE ASSESSED:
  WT1   — the Identity Anchor.
          Confirms loss/aberrance
          of the MET driver.
  EZH2  — the primary Hub.
          Confirms epigenetic lock.
  PAX2  — renal progenitor marker.
          Indicates depth of
          blastemal arrest.
  DNMT3A — secondary Hub.
```

## WHAT IS BEING MEASURED

```
PROTEIN 1 — WT1
(Wilms Tumour suppressor 1)
  The Identity Anchor.
  Nuclear staining in tumour cells.

  The critical question: is WT1
  absent or aberrant — confirming
  that the Identity Anchor has
  failed and the MET programme
  cannot be initiated?

  Low/Absent H-score (< 40):
    WT1 loss confirmed.
    Identity Anchor absent.
    The MET programme is not being
    driven. The blastemal false
    attractor is maintained by
    the complete absence of the
    exit driver.
    EZH2 is predicted to be high —
    locking the MET genes in
    the silenced state with no
    WT1 counterforce.
    Tazemetostat indicated.
    Derepression of MET genes will
    allow the downstream MET
    machinery to function even
    without WT1, because the
    downstream cascade (E-cadherin,
    PAX2 re-expression, etc.) is
    intact — it was just epigenetically
    silenced.

  Intermediate H-score (40–100):
    WT1 partially retained but
    insufficient to drive MET.
    The Identity Anchor is present
    but functionally compromised.
    EZH2 may be locking the MET
    genes that residual WT1 could
    otherwise activate.
    Tazemetostat indicated — and
    potentially more responsive
    than the low WT1 case: residual
    WT1 activity can cooperate with
    derepressed MET genes to drive
    differentiation.

  High H-score (> 100):
    WT1 retained at substantial
    levels.
    This is an unusual finding for
    nephroblastoma.
    Reassess diagnosis — consider
    whether this is a WT1-independent
    renal tumour type.
    Check PAX2 and EZH2.
    If EZH2 is high despite WT1
    being retained: EZH2 may be
    overriding intact WT1 signalling
    — aggressive deep attractor.
    Contact OrganismCore@proton.me
    for this pattern — important
    for framework refinement.

PROTEIN 2 — EZH2
(Enhancer of Zeste Homolog 2)
  The primary Hub.
  Nuclear staining in tumour cells.

  FRAMEWORK PREDICTION:
  EZH2 will be elevated in avian
  nephroblastoma blastemal cells
  — because the blastemal false
  attractor requires EZH2 to
  suppress the MET programme and
  maintain the undifferentiated
  state. This is predicted by
  conservation of the WT1/EZH2
  antagonism in vertebrate renal
  development. The IHC result
  TESTS this prediction directly.

  High H-score (> 150):
    EZH2 lock confirmed.
    Tazemetostat directly indicated.
    Framework prediction confirmed
    for this case.

  Intermediate (80–150):
    EZH2 active but not dominant.
    Tazemetostat still indicated.
    Consider whether DNMT3A is
    a co-hub.

  Low (< 80) with WT1 absent:
    EZH2 is unexpectedly low
    despite Identity Anchor loss.
    This is an important anomaly.
    Check DNMT3A — DNA methylation
    may be the primary epigenetic
    enforcement mechanism in this
    case rather than H3K27me3.
    Document carefully.
    Contact OrganismCore@proton.me.

PROTEIN 3 — PAX2
(Paired Box 2)
  Renal progenitor marker.
  Nuclear staining in tumour cells.

  PAX2 is expressed in the
  metanephric mesenchyme and is
  one of the TFs that WT1
  activates during the MET
  programme. It is also expressed
  in committed nephron progenitors.

  PAX2 high:
    The renal progenitor programme
    is partially engaged. The cells
    express a nephron progenitor
    marker despite being blastemal.
    This indicates the false attractor
    is at a relatively early stage
    — the cells have some progenitor
    programme but cannot complete
    the transition. More responsive
    to tazemetostat: the progenitor
    programme is partially active
    and will engage more fully
    when the EZH2 lock is removed.

  PAX2 low:
    The cells are deeply dedifferentiated.
    The renal progenitor programme
    is not engaged. Deeper false
    attractor. Tazemetostat may
    need to first derepress PAX2
    itself before the full
    differentiation cascade can
    proceed. Slower response
    timeline. Deeper commitment
    needed.

PROTEIN 4 — DNMT3A
(DNA Methyltransferase 3A)
  Secondary Hub.
  Nuclear staining.
  Tumour cells only.

  High with EZH2 also high:
    Dual epigenetic lock.
    Both H3K27me3 (EZH2) and
    DNA methylation (DNMT3A) active
    on MET programme promoters.
    Tazemetostat first. Reassess
    at week 8. If plateau:
    add azacitidine.

  High with EZH2 low:
    DNA methylation is the primary
    epigenetic hub.
    Azacitidine is the escalation
    drug, potentially as primary
    agent.
    Tazemetostat may still be
    added but DNMT3A dominates.
    Atypical — document.

  Low:
    DNMT3A not a dominant hub.
    EZH2/WT1 assessment sufficient.

THE COMPOSITE ASSESSMENT:

  IDEAL CASE FOR THIS PROTOCOL:
    WT1: Absent/Low (< 40)
    EZH2: High (> 150)
    PAX2: Intermediate to high
    DNMT3A: Low to intermediate
    R (WT1/EZH2): < 0.3
    → Full protocol: vinorelbine
      (or supportive) +
      tazemetostat.
    → Framework prediction confirmed:
      EZH2 elevated in avian
      nephroblastoma blastemal cells
      by conservation of developmental
      biology.
    → Expected leg function
      improvement at weeks 2–6.

  WT1 INTERMEDIATE / EZH2 HIGH:
    WT1: 40–100 (partial)
    EZH2: High (> 150)
    → Most responsive case.
      Residual WT1 will cooperate
      with derepressed MET genes
      after tazemetostat.
    → Fastest predicted response.

  DEEP ATTRACTOR — DUAL HUB:
    WT1: Absent
    EZH2: High
    DNMT3A: High
    PAX2: Low
    → Tazemetostat first. Azacitidine
      if plateau at week 8.

  EZH2 LOW, DNMT3A HIGH:
    → DNA methylation is the primary
      hub. Azacitidine-first approach.
    → Document. Contact
      OrganismCore@proton.me.
```

## THE TEST — STEP BY STEP

```
STEP 1 — TISSUE ACQUISITION:
  FNA cell block (preferred
    in living bird).
  Endoscopic biopsy (FFPE).
  Post-mortem: fresh kidney
    tissue immediate FFPE.

STEP 2 — CONFIRM MORPHOLOGY:
  H&E stain first.
  Confirm blastemal morphology:
  small, round, densely packed
  cells with scant cytoplasm,
  high nuclear:cytoplasmic ratio,
  frequent mitoses.
  Note if triphasic elements
  (blastemal + epithelial + stromal)
  are present — document proportions.
  Blastemal-predominant cases are
  most likely to carry the deep
  EZH2 lock.

STEP 3 — IHC PANEL:
  Stain 1: H&E (confirm morphology)
  Stain 2: Anti-WT1
    (Identity Anchor — confirm loss)
  Stain 3: Anti-EZH2
    (primary Hub — confirm lock)
  Stain 4: Anti-PAX2
    (depth of blastemal arrest)
  Stain 5: Anti-DNMT3A
    (secondary Hub)

STEP 4 — SCORING:
  Standard H-score from tumour
  cells for all stains.
  Focus on blastemal component
  where triphasic elements present.
  Minimum 100 cells per stain.
  Two HPF (40×). Report mean.

STEP 5 — CALCULATE R:
  R = WT1 H-score / EZH2 H-score

  R < 0.3: EZH2 dominant, WT1
    largely absent. Deep lock.
    Tazemetostat is the primary
    epigenetic agent. Slower
    predicted response — longer
    course.

  R 0.3–0.8: Partial WT1 retention.
    EZH2 is overriding residual WT1.
    Tazemetostat + residual WT1
    cooperate. Moderate timeline.

  R > 0.8: WT1 retained at
    unexpected levels. Reassess
    diagnosis. Contact
    OrganismCore@proton.me.

STEP 6 — CLASSIFY AND RECOMMEND:
  Apply decision logic above.
  Document blastemal morphology
  confirmed, WT1 status, EZH2
  status, PAX2 status, R ratio,
  depth, recommended protocol,
  and predicted leg function
  improvement timeline.
  Specify whether the bird has
  already received cytoreductive
  treatment and current leg
  lameness grade.

STEP 7 — REPORT TO CLINICIAN:
  Complete the IHC Reporting
  Template below.
```
