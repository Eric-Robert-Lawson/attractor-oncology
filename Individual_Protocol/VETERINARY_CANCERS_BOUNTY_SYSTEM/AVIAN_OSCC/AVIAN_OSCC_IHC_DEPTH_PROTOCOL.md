## STATUS: ACTIVE — REQUIRED BEFORE TAZEMETOSTAT OR AZACITIDINE

```
This test has two functions:

FUNCTION 1 — PATIENT SELECTION:
  Confirms whether tazemetostat
  (EZH2-dominant cases) or
  azacitidine (DNMT3A-dominant
  cases) is the correct second
  drug for this specific patient.

FUNCTION 2 — DEPTH SCORE:
  Establishes the depth of the
  false attractor in this patient —
  predicting how fast and how
  completely the response to
  tazemetostat will be.

  High R (TP63 > EZH2):
    Residual differentiation
    programme signal. Shallow
    false attractor. Faster
    response to tazemetostat.
    Tumour likely more
    well-differentiated.

  Low R (EZH2 >> TP63):
    Deep false attractor.
    TP63-governed differentiation
    balance heavily overridden
    by EZH2. Slower response.
    Likely poorly differentiated
    or spindle-cell morphology.

PIROXICAM CAN START BEFORE
THIS TEST.
  Piroxicam is existing standard
  first-line care for avian OSCC.
  It does not require the depth
  test. It can begin immediately
  on diagnosis.
  The depth test runs in parallel
  while piroxicam is being
  tolerated and the owner decides
  about escalation.
  The IHC result is typically
  ready before the first piroxicam
  reassessment at week 4–6.

FOUR PROTEINS ARE ASSESSED:
  TP63   — the Identity Anchor.
  SOX2   — the squamous lineage-
           survival co-factor.
           Confirmatory marker —
           not a separate target.
  EZH2   — the primary Convergence Hub.
  DNMT3A — the secondary Hub
           (if EZH2 insufficient).

NOTE ON TUMOUR DIFFERENTIATION
AND EXPECTED DEPTH:
  Well-differentiated OSCC:
    Keratin pearls present, strong
    squamous architecture, keratinised
    cells visible.
    → TP63 expression likely
      moderate to high.
    → R likely > 0.8.
    → Shallow attractor.
    → Faster tazemetostat response.

  Moderately differentiated OSCC:
    Partial squamous architecture.
    Some keratinisation.
    → R likely 0.3–0.8.
    → Intermediate depth.

  Poorly differentiated OSCC:
    No squamous architecture.
    High mitotic rate.
    Spindle-cell or undifferentiated.
    → TP63 may be low.
    → EZH2 typically high.
    → R likely < 0.3.
    → Deep attractor.
    → Slower response — commit to
      12-week minimum course.

AVIAN TISSUE CONSIDERATIONS:
  Avian oral SCC tissue is
  frequently necrotic, inflamed,
  and architecturally disrupted.
  Biopsy quality is critical.
  FNA cytology with IHC cell block
  is acceptable if biopsy under
  anaesthesia poses excessive risk
  in a severely compromised bird.
  Document all sample quality
  limitations clearly.
  Standard budgerigar weight: 30–45g.
  Body weight must be recorded.
  Weight < 25g is an anaesthetic
  risk flag. Prioritise stabilisation
  before biopsy under GA.
  Punch biopsy of surface-accessible
  masses may be performed under
  local anaesthesia with appropriate
  pain management — this is
  preferred over general anaesthesia
  in debilitated small birds.
```

## WHAT IS BEING MEASURED

```
PROTEIN 1 — TP63
(Tumour Protein 63)
  The Identity Anchor.
  The master transcription factor
  of squamous basal cell identity.
  Nuclear staining.
  Tumour cell nuclei only.
  All three isoforms (ΔNp63,
  TAp63 and others) are included
  in the H-score — do not restrict
  to a single isoform antibody
  if avian cross-reactivity
  allows broad detection.

  High H-score (> 100):
    TP63 programme partially active.
    Residual differentiation signal.
    Shallow false attractor.
    Well-differentiated or
    moderately differentiated
    morphology expected.
    Fast predicted response.

  Intermediate H-score (50–100):
    Moderate false attractor depth.
    Tazemetostat response expected
    at 6–8 weeks.

  Low H-score (< 50):
    TP63 programme heavily
    suppressed. Deep false attractor.
    Poorly differentiated morphology
    likely. Slower response — commit
    to 12-week course minimum.

  Zero staining:
    TP63 programme fully silenced.
    Very deep attractor.
    Assess DNMT3A — may indicate
    dual-hub reinforcement.

PROTEIN 2 — SOX2
(SRY-Box Transcription Factor 2)
  Squamous lineage-survival
  co-factor. Confirmatory marker.
  Nuclear staining.
  Tumour cell nuclei only.

  Expected: HIGH in most OSCC.
  SOX2 amplification is a
  characteristic feature of
  squamous lineage carcinoma.
  Its presence confirms squamous
  lineage identity of the tumour
  and the appropriateness of
  the EZH2-targeted approach.

  SOX2 high + TP63 low + EZH2 high:
    Classic deep false attractor.
    SOX2 is maintaining squamous
    lineage survival while TP63
    balance is overridden. Strongest
    indication for tazemetostat.

  SOX2 low or absent:
    Atypical for squamous cell
    lineage. Reconsider diagnosis.
    Document and contact
    OrganismCore@proton.me.
    Do not escalate without
    further assessment.

  NOTE: SOX2 is not a drug target
  in this protocol. It is a
  confirmatory marker only.
  The H-score is recorded but
  does not change the drug
  selection decision.

PROTEIN 3 — EZH2
(Enhancer of Zeste Homolog 2)
  The primary Convergence Hub.
  The PRC2 catalytic subunit.
  Nuclear staining.
  Tumour cell nuclei only.

  High H-score (> 150):
    Active EZH2 lock confirmed.
    H3K27me3 accumulation on
    differentiation gene promoters.
    TAZEMETOSTAT is the correct
    escalation drug.

  Intermediate H-score (80–150):
    EZH2 active but not dominant.
    Check DNMT3A. If also elevated:
    dual-hub case. If DNMT3A low:
    EZH2 is still primary hub —
    tazemetostat indicated.

  Low H-score (< 80):
    EZH2 not the dominant hub.
    Check DNMT3A. If high:
    azacitidine is the drug.
    If both low: atypical case —
    do not escalate, contact
    OrganismCore@proton.me.

PROTEIN 4 — DNMT3A
(DNA Methyltransferase 3A)
  The secondary Convergence Hub.
  Nuclear staining.
  Tumour cell nuclei only.

  High H-score (> 150) with
  EZH2 also high:
    Dual-hub case. Both epigenetic
    locks active. Start tazemetostat
    first, reassess at week 8,
    add azacitidine if response
    partial.

  High H-score with EZH2 low:
    DNMT3A is primary hub.
    Azacitidine is the drug.

  Low H-score:
    DNMT3A not a dominant hub.
    EZH2 pathway assessment
    is sufficient.

THE RATIO AND THE DECISION:
  R = TP63 H-score / EZH2 H-score

  R > 1.5, EZH2 high:
    Shallow EZH2 lock.
    Piroxicam + tazemetostat.
    Best expected response.
    Mass size reduction expected
    at 4–6 weeks.
    Cessation of oral bleeding
    possible earlier.

  R 0.5–1.5, EZH2 high:
    Intermediate depth.
    Piroxicam + tazemetostat.
    Moderate response expected.
    Monitor at weeks 4 and 8.

  R < 0.5, EZH2 high:
    Deep false attractor.
    Piroxicam + tazemetostat.
    Slower response. Commit to
    12-week minimum course before
    assessing efficacy.

  EZH2 low, DNMT3A high:
    DNMT3A-dominant case.
    Piroxicam + azacitidine.
    Atypical — document carefully.

  Both hubs low:
    Atypical case. Do not escalate.
    Contact OrganismCore@proton.me.

  SOX2 absent (any R):
    Reassess diagnosis.
    Do not escalate without
    pathological confirmation
    of squamous lineage.
```

## THE TEST — STEP BY STEP

```
STEP 1 — TISSUE ACQUISITION:
  Source: Incisional biopsy,
  punch biopsy, or FNA cell block
  from the oral/beak mass.

  Preferred: Incisional biopsy
  under local anaesthesia +
  sedation — avoids full GA
  in debilitated small birds.
  A 2–3mm punch biopsy of the
  accessible, viable-appearing
  margin of the mass (not the
  necrotic centre) is sufficient
  for FFPE IHC panel.

  Acceptable alternative: FNA
  cell block. Document clearly.

  Fixation: 10% neutral buffered
  formalin, standard protocol.
  Processing: FFPE block.
  Sections: 4-micron sections
  for each stain.

STEP 2 — IHC PANEL:
  Four stains run as one panel:
  Stain 1: Anti-TP63 (pan-p63)
  Stain 2: Anti-SOX2
  Stain 3: Anti-EZH2
  Stain 4: Anti-DNMT3A

  Antibody cross-reactivity note:
  Run positive mammalian control
  alongside avian section.
  Confirm staining pattern before
  scoring. Document any
  cross-reactivity failure.
  A failed cross-reactivity is
  a reportable finding — document
  it. It does not invalidate
  the framework.

STEP 3 — SCORING:
  H-score = (% cells 1+) × 1
           + (% cells 2+) × 2
           + (% cells 3+) × 3
  Range: 0–300.
  Score tumour cells only.
  Score a minimum of 100 tumour
  cells per stain from viable,
  non-necrotic areas.
  Score two representative
  high-power fields (40×).
  Report mean H-score from both.

STEP 4 — CALCULATE R:
  R = TP63 H-score / EZH2 H-score
  If EZH2 H-score = 0: R is
  undefined. Classify as EZH2-null.
  Assess DNMT3A as primary hub.

STEP 5 — CLASSIFY AND RECOMMEND:
  Apply decision tree above.
  Document all scores, R value,
  SOX2 confirmation, hub
  classification, depth
  classification, recommended
  escalation drug, and predicted
  response timeline.

STEP 6 — REPORT TO CLINICIAN:
  Complete and provide the IHC
  Reporting Template below.
```

## RESULT INTERPRETATION AND
## TREATMENT DECISION TREE

```
RESULT A — CLASSIC WELL-DIFFERENTIATED
OSCC:
  TP63: Intermediate to high
        (> 80)
  SOX2: High (> 150)
  EZH2: High (> 150)
  DNMT3A: Low to intermediate
  R: 0.5–1.5

  INTERPRETATION:
  Squamous identity partially
  active. EZH2 is the active lock.
  Classic intermediate-depth
  false attractor.

  TREATMENT:
  Piroxicam (or meloxicam) +
  Tazemetostat.
  First mass size reduction
  expected at 4–8 weeks.
  Best prognosis for this protocol.

RESULT B — POORLY DIFFERENTIATED/
DEEP ATTRACTOR:
  TP63: Low (< 50)
  SOX2: High (> 150)
  EZH2: High (> 150)
  DNMT3A: Low to intermediate
  R: < 0.3

  INTERPRETATION:
  TP63 programme heavily silenced.
  EZH2 dominant lock. Deep false
  attractor. Poorly differentiated
  morphology.

  TREATMENT:
  Piroxicam + Tazemetostat.
  Minimum 12-week commitment.
  Mass size change may not
  appear until week 8–10.
  Cessation of bleeding may
  be earlier signal.

RESULT C — DUAL HUB CASE:
  TP63: Low (< 50)
  SOX2: High (> 150)
  EZH2: High (> 150)
  DNMT3A: High (> 150)
  R: < 0.5

  INTERPRETATION:
  Both EZH2 and DNMT3A locks
  active. Complex deep attractor.

  TREATMENT:
  Piroxicam + Tazemetostat.
  Reassess at week 8.
  If partial response: add
  azacitidine as third agent.
  Contact OrganismCore@proton.me.

RESULT D — DNMT3A-DOMINANT:
  TP63: Low to zero
  SOX2: Present
  EZH2: Low (< 80)
  DNMT3A: High (> 150)
  R: < 0.3

  INTERPRETATION:
  Atypical. DNMT3A primary hub.

  TREATMENT:
  Piroxicam + Azacitidine.
  Document carefully.
  High framework value.

RESULT E — SOX2 ABSENT:
  SOX2 staining absent or low.

  ACTION:
  Reassess tumour diagnosis.
  Non-squamous lineage carcinoma
  or other tumour type in the
  differential. Do not escalate
  until diagnosis is confirmed.
  Contact OrganismCore@proton.me.

RESULT F — INDETERMINATE:
  All hubs low or antibody
  failure.

  ACTION:
  Do not escalate. Repeat biopsy
  if possible. Contact
  OrganismCore@proton.me.
```
