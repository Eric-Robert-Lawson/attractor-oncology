## THE TEST — STEP BY STEP

```
STEP 1 — TISSUE ACQUISITION:
  Punch/wedge biopsy of beak mass
    (preferred — FFPE).
  Oral cavity biopsy under
    brief anaesthesia.
  FNA cell block where biopsy
    not possible.

STEP 2 — CONFIRM MORPHOLOGY (H&E):
  Squamous nests/cords with:
    Nuclear pleomorphism and
    increased N:C ratio.
    Variable keratinisation —
    keratin pearls if present.
    Intercellular bridges (desmosomes)
    visible in well-differentiated
    areas.
    Local invasion — tumour
    nests pushing into
    subepithelial stroma or
    bone (note if bone invasion
    present).
  CRITICAL — LAYER DISTRIBUTION:
    In benign reactive hyperplasia:
    proliferating cells are largely
    confined to the expanded
    basal layer.
    In SCC: tumour cell nests
    extend into the stroma,
    disrupting the normal
    epithelial-stromal boundary.
    IHC for ΔNp63α will show
    suprabasal distribution in SCC.

STEP 3 — IHC PANEL:
  Stain 1: H&E (morphology)
  Stain 2: Anti-ΔNp63α (or
    anti-p63 — confirm isoform
    specificity where possible;
    standard p63 IHC is acceptable
    for bounty purposes)
    — Identity Anchor
    — Assess H-score AND layer
      distribution (basal-only
      vs suprabasal/diffuse)
  Stain 3: Anti-SOX2
    — Co-anchor
  Stain 4: Anti-EZH2
    — Primary Hub
  Stain 5: Anti-DNMT3A
    — Secondary Hub

STEP 4 — SCORING:
  Standard H-score for all stains.
  For ΔNp63α: report both H-score
  AND layer distribution separately.
  Minimum 100 tumour cells per stain.
  Two HPF (40×). Report mean.

STEP 5 — CALCULATE R:
  R = ΔNp63α H-score /
      EZH2 H-score

  R > 1.0: TF-dominant attractor.
    Fast response predicted.
  R 0.5–1.0: Co-dominant.
    Standard timeline.
  R < 0.5: Chromatin-dominant.
    Tazemetostat most directly
    effective at this end.

STEP 6 — ASSESS BONE INVASION:
  FROM H&E:
  ☐ No bone invasion — tumour
    confined to epithelium and
    soft tissue stroma.
  ☐ Bone invasion present —
    tumour eroding or infiltrating
    underlying bone.
  BONE INVASION IMPACT:
  Bone invasion does not preclude
  tazemetostat but affects
  prognosis and the extent of
  local treatment (surgery scope).
  Note: tazemetostat acts
  systemically — it will reach
  tumour cells in bone-invasive
  regions that intralesional
  carboplatin cannot reach.
  This is one of the most
  important potential benefits
  of the geometric addition:
  a systemic differentiation-
  forcing agent addresses the
  population that local treatment
  fundamentally cannot reach.

STEP 7 — REPORT AND RECOMMEND.
  Complete the IHC Reporting
  Template. Note bone invasion
  status. Recommend protocol
  based on depth classification.
```
