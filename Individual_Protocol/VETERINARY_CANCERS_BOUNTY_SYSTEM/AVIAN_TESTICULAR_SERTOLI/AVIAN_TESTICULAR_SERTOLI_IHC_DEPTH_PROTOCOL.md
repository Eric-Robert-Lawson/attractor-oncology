```
This test has four functions:

FUNCTION 1 — CONFIRM SERTOLI
CELL TUMOUR IDENTITY:
  Confirm the tumour is of
  Sertoli cell origin — i.e.,
  that DMRT1 is lost or reduced,
  confirming Identity Anchor
  failure. AND that FOXL2 shows
  partial or emerging expression,
  confirming the granulosa drift
  is active.

FUNCTION 2 — CONFIRM GRANULOSA
DRIFT VIA CYP19A1 (AROMATASE):
  Confirm CYP19A1 (aromatase)
  is expressed in tumour cells —
  confirming oestrogen synthesis
  is active and that the granulosa
  drift has progressed to functional
  hormone secretion. This directly
  validates the mechanism of cere
  colour change in this patient.

FUNCTION 3 — CONFIRM EZH2 HUB
ACTIVITY:
  Confirm EZH2 is elevated in
  tumour cells — confirming the
  epigenetic lock on the Sertoli
  identity programme is active
  and tazemetostat will address
  the hub.

FUNCTION 4 — DEPTH SCORE:
  Assess DMRT1/EZH2 ratio and
  FOXL2 drift depth to predict
  response timeline and whether
  DNMT3A co-hub extension will
  be needed.

THIS TEST IS UNIQUE AMONG BOUNTY
ENTRIES IN HAVING FIVE STAINS:
  DMRT1 — Identity Anchor (loss)
  FOXL2 — Drift indicator
  CYP19A1 — Functional drift
    readout (aromatase)
  EZH2   — Primary Hub
  DNMT3A — Secondary Hub

The CYP19A1 stain is unique to
this entry and directly validates
the mechanism of the cere colour
sign: aromatase expressed in the
tumour = oestrogen produced =
cere is brown. This is the IHC
confirmation of the brown cere
sign's molecular basis.

TISSUE ACQUISITION IN AVIAN
TESTICULAR NEOPLASIA:
  The testis is an intra-coelomic
  organ in birds. Biopsy options
  are limited in the living bird.

  Endoscopic biopsy:
    Avian endoscopic approach
    through the left lateral coelom.
    An experienced avian vet can
    visualise the testis and obtain
    a guided biopsy core. The
    preferred approach in a living
    bird where IHC is required.

  Ultrasound-guided FNA:
    Where endoscopy is not available:
    ultrasound-guided FNA of the
    coelomic mass where it is
    accessible. Cell block for IHC.
    Sensitivity for cytological
    confirmation of Sertoli cell
    tumour vs seminoma vs Leydig
    cell tumour is lower than
    histopathology.

  Post-mortem tissue:
    Where the bird dies or is
    euthanised: fresh testicular
    tissue immediate FFPE. The
    definitive sample. Post-mortem
    tissue is fully acceptable
    for the bounty record.

  SURGICAL ORCHIDECTOMY SPECIMEN:
    If orchidectomy is performed
    (the definitive treatment):
    the surgical specimen IS the
    IHC sample. This is the ideal
    scenario — the surgery that
    removes the tumour provides
    the tissue for definitive IHC
    characterisation. The IHC
    results from the orchidectomy
    specimen can be used to confirm
    the framework prediction
    post-operatively and to document
    the case for the bounty record.
    In this scenario, tazemetostat
    may not have been started —
    the orchidectomy specimen IHC
    is purely diagnostic and
    framework-validating.

HISTOLOGICAL TUMOUR TYPES AND
THEIR IHC PROFILES:

  SERTOLI CELL TUMOUR:
    H&E: tubular or sheet-like
    architecture, elongated or
    ovoid cells with abundant
    pale cytoplasm, low mitotic
    rate typically.
    IHC:
      DMRT1: LOW or ABSENT
        (Identity Anchor lost)
      FOXL2: LOW TO INTERMEDIATE
        (drift beginning)
      CYP19A1: ELEVATED
        (aromatase active →
        oestrogen produced →
        brown cere)
      EZH2: ELEVATED
        (hub active, locking
        Sertoli programme)
    This is the target profile
    for this protocol.

  SEMINOMA (GERM CELL TUMOUR):
    H&E: large round cells with
    vesicular nuclei, prominent
    nucleoli, diffuse pattern.
    IHC:
      DMRT1: may be retained
        (germ cell origin, not
        Sertoli)
      FOXL2: LOW
      CYP19A1: LOW
      EZH2: VARIABLE
    Note: Seminomas in birds are
    more common than Sertoli cell
    tumours in some survey data.
    Distinguishing them by IHC
    is important because the protocol
    in this document applies to
    SERTOLI CELL TUMOUR specifically.
    Seminoma has a different
    Identity Axis geometry (germ
    cell, not Sertoli cell).
    A budgerigar with a brown cere
    (oestrogen excess) is much more
    likely to have a Sertoli cell
    tumour than a pure seminoma.
    A budgerigar with testicular
    mass but NO cere colour change
    may have a seminoma or a Leydig
    cell tumour. The cere colour
    change is the pre-biopsy
    diagnostic indicator for Sertoli
    cell tumour.

  LEYDIG (INTERSTITIAL) CELL
  TUMOUR:
    H&E: polygonal cells with
    abundant eosinophilic cytoplasm.
    Testosterone-producing (not
    oestrogen). Brown cere change
    would NOT be expected.
    If cere is brown: Sertoli cell
    tumour is more likely.
    DMRT1: variable.
    FOXL2: LOW.
    CYP19A1: LOW.
    This distinction is clinically
    important — the cere colour
    change is the pre-IHC
    differential screen.
```

## WHAT IS BEING MEASURED

```
PROTEIN 1 — DMRT1
  The Identity Anchor.
  Nuclear staining in tumour cells.

  FRAMEWORK PREDICTION:
  DMRT1 will be LOW or ABSENT in
  avian Sertoli cell tumour cells —
  confirming Identity Anchor loss.
  Because DMRT1 is on the avian
  Z chromosome and is the primary
  avian sex determinant, its loss
  in a ZZ (male) bird's Sertoli
  cells is a specific failure of
  the avian sex determination
  maintenance programme.

  ABSENT/LOW (< 40):
    DMRT1 loss confirmed. Identity
    Anchor failure. The Sertoli
    identity programme is no longer
    maintained. The granulosa
    programme is no longer actively
    suppressed.
    Tazemetostat indicated: removes
    H3K27me3 from DMRT1-target
    Sertoli programme gene promoters.
    Residual DMRT1-independent
    Sertoli programme (SOX9-
    mediated) may be available
    for re-engagement.

  INTERMEDIATE (40–100):
    DMRT1 partially retained but
    insufficient to maintain
    Sertoli identity against
    EZH2 lock. Residual DMRT1
    will cooperate with tazemetostat.
    Most responsive case.

  HIGH (> 100):
    DMRT1 retained at substantial
    level. Unusual for Sertoli cell
    tumour. Reassess diagnosis.
    May be seminoma or normal
    Sertoli cells in the biopsy.
    Contact OrganismCore@proton.me.

PROTEIN 2 — FOXL2
  The drift indicator.
  Nuclear staining in tumour cells.

  FOXL2 is the master granulosa
  cell TF — the molecular opponent
  of DMRT1. In the normal testis,
  FOXL2 is completely suppressed
  by DMRT1. Its expression in
  Sertoli cell tumour cells
  indicates how far the granulosa
  drift has progressed.

  ABSENT/LOW (< 40):
    Granulosa drift is early —
    FOXL2 has not yet been
    substantially activated.
    The false attractor is still
    close to the Sertoli identity.
    Tazemetostat predicted to be
    most effective here: the
    epigenetic push back toward
    Sertoli identity encounters
    the least counterforce from
    active FOXL2 granulosa
    programme drive.
    Fastest predicted cere colour
    return.

  INTERMEDIATE (40–100):
    Granulosa drift in progress.
    FOXL2 partially active.
    Tazemetostat will face some
    competition between derepressing
    Sertoli programme and active
    FOXL2 driving granulosa
    programme. Standard timeline.

  HIGH (> 100):
    Deep granulosa drift. FOXL2
    strongly active. The granulosa
    programme is strongly driving
    the false attractor from the
    FOXL2 side. Tazemetostat must
    work against active FOXL2.
    Longer course. Consider earlier
    escalation to dual-hub approach.
    Cere colour return will be slower.
    Bone marrow suppression risk
    is highest at this depth.

PROTEIN 3 — CYP19A1 (AROMATASE)
  The functional drift readout.
  Cytoplasmic staining in tumour
  cells.

  THIS IS THE ONLY IHC STAIN
  IN THE BOUNTY SYSTEM THAT
  DIRECTLY EXPLAINS A CLINICAL
  SIGN IN THE LIVING BIRD:
  CYP19A1 high in the tumour cells
  = aromatase active = oestrogen
  being produced = testosterone
  suppressed = cere is brown.
  This stain validates the
  mechanism of the cere colour
  sign in the individual patient.

  HIGH:
    Aromatase strongly active.
    Oestrogen production confirmed
    at the tumour level.
    The brown cere sign is
    mechanistically explained.
    Framework prediction confirmed.
    Tazemetostat's mechanism of
    action is predicted to reduce
    CYP19A1 expression as DMRT1-
    dependent Sertoli programme
    re-engages and re-suppresses
    the granulosa programme
    including aromatase.

  LOW with brown cere still present:
    Important anomaly. If the
    cere is brown but CYP19A1
    is low in the tumour, the
    oestrogen source may be outside
    the primary tumour cells —
    possible necrotic/apoptotic
    tumour cell release, or a
    different tumour type (Leydig
    cell). Document carefully.
    Contact OrganismCore@proton.me.

PROTEIN 4 — EZH2
  The primary Hub.
  Nuclear staining.

  FRAMEWORK PREDICTION:
  EZH2 will be elevated in avian
  Sertoli cell tumour cells —
  because the liminal false attractor
  (between Sertoli and granulosa
  identity) requires EZH2 to lock
  the Sertoli programme in the
  H3K27me3-silenced state. Without
  this lock, even with DMRT1 absent,
  the Sertoli programme genes might
  be re-expressed by DMRT1-independent
  mechanisms. EZH2 prevents this
  re-expression.

  HIGH (> 150):
    EZH2 hub confirmed. Tazemetostat
    directly indicated. Framework
    EZH2 prediction confirmed for
    this case.

  INTERMEDIATE (80–150):
    EZH2 active. Tazemetostat
    indicated. Monitor response.

  LOW (< 80):
    EZH2 unexpectedly low.
    DNMT3A may be the dominant
    epigenetic enforcement
    mechanism.
    Contact OrganismCore@proton.me.

PROTEIN 5 — DNMT3A
  Secondary Hub.
  Nuclear staining.

  Same logic as other entries:
  High = DNA methylation co-hub.
  Tazemetostat first.
  Add azacitidine if plateau.
```

## THE TEST — STEP BY STEP

```
STEP 1 — TISSUE ACQUISITION:
  Endoscopic biopsy (preferred).
  FNA cell block.
  Orchidectomy specimen (if surgery
  performed — ideal specimen).
  Post-mortem testicular tissue.

STEP 2 — CONFIRM MORPHOLOGY (H&E):
  Identify Sertoli cell tumour
  morphology:
    Tubular or sheet architecture.
    Elongated/ovoid cells with
    pale cytoplasm.
    No discrete round cells with
    vesicular nuclei (would suggest
    seminoma).
    No polygonal eosinophilic
    cells (would suggest Leydig).
  Note: mixed testicular tumours
  occur. Document proportions
  of tumour types if mixed.
  The IHC panel applies to the
  Sertoli component specifically.

STEP 3 — IHC PANEL:
  Stain 1: H&E (morphology)
  Stain 2: Anti-DMRT1
    — Identity Anchor (confirm loss)
  Stain 3: Anti-FOXL2
    — Drift indicator (confirm
      granulosa programme activation)
  Stain 4: Anti-CYP19A1 (aromatase)
    — Functional drift readout
      (confirm oestrogen production
      source in tumour cells)
  Stain 5: Anti-EZH2
    — Primary Hub (confirm lock)
  Stain 6: Anti-DNMT3A
    — Secondary Hub

STEP 4 — SCORING:
  H-score for all stains.
  Minimum 100 tumour cells.
  For FOXL2 and CYP19A1: report
  whether positive cells are
  in the Sertoli-type component
  specifically (not contaminating
  normal vasculature or stroma).

STEP 5 — CALCULATE R:
  R = DMRT1 H-score /
      EZH2 H-score
  (Same formula as other entries.)

STEP 6 — ASSESS DRIFT DEPTH:
  Drift depth classification:
  EARLY drift:
    FOXL2 < 40, CYP19A1 intermediate
    → fast response predicted
  INTERMEDIATE drift:
    FOXL2 40–100, CYP19A1 high
    → standard timeline
  DEEP drift:
    FOXL2 > 100, CYP19A1 very high
    → extended course, consider
      dual-hub escalation

STEP 7 — CERE COLOUR ASSESSMENT:
  At the time of IHC biopsy:
  Cere colour grade (0–4):
  0 = fully blue
  1 = slightly purple-blue
  2 = purple-brown
  3 = brown with blue tinge
  4 = fully brown
  Record baseline cere colour
  grade alongside IHC results.
  The drift depth on IHC should
  correlate with cere colour
  grade. If FOXL2 and CYP19A1
  are high, cere should be deep
  brown. If they are low and cere
  is still brown, the dissociation
  is important to document.

STEP 8 — REPORT AND RECOMMEND.
  Predict cere colour return
  timeline based on drift depth
  and R ratio.
  Note whether orchidectomy is
  indicated and whether
  tazemetostat should be used
  as pre-surgical or adjunct
  (for non-surgical candidates).
```
