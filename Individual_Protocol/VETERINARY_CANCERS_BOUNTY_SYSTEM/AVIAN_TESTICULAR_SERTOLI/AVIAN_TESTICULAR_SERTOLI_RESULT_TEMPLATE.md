## INSTRUCTIONS FOR USE

```
Complete every section.
Do not skip fields.
A failure with complete records
is more valuable to the framework
than an undocumented success.

THE BEFORE-DOCUMENT (Section 1)
must be completed and dated BEFORE
the first dose of tazemetostat.

THE PRIMARY RESPONSE SIGNAL FOR
THIS PROTOCOL IS CERE COLOUR RETURN.

This is the most mechanistically
direct response signal in the entire
bounty system. The cere colour is
the in vivo hormone assay. It does
not measure tumour volume. It does
not measure nerve compression. It
measures the oestrogen/testosterone
balance in the living bird — which
is the direct molecular consequence
of the aromatase (CYP19A1) activity
in the tumour — which is the direct
consequence of the FOXL2/DMRT1
granulosa drift status — which is
exactly what tazemetostat targets.

A blue cere means:
  — Oestrogen is low.
  — Testosterone is restored.
  — Aromatase (CYP19A1) activity
    in the tumour is reduced.
  — The FOXL2 granulosa drive is
    suppressed.
  — DMRT1-target Sertoli programme
    has re-engaged.
  — Tazemetostat's mechanism is
    working.

A brown cere means:
  — Oestrogen is high.
  — Testosterone is suppressed.
  — Aromatase is active.
  — The granulosa drift is ongoing.
  — The mechanism is not yet
    engaging.

The owner takes a weekly photograph
of the cere. The colour trajectory
from brown to blue is the response
record.

THE BONE MARROW CLOCK:
Prolonged oestrogen from the Sertoli
cell tumour suppresses bone marrow
erythropoiesis. Anaemia develops.
Severe anaemia is fatal. The goal
of early treatment is to reduce
oestrogen before the bone marrow
is critically suppressed. The haem-
atocrit and reticulocyte count (where
available) are the bone marrow clock
markers.

TWO USE CASES FOR THIS TEMPLATE:

USE CASE 1 — NON-SURGICAL CANDIDATE:
  The bird is not a candidate for
  orchidectomy. Tazemetostat is
  the primary new treatment being
  added. This is the main use case
  for which this template was designed.

USE CASE 2 — ORCHIDECTOMY PERFORMED:
  Orchidectomy removes the primary
  tumour. Tazemetostat may be added
  adjuvantly if there is any residual
  disease or as a preventive measure
  against contralateral tumour
  development. In this use case,
  the IHC results are from the
  orchidectomy specimen.
  Note: in most cases where complete
  orchidectomy is achieved, the
  cere colour will return to blue
  independently of tazemetostat.
  This template applies primarily
  to Use Case 1.
  If documenting Use Case 2:
  note clearly in Section 1 that
  orchidectomy was performed and
  the template is being used for
  adjuvant or contralateral monitoring.

COMPLETE THE BEFORE-DOCUMENT
BEFORE STARTING TAZEMETOSTAT.
PHOTOGRAPH THE CERE.
THE CERE COLOUR IS THE EVIDENCE.
```
