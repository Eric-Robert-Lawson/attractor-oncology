## WHAT IS BEING MEASURED

```
PROTEIN 1 — HNF1B
(Hepatocyte Nuclear Factor 1 Beta)
  The Identity Anchor.
  Nuclear staining in renal
  tubular epithelial cells.
  Compare tumour cells to adjacent
  normal tubular epithelium in
  the same section.

  Normal tubular epithelium:
    Strong nuclear HNF1B staining.
    H-score expected > 150 in
    well-preserved normal tubules.

  Tumour cells:
  High H-score (> 100):
    HNF1B programme partially
    retained. Shallow false attractor.
    Some residual tubular identity.
    Well-differentiated tubular
    adenocarcinoma morphology likely.
    Fastest predicted response.

  Intermediate H-score (50–100):
    Partial loss of identity
    programme. Moderate depth.

  Low H-score (< 50):
    HNF1B programme largely lost.
    Deep false attractor.
    Poorly differentiated morphology.
    Slower response timeline.

  Zero staining:
    Complete identity loss.
    Very deep attractor.
    Assess SETD2 and DNMT3A
    carefully for full depth picture.

PROTEIN 2 — EZH2
(Enhancer of Zeste Homolog 2)
  The primary Hub.
  Nuclear staining.
  Tumour cells only.

  High H-score (> 150):
    Active EZH2 lock confirmed.
    H3K27me3 accumulation expected.
    TAZEMETOSTAT is the correct
    escalation drug.
    The hub is active and targetable.

  Intermediate H-score (80–150):
    EZH2 active. Check SETD2.
    If SETD2 is also low: synthetic
    lethality is active even at
    intermediate EZH2 levels.

  Low H-score (< 80):
    EZH2 not dominant. Check DNMT3A.
    If DNMT3A high: DNMT3A is the
    primary hub in this case.
    If both low: atypical — do not
    escalate, contact
    OrganismCore@proton.me.

PROTEIN 3 — SETD2
(SET Domain Containing 2)
  THE SYNTHETIC LETHALITY MARKER.
  The most critical protein in
  this panel. Nuclear staining.
  Compare tumour cells to adjacent
  normal tubular epithelium.

  Normal tubular epithelium:
    Strong nuclear SETD2 staining.
    H3K36me3 protection is active.
    This is the positive control
    for the synthetic lethality
    argument.

  Tumour cells — SETD2 LOW
  (H-score < 80, or visibly
  lower than adjacent normal):
    SETD2 loss confirmed in
    tumour cells.
    H3K36me3 protection absent.
    Synthetic lethality mechanism
    ACTIVE.
    Tazemetostat is predicted to
    be selectively lethal to these
    cells because they have no
    H3K36me3 to protect them from
    EZH2 inhibition consequences.
    This is the ideal case for
    the protocol.
    HIGHEST PREDICTED SELECTIVITY.

  Tumour cells — SETD2 HIGH
  (H-score comparable to normal):
    SETD2 retained. H3K36me3
    protection may be partially
    active. Synthetic lethality
    mechanism attenuated.
    Tazemetostat may still produce
    response via EZH2 suppression
    but the selectivity advantage
    is reduced.
    Document carefully. Proceed
    with tazemetostat but note
    that this is an atypical case
    for the synthetic lethality
    prediction. Report to
    OrganismCore@proton.me.

  SETD2 COMPARISON IS CRITICAL:
    Always score SETD2 in tumour
    cells relative to adjacent
    normal tubular epithelium
    in the same section.
    The absolute H-score matters
    less than the differential.
    A tumour H-score of 60 when
    adjacent normal is 180 is
    a SETD2-low case.
    A tumour H-score of 60 when
    adjacent normal is 70 is
    not a SETD2-low case.
    Document both scores explicitly.

PROTEIN 4 — DNMT3A
(DNA Methyltransferase 3A)
  The secondary Hub.
  Nuclear staining.
  Tumour cells only.

  High H-score (> 150) with
  EZH2 also high:
    Dual-hub case. Both EZH2 and
    DNMT3A locks active. Start
    tazemetostat first. Reassess
    at week 8. If partial plateau:
    add azacitidine.

  High H-score with EZH2 low:
    DNMT3A is the primary hub.
    Azacitidine is the geometric
    escalation drug. Atypical
    case — document carefully.

  Low H-score:
    DNMT3A not a dominant hub.
    EZH2/SETD2 assessment sufficient.

THE COMPOSITE ASSESSMENT:
  The four proteins together give:
  — Confirmation of Identity Anchor
    disruption (HNF1B low)
  — Confirmation of Hub activation
    (EZH2 high)
  — Confirmation of synthetic
    vulnerability (SETD2 low)
  — Assessment of secondary hub
    (DNMT3A)
  — R ratio for depth scoring
  — Predicted response timeline

  The ideal BUDGERIGAR_RCC case for
  this protocol:
    HNF1B: low (< 50)
    EZH2: high (> 150)
    SETD2: low in tumour relative
           to adjacent normal
    DNMT3A: low to intermediate
    → Synthetic lethality fully
      active. Tazemetostat
      selectively targets the
      tumour cell's unique
      vulnerability.
```

## THE TEST — STEP BY STEP

```
STEP 1 — TISSUE ACQUISITION:
  Preferred: FNA cell block from
  coelomic mass under ultrasound
  guidance. Only attempt if the
  mass is accessible from the
  ventral coelomic wall and the
  bird is stable enough for brief
  restraint.

  Alternative: Post-mortem tissue
  from kidney at necropsy. Fixed
  immediately in 10% NBF. FFPE
  processed.

  Minimum tissue requirement:
  Sufficient cells for four
  IHC stains plus H&E. An FNA
  cell block with > 200 tumour
  cells is sufficient.

  Fixation: 10% neutral buffered
  formalin, standard protocol.
  Processing: FFPE block.
  Sections: 4-micron sections
  for each stain.

  If tissue cannot be safely
  obtained: proceed on clinical
  diagnosis with documented
  reasoning. Note IHC not
  available in the case report.

STEP 2 — IHC PANEL:
  Four stains plus H&E:
  Stain 1: H&E (histomorphology
    confirmation — tubular
    adenocarcinoma pattern)
  Stain 2: Anti-HNF1B
  Stain 3: Anti-EZH2
  Stain 4: Anti-SETD2
  Stain 5: Anti-DNMT3A

  Antibody cross-reactivity note:
  Run positive mammalian control
  alongside avian section.
  For SETD2 especially: confirm
  strong nuclear staining in
  adjacent normal tubular cells
  before scoring the tumour.
  The adjacent normal tissue is
  the internal positive control
  for SETD2 — it is essential
  to the scoring.

STEP 3 — SCORING:
  H-score = (% cells 1+) × 1
           + (% cells 2+) × 2
           + (% cells 3+) × 3
  Range: 0–300.
  Score tumour cells only for
  HNF1B, EZH2, and DNMT3A.
  Score SETD2 in BOTH tumour cells
  AND adjacent normal tubular
  cells — and record both.
  Score a minimum of 100 tumour
  cells per stain from viable,
  non-necrotic areas.
  Score two representative
  high-power fields (40×).
  Report mean H-score from both.

STEP 4 — CALCULATE R AND SETD2
DIFFERENTIAL:
  R = HNF1B H-score (tumour) /
      EZH2 H-score (tumour)

  SETD2 differential =
    SETD2 H-score (normal) −
    SETD2 H-score (tumour)

  SETD2 differential > 50:
    SETD2 loss confirmed.
    Synthetic lethality active.

  SETD2 differential < 20:
    SETD2 retained in tumour.
    Atypical. Document.

STEP 5 — CLASSIFY AND RECOMMEND:
  Apply decision tree below.
  Document all scores, R, SETD2
  differential, hub classification,
  depth classification, recommended
  escalation drug, and predicted
  response timeline.

STEP 6 — REPORT TO CLINICIAN:
  Complete the IHC Reporting
  Template below.
```
