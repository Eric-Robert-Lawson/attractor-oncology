# BUDGERIGAR TESTICULAR TUMOR — IHC DEPTH SCORE PROTOCOL
## DMRT1 / EZH2 / DNMT3A Patient Selection and Hub Identification Test
## OrganismCore — Eric Robert Lawson
## 2026-03-08

## STATUS: ACTIVE — REQUIRED BEFORE TAZEMETOSTAT OR AZACITIDINE

```
This test has two functions:

FUNCTION 1 — PATIENT SELECTION:
  Confirms whether tazemetostat
  (EZH2-dominant cases) or
  azacitidine (DNMT3A-dominant
  cases) is the correct second
  drug for this specific patient.
  Without this test, you may
  treat a DNMT3A-negative patient
  with azacitidine — and the drug
  targets a lock that is not
  the primary maintenance mechanism
  in that patient.
  That is not a framework failure.
  That is a wrong-target treatment
  that the IHC test would have
  prevented.

FUNCTION 2 — DEPTH SCORE:
  Establishes the depth of the
  false attractor in this patient —
  predicting how fast and how
  completely the response will be.
  High R (DMRT1 > EZH2):
  residual identity signal,
  shallow false attractor,
  fast response expected.
  Low R (EZH2 >> DMRT1):
  deep false attractor,
  DMRT1 programme heavily silenced,
  slower response expected.

LEUPROLIDE CAN START BEFORE THIS TEST.
  Leuprolide is existing standard
  first-line care for avian gonadal
  tumors. It does not require
  the depth test. It can begin
  immediately on diagnosis.
  The depth test runs in parallel
  while leuprolide is being tolerated
  and the owner decides about
  escalation.
  The IHC result is ready before
  the first leuprolide re-check
  at week 4–6 in most labs.
  The vet sees the depth score
  at the same visit they assess
  the leuprolide response.
  Decision and data arrive together.

THREE PROTEINS ARE TESTED:
  DMRT1 — the Identity Anchor.
  EZH2 — the primary Convergence Hub.
  DNMT3A — the secondary Hub
           (if EZH2 insufficient
           to explain depth).

  Three stains. One block.
  One IHC panel.
  One result that determines
  which drug to add and when.

AVIAN TISSUE CONSIDERATIONS:
  Budgerigar testicular tissue is
  small. Biopsy material may be
  limited. If full biopsy is not
  possible, fine needle aspirate
  cytology with IHC run on a cell
  block preparation is acceptable
  as a lower-evidence alternative.
  Document which method was used.
  If aspirate only: note reduced
  confidence in H-score accuracy.
  Attempt full biopsy if the bird's
  condition permits anaesthesia.
  Consult avian anaesthesia specialist
  before general anaesthesia in
  any bird under 30g or severely
  compromised.
```

## WHAT IS BEING MEASURED

```
PROTEIN 1 — DMRT1
(Doublesex and Mab-3 Related
Transcription Factor 1)
  The Identity Anchor.
  The master transcription factor
  of Sertoli cell and male gonadal
  identity in all vertebrates.
  In birds: Z-linked, dose-dependent,
  primary male-fate determinant.
  Nuclear staining.
  Tumour cell nuclei only.

  High H-score (> 100):
    DMRT1 programme partially active.
    Residual male identity signal.
    Shallow false attractor.
    Strong predicted response to
    tazemetostat — EZH2 has not
    yet fully silenced the DMRT1
    programme.

  Low H-score (< 50):
    DMRT1 programme heavily suppressed.
    Deep false attractor.
    Male identity largely silenced.
    Tazemetostat still indicated
    but response will be slower.
    Longer treatment course needed.

  Zero or near-zero staining:
    DMRT1 programme fully silenced.
    Very deep false attractor.
    Assess DNMT3A — may indicate
    dual-hub case requiring both
    tazemetostat and azacitidine.

PROTEIN 2 — EZH2
(Enhancer of Zeste Homolog 2)
  The primary Convergence Hub.
  The PRC2 catalytic subunit.
  Deposits H3K27me3 — the
  repressive chromatin mark —
  maintaining the false attractor
  by silencing the DMRT1-governed
  male identity programme.
  Nuclear staining.
  Tumour cell nuclei only.

  High H-score (> 150):
    Active EZH2 lock confirmed.
    Heavy H3K27me3 accumulation
    expected on DMRT1 programme
    promoters.
    TAZEMETOSTAT is the correct
    escalation drug.

  Intermediate H-score (80–150):
    EZH2 active but not dominant.
    Check DNMT3A. If DNMT3A is
    also elevated: dual-hub case.
    If DNMT3A is low: EZH2 is
    still the primary hub.
    Tazemetostat indicated but
    dose and duration considerations
    apply. Discuss with oncologist.

  Low H-score (< 80):
    EZH2 is not the dominant hub.
    Check DNMT3A.
    If DNMT3A high: azacitidine
    is the correct drug.
    If both EZH2 and DNMT3A low:
    atypical case. Do not proceed
    with escalation without
    further assessment.
    Contact OrganismCore@proton.me
    for geometric case review.

PROTEIN 3 — DNMT3A
(DNA Methyltransferase 3A)
  The secondary Convergence Hub.
  The de novo methyltransferase
  that may contribute to silencing
  the DMRT1 programme via CpG
  methylation at gene promoters,
  in addition to or instead of
  the EZH2/H3K27me3 mechanism.
  Nuclear staining.
  Tumour cell nuclei only.

  High H-score (> 150) with
  EZH2 also high:
    Dual-hub case.
    Both epigenetic locks active.
    Consider sequential therapy:
    tazemetostat first, then
    azacitidine addition if response
    is partial after 8 weeks.
    Contact OrganismCore@proton.me
    for geometric case review.

  High H-score with EZH2 low:
    DNMT3A is the primary hub.
    Azacitidine, not tazemetostat,
    is the correct drug.
    (Uncommon in testicular tumors
    but documented in related
    gonadal stromal tumors.)

  Low H-score:
    DNMT3A is not a dominant hub.
    EZH2 pathway assessment
    is sufficient.
    Azacitidine is not indicated
    as first-line escalation.

THE RATIO AND THE DECISION:
  R = DMRT1 H-score / EZH2 H-score

  R > 1.5, EZH2 high:
    Shallow EZH2 lock.
    Leuprolide + tazemetostat.
    Best expected response.
    Fastest re-attractor toward
    normal Sertoli cell identity.
    Cere colour change response
    expected at 4–6 weeks.

  R 0.5–1.5, EZH2 high:
    Intermediate depth.
    Leuprolide + tazemetostat.
    Moderate response expected.
    Monitor for DNMT3A hub
    switching from month 2–3
    if initial response is partial.

  R < 0.5, EZH2 high:
    Deep false attractor.
    Leuprolide + tazemetostat.
    Slower response expected.
    Cere change signal may not
    appear until week 8–10.
    Commit to 12-week minimum
    treatment course before
    assessing efficacy.

  EZH2 low, DNMT3A high:
    DNMT3A-dominant case.
    Leuprolide + azacitidine.
    Note: atypical for Sertoli
    cell tumors. Document
    this finding carefully.

  Both hubs low:
    Atypical case. Do not proceed
    with escalation. Seek re-assessment.
    Contact OrganismCore@proton.me.
```

## THE TEST — STEP BY STEP

```
STEP 1 — TISSUE ACQUISITION:
  Source: Tumour biopsy or FNA
  cell block from testicular mass.
  Preferred: Excisional or
  endoscopic core biopsy.
  Acceptable alternative: FNA
  cell block — document clearly.
  Fixation: 10% neutral buffered
  formalin, standard protocol.
  Processing: FFPE (formalin-fixed
  paraffin-embedded) block.
  Sections: 4-micron sections
  for each IHC stain.

STEP 2 — IHC PANEL:
  Three stains run as one panel:
  Stain 1: Anti-DMRT1
  Stain 2: Anti-EZH2
  Stain 3: Anti-DNMT3A

  Antibody validation note:
  Most commercially available
  DMRT1, EZH2, and DNMT3A antibodies
  were validated in human and
  mouse tissue. Cross-reactivity
  in avian tissue requires
  confirmation. Request the
  pathology lab to run a
  positive control (mammalian
  tissue) alongside the avian
  section and confirm staining
  pattern is consistent with
  known biology before scoring.

  If antibody does not cross-react:
  Document this clearly.
  This is a known limitation of
  avian IHC and a finding worth
  reporting. It does not invalidate
  the framework — it identifies
  a technical barrier for future
  antibody development.

STEP 3 — SCORING:
  Score each stain using H-score:
  H-score = (% cells 1+) × 1
           + (% cells 2+) × 2
           + (% cells 3+) × 3
  Range: 0–300.

  Score tumour cells only.
  Do not score stromal or
  inflammatory infiltrate.
  Score a minimum of 100
  tumour cells per stain.
  Score two representative
  high-power fields (40×).
  Report mean H-score from
  both fields.

STEP 4 — CALCULATE R:
  R = DMRT1 H-score / EZH2 H-score

  If EZH2 H-score = 0:
  R is undefined.
  Classify as EZH2-null case.
  Assess DNMT3A as primary hub.

STEP 5 — CLASSIFY AND RECOMMEND:
  Apply the decision tree above.
  Document:
  — DMRT1 H-score
  — EZH2 H-score
  — DNMT3A H-score
  — R value
  — Hub classification
  — Depth classification
  — Recommended escalation drug
  — Predicted response timeline

STEP 6 — REPORT TO CLINICIAN:
  Provide the IHC Reporting Template
  (below) to the supervising vet.
  The vet uses this to complete
  Section 1 (Before-Document) of
  the Case Report Template before
  starting the escalation drug.
```

## RESULT INTERPRETATION AND
## TREATMENT DECISION TREE

```
RESULT A — CLASSIC SERTOLI CASE:
  DMRT1: Low (< 50)
  EZH2: High (> 150)
  DNMT3A: Low to intermediate
  R: < 0.5

  INTERPRETATION:
  DMRT1 programme deeply silenced.
  EZH2 is the active epigenetic
  lock. Classic deep false attractor.

  TREATMENT:
  Leuprolide (continue or start) +
  Tazemetostat (escalation drug).
  Minimum 12-week commitment.
  Cere change signal expected
  at 8–12 weeks.

RESULT B — SHALLOW ATTRACTOR:
  DMRT1: Intermediate (50–100)
  EZH2: High (> 150)
  DNMT3A: Low
  R: 0.5–1.0

  INTERPRETATION:
  DMRT1 partially active.
  EZH2 lock is present but
  identity signal not yet lost.
  Moderate depth false attractor.

  TREATMENT:
  Leuprolide + Tazemetostat.
  Cere change signal expected
  at 4–8 weeks.
  Best prognosis category for
  this protocol.

RESULT C — DUAL HUB CASE:
  DMRT1: Low (< 50)
  EZH2: High (> 150)
  DNMT3A: High (> 150)
  R: < 0.5

  INTERPRETATION:
  Both epigenetic locks active.
  Deep and complex false attractor.

  TREATMENT:
  Start with Leuprolide +
  Tazemetostat.
  Reassess at week 8.
  If partial response: add
  azacitidine as third agent.
  Contact OrganismCore@proton.me
  for geometric case consultation.

RESULT D — DNMT3A-DOMINANT:
  DMRT1: Low to zero
  EZH2: Low (< 80)
  DNMT3A: High (> 150)
  R: undefined or < 0.3

  INTERPRETATION:
  Atypical. DNMT3A is primary hub.

  TREATMENT:
  Leuprolide + Azacitidine.
  Document carefully.
  This is an uncommon presentation —
  reporting this case is high value
  for the framework.

RESULT E — INDETERMINATE:
  All three hubs low.
  DMRT1 not assessable (antibody
  cross-reactivity failure).

  ACTION:
  Do not start escalation drug.
  Consider repeat biopsy.
  Contact OrganismCore@proton.me.
  Document technical findings.
```
