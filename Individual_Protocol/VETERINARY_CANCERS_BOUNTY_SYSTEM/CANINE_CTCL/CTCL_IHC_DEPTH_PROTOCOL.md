```
This test is mandatory before
vorinostat is started in any

It determines:
  Whether this patient has the
  HDAC-dominant lock type that
  vorinostat is designed to address.

  The depth score — how far
  the false attractor is consolidated
  — which predicts response duration.

  Whether monotherapy (vorinostat)

  Treatment may be given to

  Response prediction is impossible.
  The case cannot contribute to
  the scientific record.
  The bounty cannot be claimed.
```

## WHAT IS BEING MEASURED

```
TWO PROTEINS:

  PROTEIN 1 — BCL11B
  (B-Cell CLL/Lymphoma 11B)
    The Identity Anchor.
    The master T-cell identity
    transcription factor.
    Nuclear staining.
    Cancer cell nuclei only.
    High = identity programme
    still partially intact.
    Low = identity programme
    deeply suppressed.

  PROTEIN 2 — HDAC2
  (Histone Deacetylase 2)
    The Convergence Hub.
    The epigenetic lock maintaining
    the false attractor.
    Nuclear staining.
    Cancer cell nuclei only.
    High = strong HDAC lock.
    Low = HDAC is not the dominant
    maintenance mechanism —
    consider EZH2 as primary hub.

THE RATIO:
  R = BCL11B H-score /
      HDAC2 H-score

  R > 1 (BCL11B > HDAC2):
    Shallow attractor.
    HDAC-dominant lock not yet
    fully consolidated.
    Vorinostat monotherapy
    is the primary protocol.
    Best expected response.

  R 0.5–1.0:
    Intermediate depth.
    HDAC lock established.
    Vorinostat monotherapy first.
    Monitor closely for early
    resistance signal.
    Low threshold to add
    tazemetostat.

  R < 0.5 (HDAC2 >> BCL11B):
    Deep attractor.
    HDAC lock highly consolidated.
    AND/OR EZH2 may already be
    the dominant hub.
    Consider vorinostat +
    tazemetostat combination
    from the start.
    Discuss with vet before
    committing to monotherapy.
```

## THE TEST — STEP BY STEP

```
TISSUE REQUIRED:
  Existing skin biopsy FFPE block.
  The block already obtained at
  diagnosis for pathology assessment.
  No new biopsy required in most cases.
  Minimum tissue: 2mm core sufficient
  for two IHC stains.

ANTIBODIES:

  BCL11B:
    Clone: 25B6 or equivalent
    validated rabbit monoclonal.
    Abcam ab18045 or equivalent.
    Species reactivity: confirmed
    cross-reactive with canine tissue.
    Dilution: 1:200–1:500
    (laboratory-optimised).
    Antigen retrieval: citrate
    buffer pH 6.0, 20 minutes,
    pressure cooker or HIER.
    Detection: standard HRP-DAB
    or equivalent.
    Expected staining: nuclear,
    lymphocytes.

  HDAC2:
    Clone: Y461 or equivalent
    validated rabbit monoclonal.
    Abcam ab32117 or equivalent.
    Species reactivity: confirmed
    cross-reactive with canine tissue.
    Dilution: 1:200–1:400
    (laboratory-optimised).
    Antigen retrieval: citrate
    buffer pH 6.0, 20 minutes.
    Detection: standard HRP-DAB
    or equivalent.
    Expected staining: nuclear,
    cancer cells and stromal cells.

  CRITICAL WARNING — HDAC2:
    HDAC2 stains stromal cells,
    endothelial cells, and
    inflammatory cells as well as
    cancer cells.
    Score CANCER CELL NUCLEI ONLY.
    Ignore all stromal staining.
    This requires a pathologist
    familiar with CETCL morphology
    to identify the neoplastic
    T-cell population confidently.
    Incorrect inclusion of stromal
    cells in the HDAC2 score will
    give a falsely high H-score
    and push the ratio toward
    deeper classification.

SCORING — H-SCORE METHOD:
  Standard H-score for each protein.
  Score cancer cell nuclei only.

  H-score formula:
    (% weak positive nuclei × 1) +
    (% moderate positive nuclei × 2) +
    (% strong positive nuclei × 3)

  Range: 0–300 for each protein.

  Assess minimum 200 cancer cell
  nuclei per stain.
  Score at 20× objective.
  Confirm at 40× for intensity
  classification.

  Record:
    BCL11B H-score (0–300).
    HDAC2 H-score (0–300).
    R = BCL11B / HDAC2.
    Depth classification
    (shallow / intermediate / deep).
    Pathologist name and date.

CONTROLS:
  Positive control:
    Known canine T-cell lymphoma
    tissue with confirmed BCL11B
    and HDAC2 expression.
    Or human tonsil tissue
    (BCL11B positive in T cells,
    HDAC2 positive in multiple
    cell types).
  Negative control:
    Primary antibody omitted.
    No staining expected.
  Inter-observer reliability:
    Ideally scored by two
    pathologists blinded to
    clinical outcome.
    ICC > 0.7 acceptable.
    Record both scores.
    Use mean for clinical decision.

TURNAROUND:
  Standard referral lab: 3–7 days.
  Academic veterinary pathology
  department: 5–10 days.

ESTIMATED COST:
  $100–250 for both stains
  at most reference labs.
  This is a one-time cost.
  It is the patient selection step.
  It is non-negotiable.
```

## RESULT INTERPRETATION AND
## TREATMENT DECISION

```
RESULT 1 — SHALLOW (R > 1):

  BCL11B H-score > HDAC2 H-score.
  Identity programme still partially
  accessible.
  HDAC lock not fully consolidated.

  TREATMENT:
    Vorinostat monotherapy.
    Standard dose per vet assessment.
    Weekly CBC monitoring weeks 1–4.
    Month 2 response gate.

  EXPECTED OUTCOME:
    Best response probability.
    First signal (reduced pruritus)
    weeks 3–6.
    Primary response gate week 8.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

RESULT 2 — INTERMEDIATE (R 0.5–1.0):

  BCL11B and HDAC2 approximately
  co-expressed.
  HDAC lock established but
  not dominant.

  TREATMENT:
    Vorinostat monotherapy first.
    Low threshold to add tazemetostat
    if resistance signal appears
    before month 4.
    Consider EZH2 add-on from month 3
    if response is partial but
    not consolidating.

  EXPECTED OUTCOME:
    Moderate response probability.
    Response duration may be shorter
    than shallow attractor cases.
    Monitor for resistance signal
    actively from month 3.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

RESULT 3 — DEEP (R < 0.5):

  HDAC2 H-score significantly
  exceeds BCL11B H-score.
  Deep false attractor.
  HDAC lock highly consolidated.
  EZH2 arm may already be active
  as co-maintainer.

  OPTION A — COMBINATION FROM START:
    Vorinostat + tazemetostat
    simultaneously.
    Addresses HDAC and EZH2 arms
    together.
    Higher drug cost.
    Discuss with vet and owner
    before committing.

  OPTION B — SEQUENTIAL:
    Vorinostat first, add tazemetostat
    at week 4–6 regardless of
    resistance signal.
    Lower initial cost.
    Acceptable if EZH2 IHC can
    be added to the baseline panel.

  ADDITIONAL IHC RECOMMENDED:
    Add EZH2 (CST D2H1 #5246) to
    the baseline panel for deep
    attractor cases.
    EZH2 H-score confirms whether
    the PRC2 arm is already active.
    If EZH2 H-score > 150:
    combination from start.

  EXPECTED OUTCOME:
    Lower response probability
    to monotherapy.
    Combination is the correct
    protocol for deep cases.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

RESULT 4 — HDAC2 NEGATIVE (H-score < 30):

  Unexpected result.
  HDAC2 is not the primary lock.

  IMPLICATION:
    The HDAC-mediated false attractor
    maintenance predicted by the
    geometry is not confirmed in
    this patient.
    Vorinostat is unlikely to
    address the actual convergence
    hub in this case.

  ACTION:
    Add EZH2 stain to the panel.
    If EZH2 high: tazemetostat
    is the indicated drug, not
    vorinostat.
    If both HDAC2 and EZH2 low:
    the convergence hub is not
    the PRC arm. The case requires
    further analysis before
    treatment proceeds.
    Contact OrganismCore@proton.me
    for geometric re-assessment.
    Do not proceed with vorinostat
    for an HDAC2-negative patient.
    This would be treating the
    wrong target.
```
