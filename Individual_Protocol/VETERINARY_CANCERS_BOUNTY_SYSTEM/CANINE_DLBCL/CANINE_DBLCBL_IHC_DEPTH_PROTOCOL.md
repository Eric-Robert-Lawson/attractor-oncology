## STATUS: ACTIVE — REQUIRED BEFORE TAZEMETOSTAT

```
THIS TEST HAS THREE FUNCTIONS:

FUNCTION 1 — SAFETY GATE (MANDATORY):
  Screen for the H3K27M-mutant
  (H3C8 mutation) subset of canine
  DLBCL that has LOW H3K27me3
  (paralysed EZH2 geometry — the
  DIPG-equivalent attractor type).
  Tazemetostat is CONTRAINDICATED
  in this subset.
  If H3K27me3 is LOW, do not treat
  with tazemetostat regardless of
  EZH2 protein level.
  This function is non-negotiable.
  The safety gate must run first.

FUNCTION 2 — HUB CONFIRMATION:
  Confirm that EZH2 is the active
  Convergence Hub in this patient.
  EZH2 protein high AND H3K27me3
  high = EZH2 is active hub.
  EZH2 protein high AND H3K27me3 LOW
  = EZH2 is paralysed. Possible
  H3K27M mutation. Tazemetostat
  contraindicated.
  EZH2 protein LOW AND H3K27me3 LOW
  = EZH2 is not the hub. Different
  mechanism. Contact OrganismCore.

FUNCTION 3 — DEPTH SCORE:
  R = PAX5 H-score / EZH2 H-score.
  Predicts relapse risk after CHOP
  and identifies which dogs need
  maintenance tazemetostat.
  Low R = deep EZH2 lock = high
  relapse risk = maintenance
  tazemetostat indicated.
  High R = shallow EZH2 lock = lower
  relapse risk = CHOP alone may
  be sufficient / tazemetostat as
  option if relapse occurs.

CHOP CAN (AND SHOULD) START
BEFORE THIS TEST IS COMPLETE:
  CHOP is the standard first-line
  treatment. In aggressive cDLBCL,
  waiting for IHC results before
  starting CHOP is inappropriate.
  Start CHOP immediately on diagnosis.
  The IHC panel runs on the
  diagnostic biopsy in parallel.
  The depth score result is typically
  available within 5–10 days.
  The tazemetostat decision (at
  remission maintenance or at
  first relapse) is made at that
  point, not before CHOP starts.

THREE PROTEINS ARE TESTED:
  PAX5 — the Identity Anchor.
  EZH2 — the Convergence Hub protein.
  H3K27me3 — the hub activity readout.
  (H3K27me3 is the direct product
  of EZH2 enzymatic activity.
  It tells you whether EZH2 is
  active, not just present.)
```

## WHAT IS BEING MEASURED

```
PROTEIN 1 — PAX5
(Paired Box Protein 5)
  The Identity Anchor.
  The master B-cell identity factor.
  Nuclear staining.
  Neoplastic lymphocyte nuclei only.

  High H-score (> 150):
    PAX5 programme moderately active.
    Shallower false attractor.
    GC lock is not complete.
    CHOP ± tazemetostat maintenance.

  Low H-score (50–150):
    PAX5 programme suppressed.
    EZH2-mediated lock more complete.
    Monitor EZH2 and H3K27me3 levels.

  Very low H-score (< 50):
    Caution — either very deep lock
    or possible lineage shift.
    Confirm B-cell lineage with CD79a
    or CD20 if PAX5 is unexpectedly
    low. Very deep attractor — high
    relapse risk if EZH2 high and
    H3K27me3 high.

PROTEIN 2 — EZH2
(Enhancer of Zeste Homolog 2)
  The Convergence Hub protein.
  Nuclear staining.
  Neoplastic lymphocyte nuclei only.

  High H-score (> 150):
    EZH2 protein overexpressed.
    Potential active hub.
    Confirm with H3K27me3.

  Moderate H-score (80–150):
    EZH2 present but not dominant.
    Check H3K27me3.

  Low H-score (< 80):
    EZH2 not overexpressed.
    May not be the primary hub.
    Check H3K27me3 to confirm.

PROTEIN 3 — H3K27me3
(Histone H3 lysine 27 trimethyl)
  THE SAFETY GATE AND HUB ACTIVITY
  READOUT. Most important of the
  three proteins for the treatment
  decision.

  H3K27me3 is the direct enzymatic
  product of EZH2 activity.
  It tells you what EZH2 is DOING,
  not just whether it is present.

  EZH2 ACTIVE (tazemetostat indicated):
    H3K27me3 HIGH on neoplastic cells.
    Broad, diffuse nuclear staining.
    EZH2 is depositing the lock.
    Tazemetostat will dissolve it.

  EZH2 PARALYSED (DIPG-type geometry):
    H3K27me3 LOW or absent on
    neoplastic cells.
    Despite EZH2 protein being present.
    This is the H3K27M (H3C8 mutation)
    signature: the histone mutation
    competitively inhibits EZH2 and
    prevents H3K27me3 deposition.
    Global H3K27me3 loss = flat
    epigenetic landscape.
    Tazemetostat is CONTRAINDICATED.
    Inhibiting an already-paralysed
    enzyme removes whatever residual
    gradient exists.

  HETEROGENEOUS STAINING:
    Some cells H3K27me3 high,
    others low.
    This indicates subclonal
    heterogeneity — a fraction of
    the tumour has EZH2 active,
    a fraction has it paralysed.
    Record % of cells in each
    category.
    Do not treat unless the
    H3K27me3-high fraction is
    the dominant population
    (> 50% of neoplastic cells).

THE SAFETY GATE DECISION:

  H3K27me3 HIGH (> 100 H-score):
    ☐ PROCEED with tazemetostat
      consideration.
    EZH2 is active. Hub confirmed.
    Go to depth score interpretation.

  H3K27me3 LOW (< 60 H-score):
    ☐ DO NOT USE TAZEMETOSTAT.
    EZH2 is not the active hub
    or is paralysed.
    Possible H3C8 (H3K27M) mutation.
    Send for H3K27M mutation testing
    if available.
    Continue CHOP ± rescue protocols.
    Contact OrganismCore@proton.me.
    Document as atypical case.
    This finding is scientifically
    important and should be
    published regardless of outcome.

  H3K27me3 INTERMEDIATE (60–100):
    Borderline. Proceed with caution.
    Consider H3K27M mutation testing
    before tazemetostat.
    Discuss with oncologist.
    Document fully.
```

## DEPTH SCORE CALCULATION AND
## INTERPRETATION

```
CALCULATION:
  R = PAX5 H-score / EZH2 H-score.
  (Only calculated if H3K27me3 > 100
  — safety gate passed.)

INTERPRETATION:

  R < 0.5 — DEEP LOCK:
    EZH2 dominates PAX5 heavily.
    Very high relapse risk after CHOP.
    Tazemetostat maintenance
    STRONGLY indicated after
    CHOP-induced remission.
    Start tazemetostat at remission
    confirmation (week 12 restaging).
    Continue through first year.
    Monitor H3K27me3 at each
    restaging — if it re-rises,
    the lock is reforming.

  R 0.5–1.0 — INTERMEDIATE LOCK:
    EZH2 moderately dominant.
    Moderate relapse risk.
    Tazemetostat maintenance
    RECOMMENDED after CHOP remission.
    Particularly if complete remission
    is not achieved by week 8.
    Consider adding tazemetostat
    at any point of reduced response.

  R 1.0–2.0 — SHALLOW LOCK:
    PAX5 and EZH2 roughly matched
    or PAX5 slightly dominant.
    Lower relapse risk.
    Tazemetostat indicated at
    FIRST SIGN OF RELAPSE rather
    than as immediate maintenance.
    Watch-and-treat approach.
    Re-biopsy at relapse — depth
    score will likely be lower
    (EZH2 dominant at relapse).

  R > 2.0 — VERY SHALLOW:
    PAX5 substantially dominant.
    Lowest relapse risk group.
    CHOP alone may achieve
    durable remission.
    Tazemetostat reserved for
    relapse or non-complete-response.
    Most likely to match EZH2
    wild-type human DLBCL data
    where tazemetostat is least
    effective as single agent.
```

## THE TEST — STEP BY STEP

```
TISSUE REQUIRED:
  Fine needle aspirate (FNA) from
  enlarged lymph node — not
  sufficient for H-score. Cytology
  from FNA is used for diagnosis
  but IHC requires tissue sections.

  REQUIRED: excisional or
  core needle biopsy of an enlarged
  lymph node, submitted in formalin,
  processed to FFPE block.
  Minimum core: 2mm × 10mm.
  Preferred: excisional biopsy
  of a prescapular or submandibular
  node for maximum tissue.
  The same block used for
  diagnostic histopathology provides
  sufficient tissue for three IHC stains.

ANTIBODIES:

  PAX5:
    Clone: 24E6 (Cell Signaling
    Technology #46967) or
    Clone: SP34 (Abcam ab115872).
    Both validated in canine
    lymphoid tissue (confirmed
    in published veterinary
    pathology literature).
    Species: Rabbit monoclonal.
    Dilution: 1:400–1:800.
    Antigen retrieval: HIER,
    citrate buffer pH 6.0,
    20 minutes.
    Expected staining: nuclear,
    strong in neoplastic B-cells.
    Stromal cells and T-cells
    should be negative — use as
    internal negative control.

  EZH2:
    Clone: D2H1 (Cell Signaling
    Technology #5246).
    Rabbit monoclonal.
    Same antibody validated in
    canine tissue in the BRCA
    IHC protocol and angiosarcoma
    derivation.
    Dilution: 1:400–1:800.
    Antigen retrieval: HEIR,
    citrate buffer pH 6.0,
    20 minutes.
    Expected staining: nuclear,
    higher in neoplastic cells
    than reactive lymphoid tissue.

  H3K27me3:
    Clone: C36B11 (Cell Signaling
    Technology #9733).
    Rabbit monoclonal.
    Validated in multiple species
    including canine tissue.
    Cross-species: H3K27me3 is a
    histone modification — the
    antibody targets a PTM that
    is fully conserved.
    Dilution: 1:200–1:400.
    Antigen retrieval: HIER,
    citrate buffer pH 6.0,
    20 minutes.
    Expected staining: nuclear,
    broad diffuse in EZH2-active
    tumours. Low/absent in
    H3K27M-mutant cases.

    INTERNAL POSITIVE CONTROL
    FOR H3K27me3:
    Normal small lymphocytes and
    stromal cells in the same section
    serve as internal positive control
    — they should show moderate
    H3K27me3 positivity.
    If these cells are also H3K27me3-
    negative, this is a technical
    failure, not a biological signal.
    Repeat the stain.

    THIS INTERNAL CONTROL IS CRITICAL.
    Global H3K27me3 loss (the H3K27M
    signature) affects ALL cells in
    the tumour, but NOT the stromal
    background. In H3K27M cases:
    tumour cells negative, stromal
    cells moderately positive.
    In technical failure: all cells
    negative.
    The distinction matters for
    the safety gate decision.

SCORING — H-SCORE METHOD:
  Standard H-score for PAX5 and EZH2.
  Score neoplastic lymphocyte nuclei only.
  Minimum 200 cells per stain.

  H-score formula:
    (% weak × 1) + (% moderate × 2) +
    (% strong × 3).
  Range: 0–300.

  H3K27me3 scoring:
    Score as percentage of neoplastic
    nuclei showing any positive staining
    (all intensities combined) ×
    mean intensity score.
    Record as H-score 0–300 for
    consistency with PAX5 and EZH2.
    Also record separately:
    "% of neoplastic cells H3K27me3-
    positive" as a simple percentage —
    this is the safety gate metric.
    If < 25% of neoplastic cells
    positive: DO NOT TREAT.
    If > 75% positive with moderate
    to strong intensity: PROCEED.
    25–75%: intermediate. Discuss.

CONTROLS:
  Positive control (PAX5):
    Reactive lymph node or tonsil
    from the same species (canine)
    preferred. Human tonsil is
    acceptable given confirmed
    cross-reactivity.
  Positive control (EZH2):
    Known EZH2-high canine tumour
    or human germinal centre tissue.
  Positive control (H3K27me3):
    Normal canine tissue with
    confirmed H3K27me3 positivity.
    Liver or muscle (both show
    H3K27me3 in differentiated cells).
  Negative control:
    Primary antibody omitted.

  Inter-observer reliability:
    Two pathologists score
    independently (blinded).
    ICC > 0.7 for each stain.
    Mean of two scores used for
    the safety gate and depth score
    calculation.

TURNAROUND:
  3–7 business days at most
  reference labs.
  Academic veterinary pathology:
  5–10 days.

ESTIMATED COST:
  Three stains: $150–$300.
  Plus pathologist interpretation:
  varies by lab.
  One-time cost per patient.
  The depth score determines the
  maintenance strategy for the
  entire post-CHOP period.
```

## RESULT INTERPRETATION AND
## TREATMENT DECISION TREE

```
ALL RESULTS IN HAND. PROCEED:

STEP 1 — SAFETY GATE:
  What is the H3K27me3 status?

  ☐ H3K27me3 HIGH (> 100 H-score,
    > 75% cells positive, stromal
    internal control positive):
    → EZH2 is ACTIVE.
    → PASS SAFETY GATE.
    → Go to STEP 2.

  ☐ H3K27me3 LOW (< 60 H-score,
    < 25% cells positive, stromal
    internal control positive):
    → EZH2 is PARALYSED or absent.
    → FAIL SAFETY GATE.
    → DO NOT USE TAZEMETOSTAT.
    → Continue CHOP.
    → Request H3K27M mutation test.
    → Document and report to
      OrganismCore@proton.me.
    → Stop here.

  ☐ H3K27me3 INTERMEDIATE (60–100):
    → Borderline.
    → Consider H3K27M mutation
      testing before proceeding.
    → Do not start tazemetostat
      until borderline is resolved.
    → Continue CHOP.
    → Discuss with veterinary
      oncologist and OrganismCore.

STEP 2 — HUB CONFIRMATION
(safety gate passed):
  Is EZH2 H-score > 120?

  ☐ YES → EZH2 protein overexpressed.
           Hub confirmed.
           Go to STEP 3.

  ☐ NO  → EZH2 not overexpressed
           despite high H3K27me3.
           Unusual case.
           Possible other PRC2
           subunit driving activity.
           Contact OrganismCore@proton.me.
           Do not start tazemetostat.

STEP 3 — DEPTH SCORE
(hub confirmed):
  Calculate R = PAX5 / EZH2.

  R < 0.5 — DEEP:
    Treatment: CHOP (start immediately,
    do not wait for IHC).
    Maintenance tazemetostat at
    confirmed remission.
    Indication: STRONGLY recommended.

  R 0.5–1.0 — INTERMEDIATE:
    Treatment: CHOP (immediate).
    Maintenance tazemetostat at
    confirmed remission.
    Indication: RECOMMENDED.

  R 1.0–2.0 — SHALLOW:
    Treatment: CHOP (immediate).
    Tazemetostat at first relapse
    or if complete remission not
    achieved by week 12.
    Watch-and-treat approach.

  R > 2.0 — VERY SHALLOW:
    Treatment: CHOP (immediate).
    Tazemetostat reserved for
    confirmed relapse.
    May achieve durable CHOP
    remission without maintenance.
```

## WHEN TO START TAZEMETOSTAT
## (AFTER SAFETY GATE PASSED)

```
OPTION A — MAINTENANCE AFTER CHOP
REMISSION (RECOMMENDED FOR R < 1.0):

  Wait for: confirmed complete
  or partial remission on week 12
  restaging (physical exam, lymph node
  assessment, +/- ultrasound).

  Start tazemetostat at: remission
  confirmation visit.

  Rationale: CHOP debulks the
  proliferating tumour mass.
  Tazemetostat then dissolves the
  EZH2 lock in residual cells
  that survived CHOP.
  The combination targets both
  the proliferating bulk (CHOP)
  and the epigenetic persistence
  mechanism (tazemetostat).

  Duration: continue through the
  end of the first year post-CHOP.
  Re-evaluate monthly.
  Stop if significant toxicity.

OPTION B — CONCURRENT WITH CHOP
(EXPERIMENTAL, DISCUSS WITH ONCOLOGIST):

  Starting tazemetostat during
  the 21-week CHOP protocol.
  Rationale: prevent EZH2-dependent
  survival of cells exposed to but
  not killed by CHOP.
  Risk: additive bone marrow
  suppression (tazemetostat alone
  has low haematological toxicity,
  but CHOP is myelosuppressive).
  Monitoring: CBC weekly during
  concurrent phase.
  Only for dogs with excellent
  performance status.

OPTION C — AT FIRST RELAPSE
(FOR R > 1.0):

  If CHOP remission has been
  achieved and the dog subsequently
  relapses, start tazemetostat
  at relapse confirmation.
  Re-biopsy at relapse if possible —
  depth score will typically be
  lower at relapse (deeper lock).
  Re-run safety gate on relapse
  biopsy before starting.

OPTION D — SALVAGE MONOTHERAPY
(CHOP-REFRACTORY CASES):

  Dog failed CHOP. Standard rescue
  protocols (rabacfosadine, lomustine,
  verdinexor) being considered.
  If H3K27me3 HIGH and R < 1.0:
  tazemetostat as single agent
  before or alongside rescue.
  Expected response rate ~20–35%
  based on human EZH2-overexpressing
  non-mutant DLBCL data.
  This is clinically meaningful
  for a dog with limited alternatives.
```

## TAZEMETOSTAT DOSING GUIDANCE
## (CANINE — OFF-LABEL)

```
IMPORTANT:
  No published canine pharmacokinetic
  data for tazemetostat exists as of
  2026-03-08. The dosing guidance below
  is extrapolated from human PK data
  using body surface area (BSA)
  conversion. It is a starting point
  for the treating veterinary oncologist
  to adjust based on clinical response
  and tolerability.

  CONSULT A VETERINARY ONCOLOGIST
  before prescribing. Off-label use
  requires informed owner consent.

HUMAN REFERENCE DOSE:
  800 mg PO BID in adults (~70kg).
  Total daily dose: 1,600 mg.
  Per kg: ~23 mg/kg/day.
  Per m² BSA: ~960 mg/m²/day.

CANINE BSA CONVERSION:
  Canine BSA (m²) = (body weight in kg)^0.67
  × 0.101 (Meeh-Rubner formula commonly
  used in veterinary oncology).

STARTING DOSE RECOMMENDATION
(BSA-based, conservative):
  400–600 mg/m² PO BID.
  (Approximately 50–60% of human BSA
  dose as a conservative starting point
  given absence of canine PK data.)

EXAMPLE DOSES BY WEIGHT:
  10 kg dog (BSA ≈ 0.46 m²):
    200 mg PO BID (one 200mg tablet BID).
  20 kg dog (BSA ≈ 0.74 m²):
    300–400 mg PO BID (1.5–2 × 200mg).
  30 kg dog (BSA ≈ 0.99 m²):
    400–600 mg PO BID (2–3 × 200mg).
  40 kg dog (BSA ≈ 1.22 m²):
    600 mg PO BID (3 × 200mg).

  These are starting dose ranges.
  Titrate based on response and
  tolerability. If significant GI
  toxicity at starting dose, reduce
  by 25% and re-assess.

ADMINISTRATION:
  With food or immediately after
  eating. The drug is pH-dependent
  (better absorbed in acidic
  conditions). Food increases
  gastric acid.
  Avoid concurrent proton pump
  inhibitors (omeprazole,
  pantoprazole) — these reduce
  gastric acidity and may impair
  absorption.

MONITORING:
  CBC and chemistry at baseline,
  week 2, week 4, then monthly.
  Liver enzymes (ALT, ALP): monthly.
  Tazemetostat has low haematological
  toxicity in humans but baseline
  data needed for canine patients.
  If ALT > 3× ULN: hold tazemetostat.
  Contact OncoLogist and OrganismCore.

DRUG INTERACTIONS:
  Tazemetostat is metabolised by
  CYP3A. In dogs:
  Avoid concurrent:
    Ketoconazole (strong CYP3A
    inhibitor — common in veterinary
    medicine for dermatological cases).
    Fluconazole (moderate CYP3A inhibitor).
    Phenobarbital (CYP3A inducer —
    common in canine epilepsy).
    Phenytoin (CYP3A inducer).
  If any of these are unavoidable,
  discuss dose adjustment with the
  prescribing oncologist.
```
