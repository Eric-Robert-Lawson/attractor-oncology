     If the safety gate is not run,
     the tazemetostat decision is not
     geometry-guided — it is guesswork.
     A dog with H3K27M geometry and
     tazemetostat is a dog receiving
     a contraindicated drug.

  3. KETOCONAZOLE INTERACTION.
     This is the most likely drug
     interaction in clinical practice.
     Many dogs with skin conditions
     are on ketoconazole.
     Ask about all concurrent
     medications before prescribing
     tazemetostat.

  4. PUBLISH NEGATIVE RESULTS.
     A depth-score-guided case where
     tazemetostat did not produce
     the predicted response, published
     honestly with complete IHC data,
     is as scientifically valuable as
     a confirmed case. It either
     extends the map (Type A) or
     invalidates the canine DLBCL
     derivation (Type B). Both outcomes
     are important. Neither is failure.
