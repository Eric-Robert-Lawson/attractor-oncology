## STATUS: ACTIVE — REQUIRED BEFORE TAZEMETOSTAT

```
THIS PROTOCOL HAS FOUR FUNCTIONS:

FUNCTION 1 — VISUAL TRIAGE (BEFORE
THE BIOPSY RESULT IS AVAILABLE):
  Pigmentation status is a free
  proxy for MITF/EZH2 depth.
  Record it at first examination.
  It immediately places the dog in
  an approximate depth category and
  guides urgency of tazemetostat
  consideration.

FUNCTION 2 — HUB CONFIRMATION:
  EZH2 H-score + H3K27me3 IHC
  confirm that EZH2 is the active
  convergence hub in this patient.
  If H3K27me3 is LOW (EZH2 paralysed),
  tazemetostat is contraindicated.
  (This is a less common finding in
  melanoma than in DLBCL, but the
  screen is mandatory.)

FUNCTION 3 — QUANTITATIVE DEPTH SCORE:
  R = MITF H-score / EZH2 H-score.
  Determines treatment urgency and
  sequence (tazemetostat alone vs
  tazemetostat + vaccine sequence).

FUNCTION 4 — TREATMENT SEQUENCING:
  The depth score determines when
  to add the ONCEPT vaccine relative
  to tazemetostat.
  The sequence is mechanistically
  important (not interchangeable).
  See treatment decision tree.

SURGERY CAN AND SHOULD PROCEED
BEFORE THIS PROTOCOL IS COMPLETE:
  Surgery for local control is the
  standard first step where feasible.
  Do not delay surgery for IHC.
  The IHC panel runs on the biopsy
  or surgical specimen.
  Tazemetostat and vaccine decisions
  are made after surgery, using the
  IHC result.
  For non-resectable or metastatic
  cases: the IHC panel determines
  first-line systemic therapy choice.
```

## STEP 0 — VISUAL TRIAGE
### Perform at first examination. No lab required.

```
RECORD TUMOUR PIGMENTATION:

☐ HEAVILY PIGMENTED (dark brown/black,
  uniform):
  Estimated R: > 2.0.
  MITF programme substantially active.
  EZH2 present but not yet dominant.
  Depth: SHALLOW.
  Immediate tazemetostat urgency: LOW.
  Plan: surgery ± radiation → ONCEPT
  vaccine (standard sequence) →
  tazemetostat at first relapse or if
  depth score returns intermediate/deep.

☐ PARTIALLY PIGMENTED (mixed dark and
  pale areas, irregular colour):
  Estimated R: 0.5–2.0.
  MITF partially suppressed.
  EZH2 moderately active.
  Depth: INTERMEDIATE.
  Tazemetostat urgency: MODERATE.
  Plan: surgery → IHC panel →
  tazemetostat + ONCEPT combination
  based on quantitative depth score.

☐ AMELANOTIC (pale pink/flesh colour,
  no visible pigment):
  Estimated R: < 0.5.
  MITF substantially suppressed.
  EZH2 dominant.
  Depth: DEEP to VERY DEEP.
  Tazemetostat urgency: HIGH.
  Plan: IHC panel → start
  tazemetostat as soon as biopsy
  result confirms melanocytic origin →
  2–4 weeks tazemetostat alone →
  ONCEPT vaccine when repigmentation
  first observed → continue both.

NOTE ON AMELANOTIC OMM DIAGNOSIS:
  Amelanotic melanoma is
  histopathologically challenging.
  It is often initially misdiagnosed
  as fibrosarcoma or poorly
  differentiated carcinoma on H&E
  alone. If the gross exam suggests
  amelanotic melanoma (pink oral mass,
  aggressive behaviour, bone invasion):
  Request extended IHC panel at
  initial biopsy submission:
  MITF, Melan-A, SOX-10, PNL2.
  At least two melanocytic markers
  must be positive to confirm
  melanocytic origin.
  Do not rely on MITF alone for
  amelanotic cases — MITF may be
  reduced. SOX-10 is the most
  sensitive marker in amelanotic OMM
  and should always be included.
```

## WHAT IS BEING MEASURED

```
PROTEIN 1 — MITF
(Microphthalmia-Associated
Transcription Factor)
  The Identity Anchor.
  The master melanocyte identity factor.
  Nuclear staining.
  Neoplastic melanocyte nuclei only.

  HIGH H-SCORE (> 150):
    MITF programme substantially active.
    Shallow attractor.
    Identity programme partially intact.
    R will likely be > 1.0.
    Tazemetostat as maintenance or
    watch-and-treat.

  INTERMEDIATE H-SCORE (80–150):
    MITF partially suppressed.
    Intermediate attractor.
    R dependent on EZH2 H-score.
    Tazemetostat likely indicated.

  LOW H-SCORE (< 80):
    MITF substantially suppressed.
    Deep attractor.
    R will likely be < 0.5 if EZH2
    is high.
    Tazemetostat strongly indicated
    (after hub confirmation with EZH2
    and H3K27me3).

  DIAGNOSTIC PANEL NOTE:
    For amelanotic tumours, also
    stain Melan-A and SOX-10 to
    confirm melanocytic origin.
    These are not part of the depth
    score calculation — they are
    diagnostic stains only.
    The depth score uses MITF, EZH2,
    and H3K27me3 only.

  ANTIBODY CLONES (validated in
  canine FFPE tissue):
    Clone MITF/915 (Novus Biologicals
    NBP2-45161, mouse monoclonal):
      Validated for canine melanoma
      IHC in peer-reviewed veterinary
      pathology literature through 2024.
      Reliable nuclear staining in
      canine melanocytic tumours.
      Also available as pre-diluted
      formulation (NBP2-48280).

    Clone MITF/2987R (rabbit monoclonal,
    Antibodies.com A252521):
      Validated for human and canine
      FFPE tissue. No cross-reactivity
      with mouse or rat — important
      for species specificity in
      research settings.
      Suitable for paired IHC with
      EZH2 D2H1 (both rabbit) using
      appropriate sequential staining
      or separate sections.

    Recommended: use MITF/915 (mouse)
    for canine tissue as primary clone
    — most validated canine experience.
    Use MITF/2987R as confirmation if
    MITF/915 result is ambiguous.

PROTEIN 2 — EZH2
(Enhancer of Zeste Homolog 2)
  The Convergence Hub protein.
  Nuclear staining.
  Neoplastic melanocyte nuclei only.

  Antibody: Clone D2H1
  (Cell Signaling Technology #5246).
  Rabbit monoclonal. Validated in
  multiple species. Same antibody
  used in the BRCA IHC calibration
  protocol and the angiosarcoma/
  hemangiosarcoma derivations.

  HIGH H-SCORE (> 150):
    EZH2 overexpressed. Potential
    active hub. Confirm with H3K27me3.

  INTERMEDIATE H-SCORE (80–150):
    EZH2 present. Check H3K27me3.

  LOW H-SCORE (< 80):
    EZH2 not overexpressed. May not
    be the primary hub. Unusual for
    amelanotic OMM — investigate
    whether BRAF is also absent.

PROTEIN 3 — H3K27me3
(Histone H3 lysine 27 trimethyl)
  THE HUB ACTIVITY READOUT.
  Most important for the treatment
  safety decision.

  Antibody: Clone C36B11
  (Cell Signaling Technology #9733).
  Rabbit monoclonal. Fully conserved
  PTM — complete cross-reactivity
  across all vertebrate species.

  H3K27ME3 HIGH (> 100, > 75% positive,
  stromal control positive):
    EZH2 is ACTIVE.
    Lock is being deposited.
    Tazemetostat will reduce this.
    PROCEED to depth score.

  H3K27ME3 LOW (< 60, < 25% positive,
  stromal control positive):
    EZH2 is PARALYSED.
    This is rare in melanoma
    (unlike DLBCL) but must be
    checked. If H3K27me3 is globally
    low in the tumour with normal
    stromal positivity: do not use
    tazemetostat. Investigate for
    H3K27M histone mutation.

  H3K27ME3 ABSENT (all cells including
  stroma negative):
    TECHNICAL FAILURE.
    REPEAT THE STAIN.
    Do not make treatment decisions
    from a technically failed H3K27me3
    stain.

  INTERNAL CONTROL — CRITICAL:
    Normal stromal fibroblasts, blood
    vessel endothelium, and inflammatory
    cells in the same section should
    show H3K27me3 positivity.
    This is the mandatory internal
    positive control.
    If stroma is negative: technical
    failure. Repeat.
    If stroma is positive but tumour
    cells are negative: biological
    finding (possible H3K27M mutation
    or alternative mechanism).
    The distinction requires the
    stromal control.
    Do not accept a result without
    confirming stromal positivity.
```

## THE TEST — STEP BY STEP

```
TISSUE REQUIRED:
  Biopsy specimen or surgical
  excision specimen.
  FFPE block from primary tumour.
  Minimum tissue: 2mm × 8mm core.
  Preferred: surgical excision
  specimen from primary tumour —
  provides maximum tissue and
  allows assessment of depth
  score across the tumour face
  (heterogeneous tumours may have
  zones of different depth).

  For purely cytological initial
  diagnosis (FNA): FNA cytology
  is diagnostic for melanocytic
  origin in pigmented cases.
  For amelanotic cases: FNA alone
  is insufficient — biopsy required
  for IHC confirmation of melanocytic
  origin AND depth score.

ANTIBODY PANEL — 3 STAINS
FOR DEPTH SCORE:
  Run on separate sequential FFPE
  sections (4 micron each) from
  the same block.

  Stain 1: MITF
    Antibody: MITF/915 (Novus)
    Dilution: 1:200–1:400
    Antigen retrieval: HIER,
    citrate buffer pH 6.0,
    20 minutes, pressure cooker
    or water bath.
    Expected: nuclear staining,
    neoplastic cells. Intensity
    varies — score all intensities.
    Internal control: normal
    melanocytes in adjacent oral
    mucosa (if present) should show
    moderate MITF positivity.

  Stain 2: EZH2
    Antibody: D2H1 (CST #5246)
    Dilution: 1:400–1:800
    Antigen retrieval: HIER,
    citrate buffer pH 6.0,
    20 minutes.
    Expected: nuclear staining,
    higher in neoplastic cells
    than stromal/inflammatory cells.

  Stain 3: H3K27me3
    Antibody: C36B11 (CST #9733)
    Dilution: 1:200–1:400
    Antigen retrieval: HIER,
    citrate buffer pH 6.0,
    20 minutes.
    Expected: nuclear staining.
    STROMAL INTERNAL CONTROL MUST
    BE POSITIVE. Fibroblasts and
    endothelium should stain
    moderately positive.

OPTIONAL DIAGNOSTIC PANEL
(for amelanotic cases — separate stains,
not part of depth score):
  Melan-A (clone A103, Dako M7196):
    Most sensitive melanocytic marker
    in canine tissue alongside SOX-10.
    Cytoplasmic/nuclear staining.
  SOX-10:
    Most sensitive single marker
    for amelanotic canine melanoma.
    Nuclear staining.
    Should always be included when
    amelanotic melanoma is suspected.
  PNL2:
    Additional melanocytic marker.
    Cytoplasmic staining.

SCORING — H-SCORE:
  Score neoplastic melanocyte
  nuclei only.
  Minimum 300 cells per stain
  (melanoma cells are larger and
  more variable than lymphocytes —
  use higher cell count for accuracy).

  Exclude:
    Inflammatory infiltrate nuclei.
    Stromal fibroblast nuclei.
    Vascular endothelium nuclei.
    (These are used as internal
    controls but not scored for R.)

  H-score formula:
    (% weak × 1) + (% moderate × 2) +
    (% strong × 3).
    Range: 0–300.

  For H3K27me3:
    Also record % neoplastic cells
    positive (any intensity) as a
    simple percentage for the safety
    gate decision.

INTER-OBSERVER RELIABILITY:
  Two observers score independently.
  ICC target: > 0.7 for each stain.
  Mean of two scores used.
  If MITF scoring is ambiguous
  (melanin pigment can obscure
  chromogen in pigmented tumours):
  Use RED (AEC) chromogen for MITF
  IHC on pigmented sections to
  distinguish brown melanin from
  DAB chromogen, OR use MITF on
  a section with minimal pigment
  if available in the block.

TURNAROUND: 3–7 business days.
ESTIMATED COST: $150–$350 for
  three stains plus pathologist fee.
```

## DEPTH SCORE CALCULATION AND
## INTERPRETATION

```
SAFETY GATE FIRST:

  H3K27me3 % neoplastic positive:

  > 75% with stromal control +:
    → HUB ACTIVE. Proceed.

  < 25% with stromal control +:
    → HUB PARALYSED. Do not treat
       with tazemetostat. Unusual
       for melanoma. Investigate.
       Contact OrganismCore@proton.me.

  All cells negative including stroma:
    → TECHNICAL FAILURE. Repeat stain.

  75–25% (intermediate):
    → Borderline. Discuss with
       oncologist. Hold tazemetostat
       pending further investigation.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

DEPTH SCORE (if hub confirmed):

  R = MITF H-score / EZH2 H-score.

  R > 2.0 — SHALLOW:
    MITF dominant. EZH2 secondary.
    Visible proxy: pigmented tumour.
    Treatment:
      Surgery + radiation (if needed)
      → ONCEPT vaccine (standard
      sequence — tyrosinase already
      expressed, no need to pre-treat
      with tazemetostat).
      → Tazemetostat at first relapse
      or if depth score at relapse
      is intermediate/deep.

  R 1.0–2.0 — INTERMEDIATE:
    MITF and EZH2 roughly matched.
    Visible proxy: partially pigmented.
    Treatment:
      Surgery (if feasible).
      → IHC result will guide sequence.
      → Tazemetostat 2 weeks post-surgery
      → ONCEPT vaccine when any
      repigmentation observed.
      → Continue both.
      → Add anti-PD-L1 if available
      after vaccine series.

  R 0.5–1.0 — DEEP:
    EZH2 approaching dominance.
    Visible proxy: mostly amelanotic.
    Treatment:
      Surgery (if feasible).
      → Tazemetostat starts within
      2 weeks of surgery.
      → Wait for repigmentation signal
      (4–8 weeks typically).
      → ONCEPT vaccine when repigmentation
      first observed.
      → Continue tazemetostat + vaccine.
      → Anti-PD-L1 after vaccine series.

  R < 0.5 — VERY DEEP:
    EZH2 dominant. MITF suppressed.
    Visible proxy: fully amelanotic.
    Treatment:
      Surgery if feasible. For
      non-resectable disease: systemic
      therapy first.
      → Tazemetostat immediately
      on hub confirmation.
      → Repigmentation expected in
      4–12 weeks (slower than shallow).
      → Do NOT start ONCEPT vaccine
      until repigmentation is confirmed
      (tyrosinase target must be
      de-repressed first).
      → ONCEPT vaccine when
      repigmentation confirmed.
      → Anti-PD-L1 after vaccine series.
      → If no repigmentation by week 12:
      consider adding HDAC inhibitor
      or anti-PD-L1 earlier — very
      deep attractor may require
      combination to dissolve.
```

## TAZEMETOSTAT DOSING GUIDANCE
## (CANINE — OFF-LABEL)

```
IMPORTANT:
  No published canine pharmacokinetic
  data for tazemetostat as of 2026-03-08.
  Off-label use. Informed consent
  required. Veterinary oncologist
  consultation required before
  prescribing.

HUMAN REFERENCE DOSE:
  800 mg PO BID in adults (~70kg).
  BSA-equivalent: ~960 mg/m²/day.

CANINE STARTING DOSE:
  400–600 mg/m² PO BID.
  (50–60% of human BSA dose —
  conservative starting point.)

CANINE BSA FORMULA:
  BSA (m²) = body weight (kg)^0.67 × 0.101

WEIGHT-BASED EXAMPLES:
  8 kg dog (BSA ≈ 0.40 m²):
    160–240 mg PO BID.
    (Compounded capsule — human
    200mg tablet gives ~500 mg/m²/day.)

  15 kg dog (BSA ≈ 0.64 m²):
    256–384 mg PO BID.
    (Compounded capsule or
    1.5 × 200mg tablets if dog
    accepts tablet form.)

  25 kg dog (BSA ≈ 0.92 m²):
    368–552 mg PO BID.
    (2–3 × 200mg tablets or
    compounded capsule.)

  35 kg dog (BSA ≈ 1.18 m²):
    472–708 mg PO BID.
    (2–3 × 200mg tablets.)

ADMINISTRATION:
  Oral. With food or immediately
  after feeding. The drug is
  pH-dependent (BCS Class 2 —
  better absorbed in acidic
  conditions). Feeding increases
  gastric acidity. Do not give
  on a completely empty stomach.

DRUG INTERACTIONS:
  AVOID CONCURRENT:
    Ketoconazole — strong CYP3A
    inhibitor. Widely used in
    veterinary dermatology.
    If dog is on ketoconazole
    for any reason: either stop
    ketoconazole (if safe) or
    reduce tazemetostat dose by
    50% with close monitoring.
    Fluconazole — moderate CYP3A
    inhibitor.
    Phenobarbital — CYP3A inducer.
    Common in canine epilepsy.
    If dog is on phenobarbital:
    tazemetostat dose may need
    to be higher to maintain
    therapeutic levels. Contact
    OrganismCore@proton.me for
    guidance.
    Omeprazole / pantoprazole —
    reduce gastric acidity, may
    impair absorption. Use only
    if GI protection is essential.
    If required, administer at
    least 2 hours after tazemetostat.

MONITORING:
  Baseline: CBC, chemistry, liver
  enzymes (ALT, ALP, AST), BUN,
  creatinine, total protein.
  Week 2: CBC + chemistry.
  Week 4: CBC + chemistry.
  Then monthly.

  ABORT CRITERIA (stop tazemetostat):
    ALT or ALP > 5× ULN on two
    consecutive measurements.
    Neutrophils < 500/μL.
    Platelets < 50,000/μL.
    Confirmed new primary tumour
    unrelated to the melanoma
    (tazemetostat theoretical
    secondary malignancy risk
    per FDA label — document any
    new masses and evaluate).
    Severe persistent GI toxicity
    (> grade 2 vomiting/diarrhoea
    not responsive to dose reduction).
    Owner elected to stop.
    Veterinarian clinical judgement.
```

## REPIGMENTATION MONITORING PROTOCOL

```
REPIGMENTATION IS THE PRIMARY
RESPONSE SIGNAL.
Monitor it actively. Document it
photographically at every visit.

PHOTOGRAPHY PROTOCOL:
  Oral examination photographs
  taken at every visit:
    Pre-treatment baseline (date +
    scale reference in image).
    Week 2 (first check).
    Week 4.
    Week 8.
    Week 12.
    Monthly thereafter.
  Photograph with consistent
  lighting and scale reference.
  Same anatomical orientation
  at each visit.
  Owner encouraged to photograph
  weekly at home and send via
  practice communication portal.

REPIGMENTATION GRADING:
  Grade 0: No change. Amelanotic.
  Grade 1: Faint patchy darkening
    at tumour margins. First sign.
  Grade 2: Partial pigmentation.
    > 25% of visible tumour surface
    shows darkening.
  Grade 3: Majority of tumour
    surface pigmented (> 50%).
  Grade 4: Near-complete pigmentation
    or pigmented nodule replacing
    previous amelanotic mass.

  First repigmentation signal
  (Grade 1):
    Confirm depth score
    de-repression is occurring.
    This is the moment to start
    ONCEPT vaccine if not yet
    started.
    The tyrosinase antigen is
    now being re-expressed.
    Document date.

  No repigmentation by week 12:
    Consider whether the attractor
    is too deep for tazemetostat
    monotherapy.
    Consider adding HDAC inhibitor
    or anti-PD-L1 earlier.
    Re-biopsy: check H3K27me3 —
    has it changed? If H3K27me3
    still high and no repigmentation:
    tazemetostat absorption issue
    (check drug interactions, check
    feeding protocol) OR alternative
    hub (not EZH2) has taken over.
    Document fully.

IMAGING:
  Oral examination is primary.
  Chest radiographs: baseline,
  month 1, month 3, then every
  3 months (metastasis monitoring).
  Abdominal ultrasound: baseline,
  month 3, then every 3 months.
  CT of skull/head: baseline,
  month 3 (bone invasion monitoring).
  Mandibular lymph node FNA or
  excision: if lymphadenopathy
  develops or at initial staging.
```
