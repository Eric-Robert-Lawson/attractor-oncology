## STATUS: ACTIVE — REQUIRED BEFORE TAZEMETOSTAT OR AZACITIDINE

```
This test has two functions:

FUNCTION 1 — PATIENT SELECTION:
  Confirms whether tazemetostat
  (EZH2-dominant cases) or
  azacitidine (DNMT3A-dominant
  cases) is the correct second
  drug for this specific patient.
  Without this test, you may
  treat a DNMT3A-negative patient
  with azacitidine — targeting
  a lock that is not the primary
  maintenance mechanism in that
  patient. That is not a framework
  failure. That is a wrong-target
  treatment that the IHC test
  would have prevented.

FUNCTION 2 — DEPTH SCORE:
  Establishes the depth of the
  false attractor in this patient —
  predicting how fast and how
  completely the response will be.
  High R (FOXL2 > EZH2):
    Residual female identity signal.
    Shallow false attractor.
    Fast response expected.
  Low R (EZH2 >> FOXL2):
    Deep false attractor.
    FOXL2 programme heavily silenced.
    Slower response expected.
    Longer treatment course needed.

DESLORELIN CAN START BEFORE THIS TEST.
  Deslorelin is existing standard
  first-line care for avian ovarian
  neoplasia. It does not require
  the depth test. It can begin
  immediately on diagnosis.
  The depth test runs in parallel
  while deslorelin is being tolerated
  and the owner decides about
  escalation.
  The IHC result is typically
  ready before the first deslorelin
  reassessment at week 4–6.
  The vet sees the depth score
  at the same visit they assess
  the deslorelin response.
  Decision and data arrive together.

THREE PROTEINS ARE TESTED:
  FOXL2  — the Identity Anchor.
  EZH2   — the primary Convergence Hub.
  DNMT3A — the secondary Hub
           (if EZH2 insufficient
           to explain depth).

  Three stains. One block.
  One IHC panel.
  One result that determines
  which drug to add and when.

STRUCTURAL NOTE:
  This protocol is the exact
  mirror of the BUDGERIGAR_TT
  IHC depth protocol, with
  FOXL2 substituted for DMRT1
  as the Identity Anchor.
  The scoring system, depth
  classification, and decision
  tree are structurally identical.
  This is not coincidental —
  it is the framework operating
  symmetrically, as predicted.

AVIAN TISSUE CONSIDERATIONS:
  Cockatiel ovarian tissue is
  small and may be architecturally
  disrupted by tumor growth.
  Biopsy material may be limited.
  FNA cytology with IHC run on
  a cell block preparation is
  acceptable as lower-evidence
  alternative if full biopsy
  poses excessive anaesthetic
  risk. Document method used.
  Note reduced confidence in
  H-score accuracy if aspirate only.
  Consult avian anaesthesia
  specialist before general
  anaesthesia in any bird under
  80g or severely compromised.
  Standard cockatiel weight range:
  80–130g. Weight at biopsy should
  be recorded and compared to
  baseline — weight loss >10%
  from baseline is an anaesthetic
  risk flag.
```

## WHAT IS BEING MEASURED

```
PROTEIN 1 — FOXL2
(Forkhead Box L2)
  The Identity Anchor.
  The master transcription factor
  of granulosa cell and ovarian
  identity in all vertebrates.
  Nuclear staining.
  Tumour cell nuclei only.

  High H-score (> 100):
    FOXL2 programme partially active.
    Residual female identity signal.
    Shallow false attractor.
    Strong predicted response to
    tazemetostat — EZH2 has not yet
    fully silenced the FOXL2
    programme.

  Low H-score (< 50):
    FOXL2 programme heavily suppressed.
    Deep false attractor. Female
    identity largely silenced.
    Tazemetostat still indicated
    but response will be slower.
    Longer treatment course needed.

  Zero or near-zero staining:
    FOXL2 programme fully silenced.
    Very deep false attractor.
    Assess DNMT3A — may indicate
    dual-hub case requiring both
    tazemetostat and azacitidine.

  Note — FOXL2 mutation context:
    In human GCTs, FOXL2 C134W
    is a gain-of-function mutation
    that alters FOXL2 DNA binding
    without eliminating protein
    expression. In avian GCTs,
    the mutation status of FOXL2
    is unstudied. IHC H-score
    measures protein presence and
    nuclear localisation — not
    mutational status. A high FOXL2
    H-score in an avian GCT may
    therefore indicate a mutant
    FOXL2 that is present but
    non-functional, not necessarily
    functional FOXL2 activity.
    This is a known limitation of
    the IHC approach in this context
    and should be documented.
    If avian FOXL2 sequencing is
    available in the clinical
    setting, this adds interpretive
    value. It is not required
    for the depth test to proceed.

PROTEIN 2 — EZH2
(Enhancer of Zeste Homolog 2)
  The primary Convergence Hub.
  The PRC2 catalytic subunit.
  Deposits H3K27me3 — the
  repressive chromatin mark —
  maintaining the false attractor
  by silencing the FOXL2-governed
  female identity programme.
  Nuclear staining.
  Tumour cell nuclei only.

  High H-score (> 150):
    Active EZH2 lock confirmed.
    Heavy H3K27me3 accumulation
    expected on FOXL2 programme
    promoters.
    TAZEMETOSTAT is the correct
    escalation drug.

  Intermediate H-score (80–150):
    EZH2 active but not dominant.
    Check DNMT3A. If DNMT3A also
    elevated: dual-hub case.
    If DNMT3A is low: EZH2 is
    still the primary hub.
    Tazemetostat indicated.

  Low H-score (< 80):
    EZH2 is not the dominant hub.
    Check DNMT3A.
    If DNMT3A high: azacitidine
    is the correct drug.
    If both EZH2 and DNMT3A low:
    atypical case. Do not proceed
    with escalation without further
    assessment. Contact
    OrganismCore@proton.me.

PROTEIN 3 — DNMT3A
(DNA Methyltransferase 3A)
  The secondary Convergence Hub.
  The de novo methyltransferase
  that may contribute to silencing
  the FOXL2 programme via CpG
  methylation in addition to or
  instead of the EZH2/H3K27me3
  mechanism.
  Nuclear staining.
  Tumour cell nuclei only.

  High H-score (> 150) with
  EZH2 also high:
    Dual-hub case.
    Both epigenetic locks active.
    Consider sequential therapy:
    tazemetostat first, then
    azacitidine addition if response
    is partial after 8 weeks.

  High H-score with EZH2 low:
    DNMT3A is the primary hub.
    Azacitidine is the correct drug.

  Low H-score:
    DNMT3A is not a dominant hub.
    EZH2 pathway assessment
    is sufficient.

THE RATIO AND THE DECISION:
  R = FOXL2 H-score / EZH2 H-score

  R > 1.5, EZH2 high:
    Shallow EZH2 lock.
    Deslorelin + tazemetostat.
    Best expected response.
    Coelomic size response expected
    at 4–6 weeks.

  R 0.5–1.5, EZH2 high:
    Intermediate depth.
    Deslorelin + tazemetostat.
    Moderate response expected.
    Monitor for DNMT3A hub
    involvement from month 2–3
    if initial response is partial.

  R < 0.5, EZH2 high:
    Deep false attractor.
    Deslorelin + tazemetostat.
    Slower response expected.
    Commit to 12-week minimum
    treatment course before
    assessing efficacy.
    Coelomic change may not
    appear until week 8–10.

  EZH2 low, DNMT3A high:
    DNMT3A-dominant case.
    Deslorelin + azacitidine.
    Document carefully — atypical
    for granulosa cell tumors.

  Both hubs low:
    Atypical case. Do not proceed
    with escalation.
    Contact OrganismCore@proton.me.
```

## THE TEST — STEP BY STEP

```
STEP 1 — TISSUE ACQUISITION:
  Source: Tumour biopsy or FNA
  cell block from ovarian or
  coelomic mass.
  Preferred: Endoscopic core
  biopsy or excisional sample
  if accessible.
  Acceptable alternative: FNA
  cell block — document clearly.
  Fixation: 10% neutral buffered
  formalin, standard protocol.
  Processing: FFPE block.
  Sections: 4-micron sections
  for each IHC stain.

STEP 2 — IHC PANEL:
  Three stains run as one panel:
  Stain 1: Anti-FOXL2
  Stain 2: Anti-EZH2
  Stain 3: Anti-DNMT3A

  Antibody validation note:
  Most commercially available
  FOXL2, EZH2, and DNMT3A antibodies
  were validated in human and mouse
  tissue. Cross-reactivity in avian
  tissue requires confirmation.
  Request the pathology lab to run
  a positive mammalian control
  alongside the avian section and
  confirm staining pattern is
  consistent with known biology
  before scoring.

  If antibody does not cross-react:
  Document this clearly. This is
  a known technical barrier for
  avian IHC and a finding worth
  reporting. It does not invalidate
  the framework.

STEP 3 — SCORING:
  Score each stain using H-score:
  H-score = (% cells 1+) × 1
           + (% cells 2+) × 2
           + (% cells 3+) × 3
  Range: 0–300.
  Score tumour cells only.
  Score a minimum of 100 tumour
  cells per stain.
  Score two representative
  high-power fields (40×).
  Report mean H-score from both.

STEP 4 — CALCULATE R:
  R = FOXL2 H-score / EZH2 H-score
  If EZH2 H-score = 0:
  R is undefined.
  Classify as EZH2-null case.
  Assess DNMT3A as primary hub.

STEP 5 — CLASSIFY AND RECOMMEND:
  Apply the decision tree above.
  Document all scores, R value,
  hub classification, depth
  classification, recommended
  escalation drug, and predicted
  response timeline.

STEP 6 — REPORT TO CLINICIAN:
  Provide the IHC Reporting Template
  (below) to the supervising vet.
  The vet uses this to complete
  Section 1 (Before-Document) of
  the Case Report Template before
  starting the escalation drug.
```

## RESULT INTERPRETATION AND
## TREATMENT DECISION TREE

```
RESULT A — CLASSIC GRANULOSA CASE:
  FOXL2: Low (< 50)
  EZH2: High (> 150)
  DNMT3A: Low to intermediate
  R: < 0.5

  INTERPRETATION:
  FOXL2 programme deeply silenced.
  EZH2 is the active epigenetic lock.
  Classic deep false attractor.

  TREATMENT:
  Deslorelin (continue or start) +
  Tazemetostat (escalation drug).
  Minimum 12-week commitment.
  Coelomic size reduction expected
  at 8–12 weeks.

RESULT B — SHALLOW ATTRACTOR:
  FOXL2: Intermediate (50–100)
  EZH2: High (> 150)
  DNMT3A: Low
  R: 0.5–1.0

  INTERPRETATION:
  FOXL2 partially active.
  EZH2 lock present but identity
  signal not yet fully lost.
  Moderate depth false attractor.

  TREATMENT:
  Deslorelin + Tazemetostat.
  First coelomic size response
  expected at 4–8 weeks.
  Best prognosis category for
  this protocol.

RESULT C — DUAL HUB CASE:
  FOXL2: Low (< 50)
  EZH2: High (> 150)
  DNMT3A: High (> 150)
  R: < 0.5

  INTERPRETATION:
  Both epigenetic locks active.
  Deep and complex false attractor.

  TREATMENT:
  Start with Deslorelin +
  Tazemetostat. Reassess at week 8.
  If partial response: add
  azacitidine as third agent.
  Contact OrganismCore@proton.me
  for geometric case consultation.

RESULT D — DNMT3A-DOMINANT:
  FOXL2: Low to zero
  EZH2: Low (< 80)
  DNMT3A: High (> 150)
  R: undefined or < 0.3

  INTERPRETATION:
  Atypical. DNMT3A is primary hub.

  TREATMENT:
  Deslorelin + Azacitidine.
  Document carefully.
  Uncommon presentation — this
  case is high value for the
  framework.

RESULT E — INDETERMINATE:
  All three hubs low or antibody
  cross-reactivity failure.

  ACTION:
  Do not start escalation drug.
  Consider repeat biopsy.
  Contact OrganismCore@proton.me.
  Document all technical findings.
```
