  belly to a photo taken at the
  first vet visit. Your vet will
  give you a baseline photo
  to keep at home.

  Score the belly using this scale:

  1 = Much smaller than baseline —
      clear improvement
  2 = Slightly smaller than baseline —
      some improvement
  3 = Same as baseline — no change
  4 = Slightly larger than baseline —
      possible progression
  5 = Much larger than baseline —
      clear progression

  Also note:
  — Whether your bird is using
    both legs normally today
  — Whether your bird is eating,
    drinking, and active as normal
  — Whether your bird is vocalising
    normally (cockatiels should
    whistle and vocalise daily)

  If the bird's belly looks much
  larger than baseline, or if the
  bird is sitting on the cage floor
  and cannot fly to its perch,
  or if the bird is breathing with
  its mouth open: call your vet
  immediately. Do not wait for
  the next scheduled visit.

  Bring this sheet to every
  vet visit. Take a photo of the
  bird from the side every week
