```
HEPATOTOXICITY MONITORING:
  If ALT rises above baseline but
  < 3× ULN: note date, continue
  with increased monitoring.
  If ALT rises to 3–5× ULN:
  HOLD tazemetostat. Recheck in
  5–7 days. If declining: discuss
  with oncologist before resuming
  at lower dose. If rising: STOP.
  If ALT > 5× ULN at any point:
  STOP permanently. Document fully.
  Report to OrganismCore@proton.me.

HAEMATOLOGICAL:
  Neutropenia (< 1,000/μL): HOLD.
  Neutropenia (< 500/μL): STOP.
  Thrombocytopenia (< 75,000/μL): HOLD.
  Thrombocytopenia (< 50,000/μL): STOP.

FELINE-SPECIFIC TOXICITIES TO WATCH:
  Hepatic lipidosis (if anorexia
  > 2–3 days): immediate risk in
  cats. Anorexia in a cat on a
  novel drug = emergency.
  At first sign of reduced appetite:
  nutritional support (syringe
  feeding, appetite stimulant —
  mirtazapine transdermal),
  reduce dose, contact oncologist.
  Do NOT allow a cat to go 48h
  without eating on tazemetostat.

  Heinz body anaemia: rare but
  cats are susceptible to oxidative
  RBC damage from certain drugs.
  Monitor HCT and reticulocytes
  if unexplained anaemia develops.

  Hypothermia: cats on any
  systemically unwell state cool
  rapidly. Monitor body temperature
  at every clinic visit.
```

```
  2. ANOREXIA IN CATS IS EMERGENCY.
     Hepatic lipidosis can develop
     within 48–72 hours of anorexia
     in cats. Any cat on tazemetostat
     that stops eating: dose reduce,
     support nutrition, contact
     oncologist immediately.

  3. ALT THRESHOLD IS 3× ULN.
     Not 5×. Cats have less hepatic
     reserve for novel compounds.
     Lower threshold = earlier
     safety response.

  4. SAFETY DATA IS THE CONTRIBUTION.
     A well-documented feline
     tazemetostat case that shows
     the drug is tolerated at
     conservative doses — even if
     the anti-tumour response is
     limited — is a publishable
     and important veterinary
     pharmacology finding.
     Do not undervalue safety data.
     Publish it.
```
