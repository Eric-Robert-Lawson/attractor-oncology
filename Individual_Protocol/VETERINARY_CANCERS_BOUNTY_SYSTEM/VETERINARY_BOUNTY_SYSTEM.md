## CASE 1A — CANINE TRANSITIONAL CELL CARCINOMA
### The Lowest-Cost Entry Point

---

**Species:** Dog
**Cancer:** Transitional Cell Carcinoma (TCC) / Urothelial Carcinoma of the bladder
**Starting drug cost:** $10–60/month (piroxicam alone)
**Breeds most affected:** Scottish Terrier (18× normal risk), West Highland White Terrier, Beagle, Shetland Sheepdog, Wire Fox Terrier

```
GEOMETRIC DERIVATION:

  Cell of origin:
    Urothelial luminal epithelial cell.

  Identity Anchor (A): FOXA1
    The pioneer transcription factor
    of luminal urothelial identity.
    What a urothelial luminal cell is.
    FOXA1 maintains the differentiation
    programme that keeps urothelial
    cells in their correct identity.

  Convergence Hub (H): DNMT3A
    De novo DNA methyltransferase.
    DNMT3A deposits CpG methylation
    directly on the FOXA1 luminal
    differentiation target promoters.
    The cancer cell uses DNMT3A to
    maintain the epigenetic silencing
    of the programme that would tell
    it to differentiate and stop
    proliferating.

  Identity Axis:
    R = FOXA1 / DNMT3A expression

  Drug target: DNMT3A
  Drug: Azacitidine
    (hypomethylating agent —
    inhibits DNMT3A, removing the
    methylation lock on FOXA1 targets)

  Anti-inflammatory co-drug: Piroxicam
    COX-2 inhibitor.
    Addresses the inflammatory
    fuel that drives proliferation.
    Already documented to produce
    70% disease control in canine TCC.
    Piroxicam is the established
    first-line palliative drug.
    Azacitidine is the geometric
    escalation that addresses the
    epigenetic lock directly.

EMPIRICAL PRECEDENT:
  Azacitidine has been tested in
  canine TCC in a published
  veterinary study and produced
  documented responses.
  The geometry now provides the
  mechanistic explanation for why
  it worked and which patients will
  respond (those with DNMT3A-dominant
  depth confirmed on IHC).

EXPECTED RESPONSE:
  Piroxicam alone:
    ~70% disease control rate
    (partial response or stable disease).
    Complete remission: 6–12%.
    Median survival: 5–8 months.
  Piroxicam + azacitidine
  (depth-score-selected patients):
    Higher response rate predicted
    in FOXA1-low / DNMT3A-high dogs.
    Depth-guided combination is the
    first geometry-directed protocol
    in canine TCC.

FIRST SIGNAL (weeks 4–6):
  Reduced haematuria
  (less blood visible in urine).
  Reduced straining to urinate.
  Improved comfort scores.
  Owner-observable. No imaging needed
  for first signal assessment.

IHC DEPTH TEST:
  FOXA1 + DNMT3A on existing
  cystoscopy or surgical biopsy block.
  $100–$250. 3–7 days turnaround.
  If FOXA1 low / DNMT3A high:
  priority case for azacitidine
  escalation.
  If FOXA1 high / DNMT3A low:
  piroxicam monotherapy first.
  Re-evaluate at week 8.

MONITORING:
  Weekly: owner urine observation
  (haematuria, straining frequency).
  Week 2: CBC + chemistry.
  Week 4: clinical reassessment.
  Week 8: PRIMARY GATE — urinalysis,
  ultrasound if available,
  quality of life score.
  Monthly thereafter if responding.

ABORT CRITERIA:
  Worsening haematuria + declining
  QoL score two consecutive weeks.
  Renal toxicity on chemistry panel.
  Anorexia > 48 hours.
  Dog is suffering more than before
  treatment: STOP. Discuss with vet.

COST BREAKDOWN:
  Piroxicam: $10–60/month.
  IHC depth test: $100–250 (one-time).
  Azacitidine (if escalated):
  $300–900/cycle.
  Minimum entry cost: $110–310
  total for first 6 weeks.
  This is the lowest entry cost
  on this list.

BOUNTY CLAIM:
```
