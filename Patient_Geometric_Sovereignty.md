### PREMISE 2: The clinical system operates
###           on population statistics.

```
The medical system is built on
evidence-based medicine.

Evidence-based medicine is built on
randomised controlled trials.

Randomised controlled trials measure
what happens to populations.

The output of this system is:

  "In a population of N patients
   with diagnosis X,
   treatment Y produced outcome Z
   in P% of cases."

This is valid science.
This is necessary science.
This has saved enormous numbers of lives.

AND —

It is a statement about populations.
Not about you.

When your oncologist recommends
a treatment, they are saying:

  "In patients like you,
   this treatment works P% of the time."

They are not saying:

  "This treatment will work for you."

They cannot say that.
The current system does not have
the instruments to say that.

The population statistic is the best
available instrument for treatment
selection within the current framework.

It is not the only possible instrument.
It is not the most specific instrument.
It is the validated, standardised,
legally recognised instrument.

These are not the same thing.
```

**Status: Established. Acknowledged by
the medical system itself.**
