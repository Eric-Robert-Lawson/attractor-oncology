## PART I — THE HOURGLASS MODEL
## STATED PRECISELY

### 1.1 What the Hourglass Model Is

Every animal embryo develops through a
sequence of stages. If you plot the
morphological similarity between embryos
of different species within a phylum
across developmental time, the curve
has the shape of an hourglass:

- **Early embryonic stages:** divergent.
  Different species start development
  differently. A frog egg and a human
  egg cleave differently. The early
  stages are species-specific.

- **The waist of the hourglass —
  the phylotypic stage:** maximally
  convergent. All vertebrate embryos
  at this stage look nearly identical.
  They all have gill arches, a notochord,
  a neural tube in the same configuration,
  somites in the same arrangement.

- **Late embryonic stages:** divergent
  again. Development then specializes
  toward the specific adult form.

**The waist of the hourglass corresponds
to the last common ancestor of the phylum.**

For vertebrates, the phylotypic stage
corresponds to the vertebrate ancestor —
a fish-like organism living approximately
500 million years ago.

This is not metaphor. The genes expressed
maximally at the phylotypic stage are
the most ancient and most conserved
genes in the vertebrate genome. They
are the genes that have not changed
since the vertebrate ancestor. The
embryo, at that specific moment, is
running the original ancestral program.

### 1.2 The Hourglass Is a Compressed
### Evolutionary Sequence

Read more carefully, the full developmental
sequence from fertilization to birth is
not merely an hourglass. It is a
**compressed traversal of the evolutionary
lineage of the organism.**

The stages, in sequence, correspond to:

| Developmental Stage | Evolutionary Grade | Approximate Timeline |
|---|---|---|
| Morula (solid cell mass) | First multicellular aggregates | ~700–800 Ma |
| Blastula (hollow ball of cells, no axes) | Earliest colonial multicellular organisms, possibly Ediacaran grade | ~635–600 Ma |
| Gastrula (two-layer organization, first inner/outer distinction) | Diploblast grade: cnidarians, jellyfish ancestors | ~600–560 Ma |
| Neurula (neural tube forms, primary axis established) | Early bilaterian ancestor | ~560–540 Ma |
| Pharyngeal arch stage (phylotypic waist) | Vertebrate ancestor | ~500 Ma |
| Organogenesis | Specific vertebrate sublineage | ~400–300 Ma |
| Late fetal development | Mammalian ancestor | ~200–60 Ma |
| Birth | The specific species | Present |

**The developmental program is the
evolutionary lineage, compressed into
the timescale of a single organism's
early development.**

This is not ontogeny literally recapitulating

## PART III — GASTRULOIDS:
## THE TECHNOLOGY THAT ALREADY DOES THIS

### 3.1 What Gastruloids Are

A gastruloid is a three-dimensional
self-organizing structure grown from
pluripotent stem cells in suspension
culture, without a uterus, without
maternal signals, and without the
extraembryonic tissues that would
normally drive development forward
into organogenesis.

Gastruloids were first systematically
developed around 2014–2017 and have
been refined extensively through 2024.

They self-organize through:
- The equivalent of blastulation
- The establishment of a primary
  anterior-posterior axis (by Wnt
  signaling, exactly as in normal
  embryos)
- The beginning of germ layer
  specification

They arrest at approximately the
equivalent of the gastrula/early
neurula stage in normal development.

**Current gastruloids are, in
developmental and attractor geometry
terms, artificial organisms that
stably occupy the early gastrula
to early neurula attractor.**

This is one to two attractor grades
above the Dickinsonia target.
The Dickinsonia target is below the
neurula. The current gastruloid
technology slightly overshoots it
into early neural specification.

The gap between current gastruloid
technology and the Ediacaran construction
target is approximately:

**"Arrest one step earlier and maintain
the attractor without allowing Wnt
signaling to proceed into neural
tube specification."**

This is a calibration problem.
It is defined and solvable.

### 3.2 What Current Gastruloid
### Technology Demonstrates

As of 2024, gastruloid research
(Nature Cell Biology, 2024;
multiple groups at Hubrecht Institute,
Helmholtz Munich, Cambridge) has
demonstrated:

- Stem cells will spontaneously
  organize into a gastrula-equivalent
  structure with defined axes and
  germ layers without any external
  template
- The axis specification is driven
  by intrinsic Wnt signaling —
  no maternal factors required
- The process is reproducible
  and quantifiable
- Human gastruloids can be produced
  from human stem cells and reach
  developmental stages equivalent
  to approximately 3–4 weeks of
  normal human embryonic development
- The structures do not develop into
  fetuses — they arrest because the
  complex signaling environment of
  the uterus and extraembryonic
  tissues is absent

**The arrest is not a failure of the
technology. It is the technology
confirming the attractor framework:
without the signals that drive
development FORWARD to the next
attractor state, the system stabilizes
at the attractor it has reached.**

This is exactly the attractor geometry
prediction. Remove the forward-driving
signal. The system stabilizes at the
current attractor. That attractor
corresponds to an evolutionarily ancient
organizational grade.

**Step 2: Define the Arrest Condition**

The Dickinsonia grade requires:
- Wnt-specified bilateral axis: ACTIVE
- Ectoderm/endoderm fate distinction: ACTIVE

- Neural specification: INHIBITED

maintain Wnt axis specification while

- Wnt pathway: active at moderate level

- Nodal/Activin signaling: inhibited

- FGF signaling: reduced (prevents
