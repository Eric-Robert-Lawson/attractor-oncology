## PART I — WHAT CHANGED AND WHY

### 1.1 The Original Protocol Statement

The original Dickinsonia Protocol
(THE_EDIACARAN_EXPERIMENT_IS_READY.md,
also Section 8 of the pre-registration)
specified the following intervention:

```
Day 1:
  Add CHIR99021 (1–3 μM) — activate Wnt/β-catenin
  Add SB505124 (10 μM) — inhibit Nodal/Activin
  Add DMH1 (1 μM) — inhibit BMP
```

The prediction attached to this protocol:
Wnt activation alone would produce
bilateral Ediacaran-grade morphology
(Dickinsonia-grade bilateral symmetry).

### 1.2 What the Literature Establishes

Integration of the Wnt/BMP literature
(Nature Reviews Genetics 2024; PLOS Genetics 2023;
Discover Developmental Biology 2024; eLife 2025)
into the attractor geometry framework produces
the following finding:

**Wnt activation alone does not produce
bilateral symmetry.**

It produces Axis 1 — the anterior-posterior
or oral-aboral axis. This is a single-axis
attractor. It is the pre-bilateral Ediacaran
grade, not the Dickinsonia grade.

Bilateral symmetry requires two orthogonal
axes simultaneously:

```
Axis 1: Wnt/β-catenin — anterior-posterior
Axis 2: BMP/ADMP — dorsal-ventral, orthogonal to Axis 1
```

These axes are not sequential refinements.
They are independent dimensions.
The transition from Axis 1 alone to
Axis 1 + Axis 2 is a dimensional expansion
of the body plan attractor space —
structurally identical to the
FECA→LECA mitochondrial expansion
(established in the companion LUCA paper,
DOI: https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.18988844)
but operating in morphological attractor space
rather than metabolic attractor space.

**The consequence for the protocol:**

The original step 3 — `Add DMH1 (1 μM) to inhibit BMP` —
was inverted.

BMP must be **activated orthogonally** to establish Axis 2,
not inhibited.

BMP inhibition maintains the organism in the
pre-bilateral Ediacaran attractor (E1).
BMP activation orthogonal to the established
Wnt axis drives the transition to the bilateral
Ediacaran attractor (E2) — the Dickinsonia grade.

### 1.3 The Correction Is Geometric, Not Empirical

This correction was not triggered by a failed
experiment. No experiment has been run.

The correction was produced by integrating
the Wnt/BMP literature into the attractor
geometry framework, which revealed that
bilateral symmetry is a two-axis geometric
structure, and that reaching it therefore
requires two independent orthogonal
interventions — one per axis.

The attractor was correctly identified in
the original protocol.
The coordinate system of the attractor
was under-specified.
This document states the full coordinate
system.

## PART II — THE GEOMETRY STATED PRECISELY

### 2.1 The Ediacaran Attractor Has Internal Structure

The original documents in this folder treated
the Ediacaran attractor as a single target.
It has two stages:

```
E1 — Pre-Bilateral Ediacaran Attractor
  One axis: Wnt/β-catenin (oral-aboral or AP)
  Symmetry: radial or disc
  Body plan: sheet or disc morphology
  Single-axis polarity confirmed
  Intervention: Wnt activation only (Step 1)
  Evolutionary example: Trichoplax-adjacent grade,
    pre-Dickinsonia disc organisms
  
        ↓  [BMP ACTIVATION, ORTHOGONAL TO WNT AXIS]
        ↓  [This is the dimensional expansion event]
  
E2 — Bilateral Ediacaran Attractor (Dickinsonia-grade)
  Two axes: Wnt (AP) + BMP (DV, orthogonal)
  Symmetry: bilateral
  Body plan: elongated bilateral form
  Both axes independently confirmed
  Intervention: Wnt activation + orthogonal BMP (Steps 1+2)
  Evolutionary example: Dickinsonia costata, Kimberella (~558 Ma)
```

E1 is an independently observable and falsifiable result.
E2 is the Dickinsonia result.
Both are now pre-registered (Zenodo Amendment A1,
DOI: https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.18989573).

### 2.2 Why the Original Protocol Accessed E1, Not E2

The original protocol inhibited BMP (DMH1, 1 μM).
BMP inhibition prevents Axis 2 from forming.
Therefore the original protocol would have
arrested the organism in E1 — the pre-bilateral
Ediacaran disc attractor — not E2 (Dickinsonia-grade).

E1 is still a valid and publishable result.
It is an independently accessible Ediacaran attractor.
It is not the bilateral Dickinsonia result.

The original statement that the experiment
would produce "bilateral symmetry" was
geometrically under-specified. Bilateral symmetry
requires two axes. The original protocol only
established one.

The LUCA paper establishes that the FECA→LECA
transition was a dimensional expansion of the

```

E1→E2:         bilateral axis dimension opens
```

Both events:

### 3.2 The Amended Two-Step Intervention

The intervention is now explicitly two steps,
not one. They are sequential. Step 2 cannot
precede Step 1. Step 1 result must be
confirmed before Step 2 is initiated.

---

**STEP 1 — Axis 1 Specification (Wnt activation)**

*Purpose: Establish the anterior-posterior axis.
Access E1 (pre-bilateral Ediacaran attractor).*

| Reagent | Concentration | Function |
|---|---|---|
| CHIR99021 (GSK-3β inhibitor) | 5–10 μM | Activates Wnt/β-catenin; establishes Axis 1 |
| SB505124 (Nodal/Activin inhibitor) | 10 μM | Prevents mesoderm induction |

*Alternative to CHIR99021: Recombinant Wnt3a protein
at 100–300 ng/mL.*

Incubate 24 h at 20°C, <1% O₂.

**Step 1 Confirmation (required before Step 2):**

Confirm Axis 1 by immunostaining for
β-catenin nuclear localisation asymmetry:
one pole enriched relative to the other.
Photograph and record. This is the E1 result.

**The E1 result is publishable independently
of whether Step 2 succeeds.**

---

**STEP 2 — Axis 2 Specification (BMP activation, orthogonal)**

*Purpose: Establish the dorsal-ventral axis
orthogonal to the confirmed Wnt axis.
Access E2 (bilateral Ediacaran attractor —
Dickinsonia-grade).*

*Only begin Step 2 after Step 1 is confirmed.*

| Reagent | Application method | Function |
|---|---|---|
| Recombinant BMP2 or BMP4 (50–200 ng/mL) | Heparin acrylic bead, placed at one lateral margin perpendicular to confirmed β-catenin gradient | Activates BMP; establishes Axis 2 |
| Chordin or Noggin (50–100 ng/mL) | Bead or bath, applied at opposite lateral margin | Establishes DV gradient (high BMP one side, low BMP the other) |

*The BMP bead must be placed perpendicular to the
established Wnt/β-catenin axis. This orthogonality
is not optional — it is the geometric requirement
for bilateral symmetry. Random or radial BMP
distribution will not produce bilateral organisation.*

Incubate further 48–72 h at 20°C, <1% O₂.
Observe every 12 h.

## PART IV — THE NEGATIVE CONTROL PREDICTION

The two-axis attractor model generates
a precise negative control that is itself
a pre-registered prediction
(Prediction A1-P4 in the Zenodo amendment,
DOI: https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.18989573):

**After Step 1 confirmation (E1 established),
add a BMP inhibitor (LDN-193189 ~$40,
or dorsomorphin) instead of Step 2.**

**Predicted result:**
Organism remains in E1 indefinitely.
No bilateral organisation.
No elongation.
No DV axis.

If BMP inhibition locks the organism in E1
after Axis 1 is established, this confirms:

1. E1 is a stable attractor, not a transient state
2. The E1→E2 transition requires BMP — it does not
   happen spontaneously
3. The dimensional expansion is gated on the
   specific BMP deployment event

**Failure condition:**
BMP inhibition after Step 1 does not prevent
bilateral organisation. Bilateral morphology
emerges without BMP regardless.
This would falsify the two-axis attractor model
(Falsifiability Condition F8 in the Zenodo amendment).
