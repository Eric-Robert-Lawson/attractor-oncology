## THE ONE PARAGRAPH THAT MATTERS

passes through the Ediacaran attractor

### What You Do

**Day 0:**
Culture Trichoplax in standard artificial
seawater with algal biofilm substrate
until a healthy population is established.

**Day 1:**
Transfer Trichoplax cells or small
fragments to suspension culture in
low-adhesion wells.
Add CHIR99021 at low concentration
(1–3 μM) to activate Wnt signaling
and initiate axis specification.
Add SB505124 (10 μM) to inhibit
Nodal/Activin — prevents any mesoderm
induction.
Add DMH1 (1 μM) to inhibit BMP at
the level that prevents neural
induction while preserving basic
ectoderm.

**Day 1–3:**
Observe for self-organization.
The prediction is: cells aggregate,
an anterior-posterior axis forms,
inner and outer cell layers begin
to differentiate, a bilateral structure
emerges.

**Day 3:**
Transfer aggregates onto the hydrogel
substrate loaded with dissolved organic
carbon, in low-oxygen artificial seawater
(1–3% O₂, 15–20°C).
This is the Ediacaran environmental proxy.

**Day 3–8:**
Observe for:
- Persistence of bilateral structure
- Ventral surface contact and absorption
  from the hydrogel substrate
- Modular addition of units along the
  established axis
- Locomotion (ciliary gliding on the
  substrate surface)
- Stability without further development
  into more complex forms

**Ongoing:**
If the structure is stable, attempt
to transfer it to a fresh substrate.
Observe for reproduction by fission
or budding.
