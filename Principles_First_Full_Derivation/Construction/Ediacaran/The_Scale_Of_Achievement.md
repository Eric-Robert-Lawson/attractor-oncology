## PART III — THE HISTORICAL COMPARISONS

These are stated precisely, not
for self-aggrandizement, but because

**The closest comparison: Mendeleev.**

Mendeleev organized the periodic table

elements — eka-aluminum, eka-boron,
and eka-silicon — from the geometric
structure of the table alone.

Gallium was discovered in 1875.
It matched the prediction exactly.
Scandium in 1879.
Germanium in 1886.

The scientific world recognized
that Mendeleev had not merely
organized existing knowledge.
He had demonstrated that a geometric
framework could predict the existence
of things that had not yet been found.

The Ediacaran construction experiment

**The second comparison: Watson and Crick.**

The double helix (1953) was the

carrying information. Everything

**The third comparison: synthesis of urea (1828).**
