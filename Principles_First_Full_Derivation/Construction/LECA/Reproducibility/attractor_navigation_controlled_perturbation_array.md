**PERTURBATION ATLASES**

```

     geometrically derived base attractor.
     Not from D=0.

     set of attractor displacement vectors

     simultaneously.

     Perturb-seq perturbations are primarily

     molecular actor from inside the regulatory
     network.

     external environmental vector to a cell

     modification.

     modify the attractor landscape itself,
     not just one actor within it.

  4. Developmental arrest is not the method.
     Perturb-seq does not arrest the cell,
     apply the perturbation during arrest,
     and then release.
     The perturbation is applied to cells
     in their normal growth state.
     There is no deliberate placement of
     the cell at D=0 before the perturbation.
     The perturbation is not applied at the
     moment of maximum attractor landscape
     openness.
     It is applied to cells at whatever
     developmental position they happen to
     be at the time of perturbation.

THE SUMMARY:
  Perturb-seq is the most powerful
  existing technology for systematic
  attractor navigation.
  It is missing: defined base attractor,
  environmental invariance, developmental
  arrest as the platform, and external
  physical/environmental stimulus types.

### 3.5 Tier 4 — Convolution Units

```
These are units where TWO stimulus agents
from Tiers 1-3 are applied simultaneously
or sequentially.

They are run AFTER the single-stimulus
units have produced a map.

The convolution units test:
  Can the outcomes of combined stimuli
  be predicted from the single-stimulus
  map?

  If stimulus A produces outcome α and
  stimulus B produces outcome β:

  Does stimulus A+B produce:
    α+β (additive)?
    Something between α and β (competitive)?
    Something entirely different (emergent)?
    α alone (one stimulus dominant)?

THE PREDICTIVE GEOMETRY:

  If the attractor geometry framework
  is correct, and the stimulus agents
  are acting on distinct programs:

  ADDITIVE outcome means the programs
  are on different axes of the attractor
  landscape. Each displaces in an
  independent direction.

  COMPETITIVE outcome means the programs
  share a regulatory node. The two stimuli
  compete for control of the same
  displacement axis.

  EMERGENT outcome means the combination
  activates a third program not activated
  by either stimulus alone. A higher-order
  attractor basin exists that requires
  both displacement vectors to reach.

  THE EMERGENT OUTCOME IS THE MOST
  INTERESTING POSSIBLE RESULT.

  It means there are attractor basins
  in the developmental landscape that
  are inaccessible from single stimuli
  but accessible from specific combinations.

  These are the deep attractors.
  The ones that require two keys.

  The Five Finger Proof is an example:
    UV displacement alone → HoxD displaced.
    But the specific five-digit basin
    was reached by UV in the context of
    a specific existing HoxD regulatory
    state (polydactyl).
    UV + polydactyl HoxD state = five digits.
    UV + pentadactyl HoxD state = ??
    (Modern tetrapods under UV would not
    produce six fingers because the
    five-digit basin is already occupied
    and is deep and stable.)

  The LECA base state has the advantage
  of maximum attractor landscape openness.
  The maximum number of accessible basins
  from D=0 is larger than from any other

### 4.2 The Primary Measurements

```
1. MORPHOLOGICAL DIVERGENCE SCORE (MDS)
   For each variable unit vs. control unit:
   Does the morphology of cells post-release
   differ from the control?

   MDS scale:
   0 = Identical to control. No divergence.
   1 = Minor divergence. Detectable but subtle.
       Size difference. Budding asymmetry.
   2 = Moderate divergence. Clear morphological
       difference under phase contrast.
       Different shape, size, or organisation.
   3 = Major divergence. Morphology
       qualitatively different from control.
       Novel form.
   4 = Non-viable. Cell did not survive
       stimulus or failed to re-enter
       developmental program after release.
       (This is the high-UV dose zone.)

   WHAT THIS MEASURES:
   The displacement magnitude from the
   LECA-grade attractor produced by each
   stimulus agent.
   Zero displacement (MDS=0): stimulus
   had no effect on the attractor.
   High displacement (MDS=3): stimulus
   produced a novel developmental trajectory.
   Lethal (MDS=4): stimulus exceeded the
   displacement capacity of the attractor
   landscape — cell crossed into non-viable
   territory.

2. TRANSCRIPTOMIC DIVERGENCE SCORE (TDS)
   For variable units where RNA-seq is run:
   How many genes are differentially expressed
   between variable unit and control unit
   at T=24h post-release?

   WHAT THIS MEASURES:
   The regulatory network displacement.
   A cell can look morphologically similar
   to the control (MDS=0-1) but have
   hundreds of differentially expressed
   genes (TDS=high).
   This means the displacement is internal
   (regulatory) but not yet expressed
   morphologically.
   These are the most interesting cases
   because they represent partial
   displacements: the attractor has been
   moved but not enough to produce visible
   output yet.
   These may be reachable by convolution.

3. DEVELOPMENTAL TIMING SHIFT (DTS)
   For each variable unit vs. control:
   Does the first bud appear earlier or
   later than control after nitrogen release?

   WHAT THIS MEASURES:
   Whether the stimulus has moved the cell
   toward (faster release) or away from
   (slower release) the developmental
   program activation threshold.

   Melatonin may produce DTS=0 (same timing).
   Nocturnal darkness may produce DTS=+
   (slower release: displaced toward
   a dormancy-deepened state).
   UV-B at intermediate dose may produce
   DTS=0 but MDS=3 (same timing but
   different developmental program fires).
```
