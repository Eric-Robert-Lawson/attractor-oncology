Developmental biology and evolutionary
biology are separate disciplines.

Developmental biologists know the zygote
is totipotent. They do not frame this
as "the zygote re-expresses the LECA
state."

Evolutionary biologists know all
eukaryotes share a common ancestor.
They do not frame this as "the common
ancestor is accessible via developmental
arrest."

The connection requires treating
development and evolution as the same
process at different timescales.
This is the Reproduction Theorem.
The Reproduction Theorem was not a
theorem before it was derived here.
It was an implicit assumption in
evo-devo but never stated as a
theorem with precise geometric
consequences for experimental access
to ancestral states.

### Barrier 2: Inverted Problem Framing

In every developmental biology
laboratory, developmental arrest is
a failure mode to be prevented.

The arrested embryo goes in the waste.
The goal is developmental progression.

Inverting this — treating developmental
arrest as the target state, as the
specific organism you are trying to
produce — requires a reason to invert.

The reason is the framework.
Without the framework, there is no
reason to look for the LECA in an
arrested zygote.
With the framework, the arrested
zygote IS the LECA and the failure
case is the success case.
