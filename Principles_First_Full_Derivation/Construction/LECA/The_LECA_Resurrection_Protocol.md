## PART I — WHAT THE LECA WAS

### 1.1 The Organism

The Last Eukaryotic Common Ancestor
existed approximately 1.5 billion
years ago.

Based on conserved gene presence
across all eukaryotic kingdoms,
its characteristics are derivable:

**Physical structure:**
- Single cell
- Approximately 10–20 micrometers
  in diameter
- Flexible plasma membrane
- No cell wall (unlike modern plants
  and fungi)
- One or more flagella for motility
- Heterotrophic: feeding on organic
  matter or other cells
- Aquatic: freshwater or shallow
  marine environment

**Internal organization:**
Already fully eukaryotic.
Already complex.
This is critical.

- Nucleus with nuclear envelope
  and nuclear pores
- Mitochondria — already integrated
  via endosymbiosis with an
  alphaproteobacterium ancestor
  (the endosymbiosis predates LECA)
- Endoplasmic reticulum
- Golgi apparatus
- Cytoskeleton: actin and tubulin
  both present
- Flagella: tubulin-based, dynein-
  powered, structurally identical
  to modern eukaryotic flagella
- Lysosomes and vacuoles
- Fully functional cell cycle:
  cyclins, CDKs, all conserved
- Sexual reproduction machinery:
  meiosis core proteins (Spo11,
  DMC1, RAD51, SYCP, cohesins)
  all present and conserved

**Genome:**
Estimated 4,000–6,000 protein-coding
genes.
All of these genes are still present
in every modern eukaryote.
They are the conserved LECA core —
the 15–25% of modern eukaryotic
genomes that is orthologous across
the entire eukaryotic tree of life.

**The LECA was not a simple organism.**
It was a sophisticated single cell
with the full eukaryotic toolkit.
What it lacked was multicellular
organization. Every column added
after LECA — tissue identity, organ
identity, neural identity, vascular
identity — was absent.
The single cell was the entirety
of the organism.
Row 3 at the organism scale was
one cell.

## PART IV — THE EXPERIMENTAL PROTOCOL

### 4.1 The Conceptual Protocol

**Principle:**
Arrest the developmental program of
any eukaryotic cell before the first
multicellular organizational step.
Maintain the arrested single-cell
state in conditions that support
LECA-grade physiology.
Observe whether the arrested cell
is morphologically consistent with
the derived LECA characteristics.

**What "arrest before multicellular
organization" means:**

In animals:
The zygote undergoes its first cleavage
divisions. These early divisions do
NOT constitute multicellular organization
— they are cytoplasmic partitioning
of a single cell's contents. True
multicellular organization begins at
compaction (morula stage in mammals)
when cells begin to establish distinct
identities and adhesion programs.

Arrest target: before compaction.
At the 1-cell to 4-cell stage.
The cell is in the LECA-grade
single-cell attractor.

In plants:
The zygote undergoes its first asymmetric
division, which immediately begins to
establish the embryonic axis.
This first division IS the first step
away from the LECA attractor.

Arrest target: the zygote itself,
before first division.
This is achievable by inhibiting
the asymmetric division program
while maintaining zygote viability.

In fungi:
The germinating spore begins hyphal
tube formation — this is the first
multicellular step.
Arrest target: the spore before
germination pore formation.
Inhibit the germination program.
Maintain the spore in the single-cell
arrested state.

### 4.2 The Intervention Logic

**In animals (most accessible):**

Cell-cycle arrest of the zygote
at G1 or G2/M boundary prevents
the division program from initiating
multicellular organization.

Tools:
- CDK1 inhibitor (RO-3306): arrests
  at G2/M boundary
- Aphidicolin: arrests DNA replication
- Low temperature (4°C): slows but
  does not stop all processes
- Removal of calcium signaling:
  disrupts compaction program

The arrested zygote maintains:
- Nuclear integrity
- Mitochondrial function
- Flagellar capacity (if present)
- Metabolic activity at LECA-grade
  level

**In plants (seed):**

Seed dormancy is the plant's native
developmental arrest mechanism.
The dormant seed embryo is arrested
at an early developmental stage.
Deeper arrest — to the zygote stage —
requires preventing the first
asymmetric division.

Tools:
- Auxin transport inhibitors
  (NPA: N-1-naphthylphthalamic acid):
  disrupt the auxin gradient that
  drives asymmetric division
- PIN protein inhibitors: same effect
- Cytokinin manipulation: disrupts
  the balance that drives first division

**In fungi (most technically simple):**

Fungal spores are already arrested
in a single-cell state. The germination
program must be suppressed to maintain
this arrest.

Tools:
- Germination inhibitors (fatty acids,
  specific small molecules)
- Nutrient deprivation maintained
  below germination threshold
- Temperature control

**The fungal experiment is technically
the simplest starting point.**

A fungal spore maintained in
suppressed-germination conditions
IS the LECA-grade attractor, with
minimal intervention required.

### 4.3 The Environmental Conditions

The arrested cell requires conditions
that support LECA-grade single-cell
physiology:

**Medium composition:**
- Aquatic base (sterile distilled water
  or minimal salt solution)
- Mineral salts approximating
  Precambrian ocean chemistry:
  Na+, K+, Mg2+, Ca2+, Fe2+, PO43-
- Organic carbon source for
  heterotrophic maintenance
  (glucose or acetate at low
  concentration)
- No complex multicellular growth
  factors (no EGF, no auxin, no
  gibberellin — these activate
  downstream programs)
- pH: 6.5–7.5 (consistent with
  early ocean estimates)
- Temperature: 20–25°C
- Anaerobic to microaerophilic
  (LECA predates the Great Oxidation
  Event's full atmospheric effect)

**Container:**
Standard culture dish or microfluidic
chamber.

**Morphological validation (primary):**
The arrested cell displays:
- Single-cell form
- Nucleus visible under light
  microscopy
- Diameter 10–30 micrometers
