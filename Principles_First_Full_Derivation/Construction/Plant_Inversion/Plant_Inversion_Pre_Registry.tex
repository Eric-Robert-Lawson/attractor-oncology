\section{Background and Theoretical Basis}

\subsection{The Standard Model and Its Incompleteness}

Contemporary biology operates on the model:
\textbf{Genome $\rightarrow$ Organism.}
The genome is treated as the blueprint.
To change the organism, one changes the genome
(CRISPR, directed evolution, synthetic biology).

This model is incomplete.

The complete model is:
\textbf{Genome $+$ Coherence Gradient Field $\rightarrow$ Organism.}

The genome contains \textit{build capacity} ---
the capacity to build every structure the organism has built
across its evolutionary history, and potentially more.
The coherence gradient field is the \textit{instruction set}
that tells the genome which programs to express, where,
and in which direction to navigate.

The organism that emerges is the resolution of the genome's
build capacity within the field's instruction set.

\subsection{Plant Architecture as Field Navigation}

Standard plant architecture --- root growing downward,
shoot growing upward --- is not genetically fixed.
It is the attractor the genome resolves to within
the standard coherence gradient field:
water and minerals below, light above, gravity present.

Each axis navigates toward its coherence gradient:
\begin{itemize}[itemsep=0.1em]
  \item \textbf{Root:} Navigates toward water
    (hydrotropism, MIZ1/MIZ2 pathway),
    minerals, darkness, and physical medium.
    Negative phototropism: avoids light.
    Positive gravitropism: grows downward.
  \item \textbf{Shoot:} Navigates toward light
    (phototropism, phototropin pathway),
    CO\textsubscript{2}, and warmth.
    Negative gravitropism: grows upward.
\end{itemize}

Standard soil architecture is the attractor produced when
all gradients point in the same direction:
root gradients (water, minerals, darkness) are downward;
shoot gradients (light) are upward.
Gravity confirms both directions.

\subsection{The Geometric Prediction}

If plant architecture is a field-navigated attractor
rather than a genetically fixed output, then:

\textbf{Inverting the coherence gradient field before
germination should produce an inverted architectural attractor.}

Specifically: placing water above the seed and light below
the seed --- before germination begins --- should cause
the root to navigate upward (toward water) and the shoot
to navigate downward (toward light).
Gravity alone cannot override two simultaneous coherence
gradient signals pointing in the opposite direction.

This prediction is geometrically derived from the attractor
framework \textit{before} the experiment is run.
The conditions are specified from the geometry.
The outcome is predicted from the conditions.
The experiment confirms or falsifies the geometry.

\subsection{Empirical Basis for Individual Mechanisms}

Both component mechanisms are independently established
in the published literature:

\begin{itemize}[itemsep=0.1em]
  \item \textbf{Hydrotropism overriding gravitropism:}
    Miao et al.\ (2025), \textit{PNAS}.
    Root hydrotropism in \textit{Arabidopsis} overrides
    gravitropism in the presence of a water gradient.
    The MIZ1 protein is the primary hydrotropism regulator.
    Upward root growth confirmed in moisture gradients
    pointing against gravity.
  \item \textbf{Root negative phototropism:}
    Galen et al.\ (2007), \textit{Functional Ecology}.
    Roots exhibit negative phototropism to blue and UV-A
    wavelengths (phototropin receptors present in roots).
    Root grows away from light source.
  \item \textbf{Shoot positive phototropism:}
    Christie (2007), \textit{Annual Review of Plant Biology}.
    Shoot phototropism well-established across all
    flowering plant species.
    Blue and UV-A light activates phototropin-1
    and phototropin-2, driving shoot toward light.
\end{itemize}

This pre-registration predicts that \textbf{combining}
these two independently established mechanisms ---
water above and light below --- produces full
architectural inversion (AIS\,=\,2), not merely
partial deflection of one axis.
This combination and its outcome is the novel
pre-specified prediction.

\subsection{Overview}

Four chambers are run simultaneously in the same environment.
All chambers use the same seed batch, same agar preparation,
same temperature, and same room.
The only variable between chambers is the

\subsection{Mineral Solution Composition}

Option A (from salts):
\begin{itemize}[itemsep=0.1em]
  \item 1\,mM KNO\textsubscript{3}
  \item 0.5\,mM KH\textsubscript{2}PO\textsubscript{4}
  \item 0.2\,mM MgSO\textsubscript{4}
  \item pH 5.8--6.5, adjusted with dilute KOH if needed
  \item Prepared in distilled water
\end{itemize}

Option B (simplified):
Commercially available mineral water (Evian, Volvic,
or equivalent) used undiluted.
Adequate ionic content for hydrotropism signalling.

\subsection{Chamber Assembly}

Each chamber consists of a transparent container
(clear plastic cup, 300--500\,mL, or equivalent glass jar)
constructed as follows:

\begin{enumerate}[itemsep=0.2em]
  \item Pour 5--8\,mL of 1\% agar (1\,g per 100\,mL water,
    boiled and cooled to $\sim$50°C) into the base.
    Allow to set on a level surface for 15\,minutes
    (confirmed solid by toothpick test).
  \item Place 3--5 seeds on the agar surface,
    spaced $\geq$1.5\,cm apart.
    Seeds sit ON the surface, not pressed in.
  \item For EXP and C4: add 30--40\,mL of mineral
    solution on top of the agar and seeds
    (the water gradient above the seeds).
  \item For EXP and C3: install UV-A LED beneath
    the chamber, pointing upward.
    Cover the top of the chamber with black cardboard,
    confirmed light-tight.
  \item For C4: top remains uncovered or covered with
    transparent film. Ambient room light from above.
  \item For C1: standard orientation, ambient light above,
    no water gradient above seeds, no LED.
\end{enumerate}

All chambers placed in the same room at 18--24°C.
All chambers remain stationary throughout the
experiment. Do not move chambers during observation.

\section{Success Criteria and Falsification}

\subsection{Confirmation of P-INVERSION-1}

P-INVERSION-1 is confirmed if and only if:
\begin{enumerate}[itemsep=0.1em]
  \item EXP: AIS\,=\,2 at T\,=\,72\,h
    \textbf{and} maintained at T\,=\,168\,h.
  \item C1: AIS\,=\,0 at all timepoints.
  \item C4: Root angle $>120°$ at T\,=\,72\,h.
    (The hydrotropism mechanism is confirmed
    functioning in this setup before EXP is interpreted.)
\end{enumerate}

P-INVERSION-1 is \textbf{not} confirmed if:
EXP achieves AIS\,=\,2 but C4 does not show
upward root ($>120°$).
In this case the inversion result is real but the
mechanism attributed (hydrotropism) is not confirmed
by this setup.
The result requires investigation before
the mechanism claim is made.

\subsection{Falsification Criteria}

The geometric prediction is falsified if:
\begin{itemize}[itemsep=0.1em]
  \item EXP: AIS\,=\,0 at T\,=\,72\,h
    (standard architecture despite inverted field).
    This would indicate gravity dominates over both
    combined coherence gradients at this field strength.
  \item EXP: AIS\,=\,1 at T\,=\,72\,h
    (partial inversion only).
    This is a partial result requiring further
    investigation of field strength.
    Not a confirmation of the full geometric prediction.
  \item EXP: AIS\,=\,$-1$ (both axes disoriented).
    This would indicate the combined field produced
    developmental confusion rather than inversion.
    A distinct and informative failure mode.
\end{itemize}

\section{Reproducibility Requirements}

For any replication of this experiment to be
considered valid and comparable to this pre-registration,
the following minimum documentation must be provided:

\begin{enumerate}[itemsep=0.2em]
  \item Species and seed source (cultivar, supplier).
  \item Seed viability test result
    (germination rate from pre-experiment test).
  \item Agar concentration (g per 100\,mL water).
  \item Water gradient composition
    (salt concentrations or mineral water brand).
  \item LED specification
    (wavelength range in nm, power in mW or W,
    distance from seed position in cm).
  \item Light blocking method and confirmation
    (material used, how light leak was checked).
  \item Temperature during experiment.
  \item All four chambers run simultaneously
    in the same environment.
  \item AIS scores at T\,=\,72\,h, T\,=\,120\,h,
    T\,=\,168\,h for all chambers.
  \item At least one photograph per chamber
    per timepoint, labelled with chamber ID,
    timepoint, and date.
\end{enumerate}
