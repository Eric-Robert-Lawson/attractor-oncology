## SECTION 3 — REALISTIC FAILURE BUDGET
## FOR A COMPLETE BEGINNER

```
Based on the 21 documented failure
modes in the operative protocol,
here is the realistic distribution
of what will go wrong and when.

RUN 1 — "THE ORIENTATION RUN"
What you are learning:
  How to pour agar without it gelling
  before you finish.
  How thin the agar needs to be.
  How to add water without dislodging seeds.
  How to actually seal the black cover.

Most likely failure:
  You pour the agar too thick (10mm
  instead of 5mm). Light from the LED
  below does not penetrate well.
  Shoot does not navigate downward
  because the light signal is weak.
  You get AIS = 0 or AIS = 1.
  The root may still go upward
  (water gradient works independently
  of agar thickness).
  Partial result. Not clean.

Second most likely failure:
  Overhead light leaks through the
  black cover. Shoot goes upward
  despite the LED below.
  You get AIS = 0 in EXP
  but C4 shows root going upward
  (confirming the water gradient
  is working).
  You identify the light leak and
  fix it for Run 2.

What Run 1 teaches you:
  Exactly which parameter failed.
  Whether it was the agar, the light,
  or the water gradient.
  The controls tell you this.
  C4 (water only) is your most
  important diagnostic tool.
  If C4 shows upward root:
  water gradient is working.
  Fix only the light component.
  If C4 shows downward root:
  water gradient is not working.
  Fix that first.

Estimated extra supplies needed: $0
(Run 1 is using the initial supplies)
Time lost: 7 days

RUN 2 — "THE LIGHT LEAK FIX RUN"
What you are fixing:
  Probably the black cover.
  Add more layers of black paper.
  Add black tape around all edges.
  Run the experiment at night or
  in a dark room for the first
  48 hours to eliminate all ambient
  light contamination during the
  critical germination window.

Most likely failure:
  Fungal contamination in the agar.
  By Run 2 you are handling seeds
  and agar more confidently but
  your sterile technique has gaps.
  White fluffy growth appears on
  the agar surface around or on the seeds.
  This is almost always a mold.
  It outcompetes the seedling for
  nutrients and physically blocks
  root growth.
  You may see germination begin
  and then stop as the fungus
  takes over.
  This does not mean the experiment
  failed for wrong scientific reasons.
  It means sterile technique needs work.

Second most likely failure:
  The mineral water layer evaporates
  faster than expected.
  After 72 hours the water layer
  is gone and the root, which was
  growing upward, now has no gradient
  to follow and begins correcting
  downward.
  You miss the observation window.

What Run 2 teaches you:
  How important the seal is for
  maintaining the water layer.
  How to improve sterile technique
  (seed sterilisation, ethanol wipe,
  not breathing directly over
  open agar).

Extra supplies needed: $2
(one more run worth of consumables)
Time lost: 7 more days

RUN 3 — "THE FIRST CLEAN ATTEMPT"
What you are fixing:
  Better black cover (doubled paper,
  all edges taped).
  Better sterile technique (bleach
  seed sterilisation now done properly,
  work surface wiped with 70% ethanol).
  Sealed chamber top to prevent
  evaporation (cling film over the
  top with a pinhole for gas exchange).

Most likely outcome:
  This is the run where you are
  likely to see a clean result.
  C4 (water above, no UV): root
  points upward. Confirmed.
  C1 (standard): root down, shoot up.
  Confirmed normal.
  EXP: root upward, shoot downward.
  AIS = 1 or AIS = 2.

Most likely remaining issue:
  The agar layer may be slightly
  too thick still, reducing the
  light signal from the LED below.
  AIS = 1 rather than AIS = 2
  (root goes up clearly, shoot
  is ambiguous — goes neither
  clearly down nor clearly up).

What to do:
  This is a partial result.
  It is still scientifically meaningful
  (root inversion confirmed = water
  gradient overrides gravity).
  The shoot component needs a stronger
  light signal.
  Move the LED 2cm closer to the seed.
  Or: use a slightly stronger LED.

Extra supplies needed: $2
Time: 7 more days

RUN 4 — "THE CLEAN RESULT"
What you are fixing:
  LED position (closer, or stronger).
  Agar thickness (now 3-4mm not 5-6mm,
  confirmed by measuring before
  seeds are placed).
  All previous fixes in place.

Most likely outcome:
  AIS = 2.
  Root points upward (toward mineral
  water above).
  Shoot points downward (toward
  UV LED below).
  The architecture is inverted.
  All controls confirm as expected.
  This is the result.
  Photograph everything.

Extra supplies needed: $2
Time: 7 more days

SUMMARY OF REALISTIC FAILURE BUDGET:
```
