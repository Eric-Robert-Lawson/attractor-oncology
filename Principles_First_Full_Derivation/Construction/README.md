# **CHRONOLOGY**

Fist came the LUCA, then the LECA, then the dickinsonia.

Brief understanding:

LUCA = not extinct, is ribosome, and cannot be derived from developmental arrest.

LECA = derivable, last and easiest derivable developmental arrest, ~200-400 a run. Look at inverse cost relationship to understand why something that is 1.5 billion years old is cheaper to bring back than something 540 million years old.

Dickinsonia = derivable, ediacaran life form, easy to develop but harder than LECA, look into the reproduction theorem in the folder.


I did the geometric derivation in following order: Dickinsonia, LECA, and LUCA.
