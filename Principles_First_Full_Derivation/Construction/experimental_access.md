## THE CORE REALIZATION

There exists, in the northwest Chicago metropolitan area,
a fully equipped BSL-1 community biology laboratory
that is open to any member of the public, requires
no credentials, no institutional affiliation, and
no formal biology training to access.

It has:
- Shaking incubators
- Light microscopes
- Laminar flow cabinets
- Standard molecular biology reagents
- An open membership model
- A community of practicing citizen scientists

The fungal LECA arrest experiment — the simplest and
most accessible experiment in the pre-registration
record (DOI: https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.18986790)
— requires exactly this equipment and nothing more.

This means the barrier between the pre-registered
prediction and the first experimental run is not
credentials. It is not institutional access. It is
not funding beyond what a single individual can
provide. It is a membership fee and a bus ride.

This document records that realization and its
operational consequences.

## PART II — THE EXPERIMENT THAT CAN BE RUN

### 2.1 Which Experiment

**The Fungal LECA Arrest Protocol**
as specified in the pre-registration record:
DOI: https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.18986790
Section 7 — The LECA Resurrection Protocol
Subsection 7.3 — Protocol: fungal version

This is the simplest, cheapest, most accessible,
and most immediately executable experiment in
the entire pre-registration document.

It requires no mammalian cells.
It requires no animal facility.
It requires no ethics committee approval.
It requires no institutional affiliation.
It requires no dangerous chemicals.

*Saccharomyces cerevisiae* (baker's yeast) is
classified BSL-1 — the lowest possible biosafety
level. It is the canonical model organism of
molecular biology precisely because it is safe,
robust, and forgiving for the experimenter.

---

### 2.2 What the Experiment Actually Is

**In plain language:**

Take yeast spores. Put them in water with minimal
salts and a small amount of glucose. Withhold
nitrogen. Keep temperature at 20–22°C (below the
germination threshold). Observe under microscope
at 2h, 6h, 24h, 48h, 72h.

If the arrested cell maintains:
- Single-cell form
- Visible nucleus (40× objective or higher)
- Diameter 10–30 μm
- No germ tube formation
- No multicellular adhesion
- Viable (metabolically active) for ≥72 hours

...then the prediction is satisfied at the
morphological level for the fungal version.

**This is not a complex experiment.**
This is a nutrient deprivation timecourse
with light microscopy readout.
It is genuinely one of the most accessible
wet biology experiments that exists.

## PART III — THE TRAINING PATHWAY

### 3.1 What Skills Are Needed

The fungal LECA protocol requires:
1. **Sterile technique** — how to work without
   contaminating cultures. This is learnable in
   one workshop session. ChiTownBio offers this.
2. **Light microscopy** — how to prepare slides,
   focus a compound microscope, image cells at
   40× and higher magnification. Learnable in
   one session.
3. **Culture preparation** — making minimal medium,
   adjusting pH, suspending spores. Follows a
   written protocol. No specialized knowledge
   required beyond the ability to follow
   instructions carefully.

**None of these skills require formal education.**
They require practice and attention to detail.
Both are available at ChiTownBio through their
workshop program.

---

### 3.2 Recommended Preparatory Steps at ChiTownBio

**Step 1:** Attend one introductory microbiology
or sterile technique workshop at ChiTownBio.
This establishes the sterile technique foundation.

**Step 2:** Attend one microscopy session or
workshop. Become comfortable with the microscopes
available. Practice imaging fixed yeast cells
(pre-prepared slides can be purchased for
orientation purposes).

**Step 3:** Introduce the project to the ChiTownBio
community. Not as a pitch for validation — as a
genuine description of what the experiment is and
why it is being run. Community labs thrive on
interesting projects. A principles-first de-
extinction prediction run by a citizen scientist
with a Zenodo DOI is exactly the kind of project
that energizes a community lab.

**Step 4:** Run the experiment.

---

### 3.3 The Documentation Standard

Every experimental run must be documented to the
standard of the pre-registration record.

**For each run, record:**
- Date and time of experiment
- Strain used (source, lot number)
- Medium composition (exact concentrations)
- Temperature (measured, not assumed)
- Arrest intervention applied
- Timepoints observed (2h, 6h, 24h, 48h, 72h)
- Microscopy images at each timepoint
  (photograph through eyepiece acceptable
  as a first approximation)
- Morphological assessment at each timepoint
  (cell diameter estimate, nucleus visibility,
  germ tube presence/absence)
- Outcome: arrested / germinated / ambiguous

**Why this matters:**
The pre-registration timestamp is locked.
Every experimental run conducted after that
timestamp is either a confirmation or a
falsification of a pre-specified prediction.
The documentation must be sufficient to
establish that the experiment was run faithfully
to the protocol and that the outcome was
observed honestly.

This is the standard that makes the result
publishable, citable, and resistant to the
objection that the data was adjusted after
the fact.

**This is a legitimate and documented pathway.**

It is called sponsored research.
Independent researchers fund experiments in
academic labs routinely.
The mechanism is a direct gift to the lab's
discretionary account or a formal sponsored
research agreement.

**What you are offering:**
- Full reagent and equipment costs paid
- Pre-registered prediction already locked
  with your name as originator
- No publication rights claimed beyond
  co-authorship acknowledgment of the prediction
- No institutional overhead expected
- No strings on the lab's interpretation
  of the results

**What the lab receives:**
- A fully funded experiment that costs
  them nothing
- First-mover access to a result that,
  if confirmed, has no precedent in biology
- A pre-registration record that protects
  both parties — the prediction was locked
  before the experiment, so confirmation
  cannot be disputed as post-hoc

**A lab that declines a fully funded, pre-registered,
$400 experiment with a locked Zenodo DOI

## PART V — THE EPISTEMIC POSITION

### 5.1 What Running This Experiment Establishes

If the fungal LECA arrest protocol produces
a cell that:
- Maintains single-cell form for ≥72 hours
- Is morphologically consistent with LECA-grade
  characteristics
- Does so under simple nutrient deprivation

...this establishes the first data point
consistent with the Inverse Accessibility Theorem
and the LECA Resurrection Protocol prediction.

It does not alone confirm the cross-kingdom
convergence prediction.
It does not alone prove the Reproduction Theorem.
It establishes that the arrest intervention works
as described and that the attractor state is
accessible from a fungal starting point.

**This is the first rung of the experimental ladder.
It is the rung that is reachable right now.**

---

### 5.2 What a Negative Result Establishes

If the fungal LECA protocol fails — if the
arrested cell is not viable, or immediately
progresses to germination despite the intervention,
or shows morphology inconsistent with LECA-grade
characteristics — this is a **Type B failure**
as defined in the falsifiability record.

It requires explicit acknowledgment.
It requires investigation of whether the failure
is a protocol failure (wrong strain, wrong medium,
wrong temperature) or a framework failure
(the prediction is wrong).

A protocol failure is not a framework failure.
A framework failure requires ruling out protocol
explanations first.

**The pre-registration record makes this
distinction explicit. Both outcomes advance
the epistemic position.**

---

### 5.3 On Having No Credentials

The absence of formal credentials is not a
weakness in this context. It is information.

The framework was derived by someone with
no formal biology training, no laboratory,
no institutional affiliation, and no prior
knowledge of the structures it predicted
(the ribosome's geometric role was derived
without prior knowledge of ribosome structure).

The derivation order — geometry first,
identification second, literature confirmation
third — is auditable in the pre-registration
record. The Zenodo DOI is the proof.

**The credential is the prediction record.**
**The prediction record is locked.**
**The prediction record is publicly citable.**
**No institution can revoke it.**
