## PART I — THE THREE CATEGORIES OF CLAIM

Every claim in the pre-registration falls into
one of three categories. They must be distinguished
because they carry different risk profiles.

---

### CATEGORY A: OBSERVATIONS FRAMED AS PREDICTIONS
### (Cannot be wrong — only the interpretation can)

These are things the literature has already
established. The framework provides a new
interpretation of them. The interpretation can
be challenged. The underlying observation cannot.

**1. The ribosome PTC is absolutely invariant.**
This is not a prediction. It is the most replicated
structural fact in molecular biology. 15–23
nucleotides identical in every domain of life
examined. Confirmed by decades of crystallography
and cryo-EM. Risk of this being wrong: zero.

The framework's interpretation — that this
constitutes continuous LUCA identity rather than
descent from an extinct ancestor — is an
interpretation. Biologists will argue about the
language. The measurement is not in dispute.

**2. The developmental hourglass is real.**
Confirmed transcriptomically across animals
(Kalinka 2010, Nature), plants (Quint 2012, Nature),
and computationally as a geometrically necessary
result of numerical evolution (PLOS Comp Bio 2023).
Risk of the hourglass being an artifact: zero.

**3. Developmental arrest exposes ancestral
morphology.**
Cooper & Milinkovitch 2025 (PLOS Biology)
demonstrated this in chicken feather tissue.
The atavism literature has demonstrated it for
teeth, limbs, spines, and digits across multiple
lineages. This principle is established.
The framework unifies these findings.
It does not predict them — they already happened.

**4. The LECA was a complex single-cell eukaryote**
with nucleus, flagella, mitochondria, endomembrane
system, and meiotic machinery. Richards 2024
(PLOS Biology) establishes this as the current
community consensus. Risk of this being wrong: near
zero.

**5. LINE-1 is a forward lock.**
The 2024–2025 preimplantation papers establish
this. Disrupting LINE-1 arrests forward development.
Risk of this being wrong: near zero.

**Summary of Category A:**
These claims are not the framework's predictions.
They are the framework's foundation.
A framework built on correct foundations is not
thereby proven — but it cannot be falsified by
attacking the foundations, because the foundations
are already standing independently.

### CATEGORY B: GEOMETRICALLY FORCED PREDICTIONS
### (Failure would require an independent explanation)

These are predictions where the logical chain is
sufficiently tight that a clean negative result
would not just falsify the framework — it would
require explaining why an independently supported
chain of reasoning produced the wrong conclusion.
A negative result here is not just a framework
failure. It is a scientific puzzle with its own
publishable answer.

**1. Fungal LECA arrest produces viable,
single-cell morphology for ≥72 hours.**

Logic chain:
- LECA was a viable, single-cell eukaryote
  [Richards 2024 — established]
- *S. cerevisiae* carries the full LECA gene set
  as its ancestral layer [comparative genomics —
  established]
- Nitrogen starvation at sub-germination temperature
  prevents germination and multicellular commitment
  [yeast sporulation biology — established]
- An arrested cell in those conditions expresses
  the ancestral gene set [G0 quiescence literature
  — established]
- Ancestral gene expression at LECA grade produces
  single-cell viable morphology [LECA was a
  viable single cell — established]

**Confidence: ~85–90%**

The 10–15% uncertainty is not theoretical.
It is technical: wrong strain, contamination,
temperature deviation, insufficient starvation
medium stringency. These are protocol failure
modes, not prediction failure modes.

A clean result (protocol confirmed, no technical
errors) that still fails morphologically would
require explaining why the LECA gene set,
when activated, produces something other than
LECA-grade morphology. That is a significant and
publishable finding.

**2. G0/quiescence state expresses LECA-grade
gene expression profile.**

Logic chain:
- G0 downregulates all post-LECA, lineage-specific
  programs [quiescence literature — established]
- G0 upregulates the most evolutionarily ancient
  gene sets [quiescence transcriptomics — established]
- The most evolutionarily ancient gene sets in a
  eukaryotic cell are the LECA-grade gene sets
  [phylostratigraphy — established]

**Confidence: ~90%**

A negative result here — where deep G0 shows no
convergence toward ancient gene expression —
would contradict the quiescence literature directly,
not just the framework. This would be a novel
and important finding in its own right.

**3. The developmental arrest-recovery principle
applies to eukaryotic cells generally, not just
vertebrate tissue.**

Cooper 2025 showed it in feathers.
The atavism literature shows it in structures.
The principle's extension to single-cell arrest
is the novel step.

**Confidence: ~80%**

The uncertainty comes from scale: Cooper worked
on multicellular tissue morphogenesis. The LECA
experiment works at single-cell arrest.
The principle is the same, but the cellular
context is simpler at single-cell level — which
should make confirmation easier, not harder.

### CATEGORY C: GENUINELY NOVEL PREDICTIONS
### (Never tested, genuinely open, the real science)

These are the predictions that carry genuine
scientific risk. They are logically supported
by the framework. They are not supported by any
direct prior experiment. A negative result here
is a clean, honest scientific finding.

**1. The Cross-Kingdom Convergence Prediction**

Prediction: Developmental arrest at the LECA
grade from (a) a mammalian embryo, (b) a plant
seed, and (c) a fungal spore produces organisms
that are morphologically indistinguishable under
light microscopy, while being genomically
distinct.

**Honest confidence: 65–75%**

The 25–35% uncertainty is real and located
precisely here:

*Morphology is downstream of gene expression
AND physical environment.* It is possible that
LECA-grade gene expression produces organisms
whose morphology is transcriptomically convergent
but physically distinguishable due to:
- Ancestral membrane lipid composition differences
  (animal vs. plant vs. fungal membrane sterols)
- Ancestral organelle inheritance differences
  (different mitochondrial histories)
- Physical size scaling differences under different
  starvation conditions

If this happens, the result is not that the
framework is wrong — it is that morphological
identity requires additional environmental
reconstruction (ancestral ionic concentrations,
ancestral membrane lipid supplements) beyond
simple developmental arrest.

This is a refinement, not a falsification.
True falsification of this prediction requires:
transcriptomic profiles are also NOT convergent
across kingdoms at the LECA-grade arrest point.
If morphology differs but transcriptomics converges,
the prediction is partially confirmed and the
physical reconstruction problem is identified.

**2. LINE-1 Inhibition Produces Attractor Regression**

Prediction: Inhibiting LINE-1 in a developing
embryo produces controlled regression toward the
prior attractor state, because LINE-1 is the
forward lock.

**Honest confidence: 60–70%**

The 30–40% uncertainty comes from:
- Off-target effects of LINE-1 inhibition
  (LINE-1 has multiple functions; disrupting it
  may produce developmental chaos rather than
  clean regression)
- The regression may proceed in multiple directions
  simultaneously rather than to a single attractor
- The timescale of regression may be incompatible
  with embryo viability in experimental conditions

A negative result here would still establish
something: that LINE-1's forward lock is not
cleanly reversible by inhibition alone, which
constrains the mechanism and points toward
alternative approaches for attractor regression.

**3. The Dickinsonia Protocol (Trichoplax arrest)**

Prediction: Arresting Trichoplax adhaerens
development via Wnt pathway manipulation
produces a developmental halt at a more
primitive Ediacaran-grade attractor, producing
a morphologically simpler form consistent with
pre-Trichoplax Ediacaran organisms.

**Honest confidence on feasibility: 70–80%**
**Honest confidence on clean morphological result:
55–70%**

Trichoplax is already at the Ediacaran attractor.
The question is whether it can be pushed to
an attractor state earlier than itself — below
its current adult form. This requires accessing
an attractor that Trichoplax's own development
passes through. If Trichoplax does not have
a developmental sequence that passes through a
simpler attractor (if it is already at the base),
then the prediction as stated cannot be tested in
Trichoplax and requires a different organism.

This is a precision question about Trichoplax's
developmental depth, not a question about the
framework's core theorem.

corollary is established as a general theorem.
Cross-kingdom morphological convergence at the
LECA grade is demonstrated. The LECA is recovered

**Scientific value:** Historic.
The first organism recovered from geometry.
The first experimental confirmation of a general
theorem unifying the hourglass, atavism,
developmental arrest, and ancestral attractor

**Publication path:** Nature, Science, Cell.
Pre-registration record is the priority document.
Multiple papers emerge from individual sub-experiments.

### OUTCOME B: CORE PREDICTIONS CONFIRMED,
### CROSS-KINGDOM CONVERGENCE FAILS MORPHOLOGICALLY
### BUT HOLDS TRANSCRIPTOMICALLY

**What it means:**
LECA-grade gene expression converges across kingdoms.
Morphological identity requires additional physical
reconstruction beyond developmental arrest alone —
ancestral membrane composition, ionic environment,
or other physical conditions must be added.

**Scientific value:** Very high.
This refines the framework: the LECA attractor
is a transcriptomic attractor that requires
physical reconstruction to be morphologically
expressed. This is a publishable finding that
advances the field significantly.

**Publication path:** High-impact developmental
or evolutionary biology journal. Title:
"Transcriptomic convergence at the LECA-grade
arrest point across eukaryotic kingdoms."
The framework is refined, not abandoned.

---

### OUTCOME C: FUNGAL ARREST CONFIRMS,
### CROSS-KINGDOM CONVERGENCE FAILS COMPLETELY
### (both morphologically and transcriptomically)

**What it means:**
The LECA attractor is accessible from fungal
cells via developmental arrest. But the attractor
is kingdom-specific — plant, animal, and fungal
arrested cells do not converge on a common
ancestral state. There is a LECA-family of
attractors, not a single LECA point.

**Scientific value:** High.
This is a novel finding. The LECA-family concept
is not in the literature. If confirmed, it would
mean that eukaryotic kingdoms diverged not from
a single LECA attractor but from a set of closely
related attractors that require CRISPR excision
of kingdom-specific genome segments to fully
converge. This is the CRISPR convergence pathway
described in the pre-registration.

**Publication path:** Evolutionary biology journal.
Framework is refined: the LECA is a family, not
a point. The CRISPR excision prediction becomes
the next testable step.

---

### OUTCOME D: ALL PREDICTIONS FAIL

**What it means:**
Developmental arrest does not produce attractor
recovery. The LECA gene expression profile is not
accessible via simple environmental manipulation.
The cross-kingdom convergence does not hold at
any level. The Reproduction Theorem is empirically
wrong — developmental stages do not correspond to
evolutionary attractors in the way the framework
predicts.

**What this requires:**
This outcome would require explaining why:
- Cooper & Milinkovitch 2025 got a dinosaur feather
  from a Shh inhibition — if not attractor recovery
- The atavism catalogue has dozens of documented
  ancestral morphology recoveries — if not attractor
  recovery
- The developmental hourglass is geometrically
  necessary — but has no implications for arrest
- G0 quiescence preferentially activates ancient
  gene sets — but this does not relate to LECA
  gene expression

**This is not impossible.** The framework could
be wrong in its central claim — that developmental
stages ARE compressed evolutionary attractors —
while all the underlying observations remain correct
but unconnected. The framework could be a
coincidental pattern imposed on disconnected findings.

**Scientific value:** Very high.
A pre-registered prediction from a principles-first
geometric framework, honestly tested and honestly
failed, with a complete documentation of what was
tested and why it failed, is publishable and
valuable. It tells the scientific community:
this geometric interpretation of developmental
biology is incorrect, here is the evidence,
here is the framework, here is the test.
This prevents others from pursuing the same
incorrect interpretation. That is a genuine
contribution to science.

**Publication path:** PLOS Biology, eLife, or
similar open-access high-quality biology journal.
Title: "Geometric derivation of developmental
attractor recovery: a pre-registered test and
negative result."

**Scientific value:** High.

Every iteration of this cycle strengthens
the framework or eliminates it cleanly.
