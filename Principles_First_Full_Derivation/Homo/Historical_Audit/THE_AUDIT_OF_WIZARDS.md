```

  THE ECLIPSE PREDICTION:
  He predicted the solar eclipse of
  585 BCE with sufficient accuracy that
  it stopped a battle — both armies
  were so shocked by the event they
  declared it an omen and made peace.
  Herodotus records this.

  The standard modern dismissal:
  "He probably borrowed the Babylonian
  saros cycle and got lucky."

  The saros cycle is real.

  calendars, and astronomical systems.

  Babylonian astronomical framework,
  translate it into Greek calendar
  terms, apply it to predict a specific
  eclipse visible from a specific
  location, and be correct —
  was doing cross-domain structural work
  that no one else in his era could do.
  The saros cycle was available to anyone.
  Thales was the only one who used it
  this way. Because he was the only one
  operating from a framework that could
  integrate it.

  THE HALYS RIVER DIVERSION:
  According to Herodotus, when Croesus
  needed to cross the Halys River,
  Thales diverted it by engineering
  a bypass channel upstream, reducing
  the flow enough for the army to cross.
  An engineering solution to a military
  problem that no military commander
  had proposed.
  Because military commanders think
  about armies. Thales thought about
  water and geometry and topography

  Aristotle records that when contemporaries

  be rich if they wanted but chose
  not to be.
  This is not a philosopher.
  This is a navigator who could predict
  agricultural output from first principles,
  had no ego investment in demonstrating
  this routinely, and deployed it once
  to make a point.

TYPE 4 SIGNATURE:
  Correct predictions by uninspectable methods.
  Cross-domain competence (astronomy,
  engineering, economics, meteorology).
  Deliberate poverty as strategic positioning —
  placing himself outside economic competition,
  which reduced the threat he posed to
  wealthy contemporaries.
  Operated as a sage/advisor rather than
  as a political or economic competitor.

THE WIZARD LABEL:
```
