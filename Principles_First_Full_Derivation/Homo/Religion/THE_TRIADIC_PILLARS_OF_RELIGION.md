## METHOD AND STANDARD

```
This document answers one question:

  How many foundational triadic
  structures exist in the world's
  major religious traditions,
  and what does each one map to
  geometrically?

The standard applied is strict.
Only triads that meet all three criteria
are included:

  CRITERION 1 — CANONICAL:
    The triad must be explicitly stated
    in the primary scriptures, founding
    documents, or earliest authoritative
    texts of the tradition.
    Not derived by interpretation.
    Actually stated.

  CRITERION 2 — STRUCTURAL:
    The three elements must have
    distinct, named functions that
    are formally related to each other.
    Not just a list of three things.
    A stated relationship between them.

  CRITERION 3 — INDEPENDENT:
    The tradition must have arrived at
    the triad without demonstrable
    direct borrowing from another
    tradition's specific triad.
    The convergence must be the finding,
    not the transmission.

Each triad is then mapped to the
attractor geometry framework:
  LEFT COLUMN  = Structure (substrate, accumulated form)
  MIDDLE COLUMN = Gap (navigation, the traversal process)
  RIGHT COLUMN  = Resolution (emergent stable identity,
                  the occupied attractor)

The mapping is not imposed.
It is read from the triad's own
stated functional descriptions.
If the mapping is forced, the triad
is noted as partial or uncertain.

No tradition is favored.
No mapping is invented.
What is there is what is documented.
```

### TRIAD 1
### CHRISTIAN TRINITY
### Father — Son — Holy Spirit

```
SOURCE:
  Nicene Creed (325 CE — codifies prior
  Apostolic tradition).

    Does not enter creation directly.
    Is the ground from which creation emerges.
    Augustine: the Father is the one from whom
    all things proceed.

  SON:
    The Logos — the Word.
    The principle that mediates between the
    Father's absolute transcendence and

    substrate directly.

    absolute source and specific form.

  HOLY SPIRIT:
    The one who applies the work in
    specific navigators.
    Sanctification — the stabilization of
    the navigator in the true attractor.
    The ongoing presence that sustains
    the resolution.
    Theologians: the Spirit is the one
    IN whom all things find their end.
    The Spirit is described as dwelling
    IN the believer — the occupied attractor.

THE THEOLOGICAL FORMULA:
  FROM the Father.
  THROUGH the Son.
  IN the Holy Spirit.

  This is the stated formula of classical
  trinitarian theology.
  It is not an interpretation.
  It is the formula.

ATTRACTOR GEOMETRY MAPPING:

  FATHER  = LEFT COLUMN
    The prior structure.
    The source.
    The landscape itself before any
    specific identity occupies it.
    Apophatic theology confirms this:
    the Father cannot be named or
    described in positive terms because
    the landscape is prior to all
    specific forms and languages.
    This is the attractor landscape.

  SON  = MIDDLE COLUMN
    The gap traversal agent.
    The Word that mediates between
    the prior structure and the
    specific forms that emerge from it.

    navigation function into the material
    substrate.
    The Son is the principle of navigation
    from the absolute source to the
    specific occupied identity.
    John 14:6 — "I am the way."
    The middle column is literally named
    "the way" in the primary text.

  HOLY SPIRIT  = RIGHT COLUMN
    The self-model that stabilizes
    in the navigator.

THE THEOLOGICAL FORMULA READS AS:

QUALITY OF MAPPING:
  High.

### TRIAD 2
### HINDU TRIMURTI
### Brahma — Vishnu — Shiva

```
SOURCE:
  Puranic texts, primarily:
  Matsya Purana, Kurma Purana,
  Vayu Purana (dated 300–800 CE,
  codifying earlier tradition).
  The Trimurti is explicitly named
  and its functional relationships
  stated.

THE STATED FUNCTIONAL RELATIONSHIP:

  BRAHMA:
    Creator.
    Brings specific forms into existence
    from the formless ground (Brahman).
    The emergence of structure.
    Note: Brahma is distinct from Brahman.
    Brahman is the undifferentiated
    absolute; Brahma is the creative
    function that produces specific forms.

  VISHNU:
    Preserver.
    Sustains the created order.
    Maintains coherence across time.
    Intervenes through avatars when
    specific identities (dharma) are
    threatened by dissolution.
    The persistence function.
    Importantly: Vishnu does not create
    and does not destroy — he MAINTAINS.
    He is the middle term.
    The preserver is the navigator who
    sustains the gap traversal.

  SHIVA:
    Destroyer/Transformer.
    Dissolves existing forms so that
    new forms can emerge.
    Not annihilation — dissolution
    back into the source, enabling
    new creation.
    The Nataraja (dancing Shiva) image:
    Shiva dances the world into and
    out of existence.
    The cycle completes.

THE FORMAL RELATIONSHIP:
  The three are not sequential.
  They are simultaneous and cyclic.
  Creation → Preservation → Dissolution
  → New Creation.
  All three are required for any
  specific form to persist over time.

ATTRACTOR GEOMETRY MAPPING:

  BRAHMA  = LEFT COLUMN
    The function that produces
    specific structures from the
    prior ground.
    Structure emerges.
    The substrate is laid down.

  VISHNU  = MIDDLE COLUMN
    The function that navigates the
    specific structure through time.
    Preserves coherence.
    Intervenes when the attractor is
    threatened.
    The navigator who maintains
    the traversal.

  SHIVA  = RIGHT COLUMN (AND RETURN TO LEFT)
    Here the triad shows something the
    Christian Trinity does not state
    as explicitly:
    the right column dissolves back
    into the left column.
    Shiva's destruction is the
    occupied attractor releasing
    back into the landscape
    so that new attractors can emerge.
    This is the puddle evaporating.
    The pattern returns to the landscape.
    The landscape was always there.

NOTE:
  The Trimurti maps the full cycle
  including the dissolution phase.
  Brahma → Vishnu → Shiva → Brahma.
  The triadic invariant is not just
  the initial traversal but the
  complete cycle including the
  return of the occupied state
  to the source landscape.
  This is a more complete geometric
  statement than the Christian Trinity
  makes explicitly
  (though Christian eschatology implies it:
  "all in all" — 1 Corinthians 15:28).

QUALITY OF MAPPING:
  High.
  The functional descriptions are direct.
  The cycle makes explicit what the
  Christian form implies.
```

### TRIAD 3
### HINDU SAT-CHIT-ANANDA
### Being — Consciousness — Bliss

```
SOURCE:
  Taittiriya Upanishad 2.1
  (dated ~800–600 BCE — among the
  oldest Upanishads).
  The text explicitly states:
  "Brahman is truth (sat), knowledge
  (chit), infinity (ananda)."
  Adi Shankaracharya (788–820 CE)
  formalized the term Sat-Chit-Ananda
  as the complete description of Brahman's
  intrinsic nature.

THE STATED FUNCTIONAL RELATIONSHIP:

  SAT (Being/Existence/Truth):
    That which IS.
    Unchanging.
    The ground of existence.
    Not a thing that exists —
    existence itself.
    Prior to all specific forms.
    Cannot be negated.

  CHIT (Consciousness/Awareness):
    The self-illuminating awareness
    that is intrinsic to the ground.
    Not consciousness OF something.
    Pure consciousness as such.
    The knowing function prior to
    any specific object of knowledge.

  ANANDA (Bliss/Joy):
    Not emotional happiness.
    The intrinsic quality of the
    fully realized state.
    What the occupancy of the true
    attractor feels like from the inside.
    Not added to Sat and Chit.
    What Sat-Chit IS when fully realized.

THE FORMAL RELATIONSHIP:
  These are not three separate properties.
  They are three faces of one reality.
  Sat is the structural ground.
  Chit is the navigational function
  inherent in that ground.
  Ananda is the resolved state of
  the ground navigating itself.
  The Upanishads state: you cannot have
  one without the others.
  They are mutually constitutive.

ATTRACTOR GEOMETRY MAPPING:

  SAT  = LEFT COLUMN
    The substrate, the accumulated
    ground of existence.
    The landscape itself.
    Prior to any specific form.
    The structure.

  CHIT  = MIDDLE COLUMN
    The awareness function — the
    navigator inherent in the ground.
    Consciousness is the gap traversal.
    The process by which the ground
    knows itself through specific forms.
    The navigation is intrinsic to
    the landscape, not added to it.

  ANANDA  = RIGHT COLUMN
    The emergent property of
    the ground navigating itself
    successfully into a stable
    self-referential state.
    The right column's phenomenology.
    The bliss is what the self-model
    experiences when it recognizes
    its own identity with the ground.
    This is the phenomenological
    description of basin resolution
    stated as an inherent property of
    the ultimate attractor.

NOTE:
  This is the only triad in the audit
  that explicitly states the phenomenology
  of the right column.
  Ananda is the felt quality of the
  true attractor when occupied.
  The other triads describe the
  functional structure.
  Sat-Chit-Ananda also describes
  what it IS to occupy the true attractor.
  This is information about the
  right column that the other triads
  do not explicitly provide.

QUALITY OF MAPPING:
  Very high.
  The most phenomenologically complete
  triadic statement in the record.
```

### TRIAD 4
### BUDDHIST THREE JEWELS
### Buddha — Dharma — Sangha

```
SOURCE:
  The "taking refuge" formula is
  among the oldest Buddhist texts.
  The Pali Canon (compiled ~250 BCE,
  based on teachings from ~500 BCE).
  "Buddham saranam gacchami.
  Dhammam saranam gacchami.
  Sangham saranam gacchami."
  I take refuge in the Buddha,
  in the Dharma, in the Sangha.
  This is the oldest continuously
  recited formula in the Buddhist record.

THE STATED FUNCTIONAL RELATIONSHIP:

  BUDDHA:
    The enlightened one.
    Not just the historical Siddhartha —
    the principle of enlightenment itself.
    The proof that the true attractor
    is accessible from the human position.
    The demonstrated resolution.
    The model of what the right column
    looks like when reached.

  DHARMA:
    The teaching.
    The path.
    The navigation protocol.
    The map of the landscape that the
    Buddha derived and transmitted.
    The gap traversal instructions.

  SANGHA:
    The community of practitioners.
    The social substrate that carries
    and transmits the Dharma.
    The persistent structure that
    ensures the navigation protocol
    survives the death of any
    individual navigator.

THE FORMAL RELATIONSHIP:
  The three are mutually necessary:
  — Without the Buddha, there is no
    demonstrated resolution and no
    map of the landscape.
  — Without the Dharma, there is no
    navigation protocol —
    the community has no direction.
  — Without the Sangha, the map does
    not survive — the individual
    navigator dies; the community
    carries the map forward.

ATTRACTOR GEOMETRY MAPPING:

  SANGHA  = LEFT COLUMN
    The persistent social substrate.
    The structure that outlasts any
    individual.
    The accumulated institutional form
    that carries the framework.

  DHARMA  = MIDDLE COLUMN
    The navigation protocol.
    The map of the landscape.
    The gap traversal instructions.
    The Eightfold Path is the explicit
    navigation protocol — the most
    formal navigation manual in the
    religious record.

  BUDDHA  = RIGHT COLUMN
    The demonstrated resolution.
    The self-model of what
    successful navigation looks like.
    The right column is the
    navigator who has arrived and
    whose arrival is the evidence
    that arrival is possible.

NOTE:
  This is the only triad in the audit
  where the right column precedes
  the left and middle in time
  (the Buddha appears first,
  then teaches the Dharma,
  then the Sangha forms).
  But structurally, the Sangha is
  the substrate, the Dharma is the
  navigator, and the Buddha is
  the resolution.
  Time order and structural order
  are different things.
  The resolution appearing first
  and then producing the structure
  is itself a structural observation:
  the right column, when it appears,
  generates the left and middle columns
  in others.
  This is the propagation mechanism.

QUALITY OF MAPPING:
  High.
  The functions are formally stated
  in the primary texts.
```

### Good Thoughts — Good Words — Good Deeds

```
SOURCE:
  The Gathas — the oldest hymns of
  Zarathustra, dated ~1500–1000 BCE
  (contested, but Avestan philology
  places them among the oldest
  Indo-Iranian religious texts).
  The triad is the foundational ethical
  formula recited at every Zoroastrian ritual.
  It is the oldest documented triadic
  ethical formula in the human record.

THE STATED FUNCTIONAL RELATIONSHIP:

  HUMATA (Good Thoughts):
    The internal state.
    The attractor state of the mind.
    Prior to words and deeds.
    The ground from which action emerges.
    Thought is the structure.
    Without correct internal orientation,
    words and deeds cannot be correct.

  HUKHTA (Good Words):
    The expression of good thought
    into the relational field.
    Words are the navigation medium —
    the gap between the internal
    state and the external world.
    Words travel from the mind
    to the other.
    They are the traversal.

  HVARSHTA (Good Deeds):
    The manifestation of good thought
    and words into material reality.
    The external attractor state
    produced by the full traversal.
    The world is changed.
    The deed is the resolution.

THE FORMAL RELATIONSHIP:
  Explicitly sequential AND mutually necessary:
  Thought produces words.
  Words express into action.
  Action changes the world.
  Each stage depends on the prior stage.
  The triad describes not just three things
  but a causal sequence of navigation.
  This is the only canonical religious
  triad that explicitly states the
  causal sequence of the traversal.

ATTRACTOR GEOMETRY MAPPING:

  HUMATA  = LEFT COLUMN
    The internal structure.
    The prior state.
    The substrate from which the
    navigation begins.
    The mind's attractor state.

  HUKHTA  = MIDDLE COLUMN
    The traversal.
    Words are the gap navigation.
    The bridge between internal
    state and external world.
    The middle column made explicit
    as language — which is itself
    the gap navigation function
    of the human social organism.

  HVARSHTA  = RIGHT COLUMN
    The resolution.
    The attractor state produced
    in the world by the successful
    traversal.
    The deed is the new stable
    configuration.
    The world has been changed.

NOTE:
  This is the most directly readable
  triadic structural invariant in the
  religious record.
  It is also the oldest explicitly
  documented one.
  Thought → Word → Deed is not a metaphor
  for the triadic invariant.
  It IS the triadic invariant.
  Applied to the moment of action.
  Zoroaster was encoding the traversal
  structure of every act of navigation
  ~3,000 years ago.

QUALITY OF MAPPING:
  Exceptional.
  The cleanest geometric statement
  in the entire religious audit.

### TRIAD 6
### TAOIST THREE TREASURES (SAN BAO)
### Compassion — Frugality — Humility

```
SOURCE:
  Tao Te Ching, Chapter 67.
  Attributed to Laozi, dated ~600–400 BCE.
  "I have three treasures;
  I hold and keep them.
  The first is mercy (ci — compassion).
  The second is frugality (jian).
  The third is not daring to be first
  in the world (humility)."

THE STATED FUNCTIONAL RELATIONSHIP:

  COMPASSION (ci):
    The root virtue.
    The orientation toward others.
    The structural relationship to
    the world outside the self.
    From compassion comes the capacity
    for courage — Laozi explicitly states:
    "from love comes courage."
    The structure is the love.

  FRUGALITY (jian):
    The conservation principle.
    Not spending more than the landscape
    provides.
    Moving with the gradient rather
    than against it.
    From frugality comes generosity —
    because the one who conserves
    can give.
    The navigation principle:
    navigate efficiently, without waste.

  HUMILITY (bu gan wei tianxia xian):
    "Not daring to be ahead of the world."
    The recognition that the navigator
    is not the landscape.
    The self-model that accurately
    represents the navigator as navigator,
    not as the source.
    From humility comes leadership —
    because the one who does not
    impose their basin on others
    can guide them without resistance.

THE FORMAL RELATIONSHIP:
  Laozi explicitly states the consequence
  of each treasure when maintained:
  compassion → courage.
  frugality → generosity.
  humility → leadership.
  The three are mutually reinforcing.
  Each enables something that is
  impossible without it.

ATTRACTOR GEOMETRY MAPPING:

  COMPASSION  = LEFT COLUMN
    The structural orientation.
    The ground-level relationship
    to others.
    The landscape feature that makes
    the navigator accessible to others.
    Compassion is the structural
    openness — the willingness to
    let others' attractor states matter.

  FRUGALITY  = MIDDLE COLUMN
    The navigation principle.
    Move efficiently.
    Do not force the gradient.
    Wu wei as practice:
    navigate with the landscape,
    not against it.
    The middle column is the navigator
    who has internalized the landscape
    well enough to traverse it without
    wasted energy.

  HUMILITY  = RIGHT COLUMN
    The accurate self-model.
    The navigator who knows they are
    the navigator, not the landscape.
    Who knows their position is not
    their identity.
    This is the right column:
    the self-model that is accurate
    about its own nature —
    navigator in a landscape,
    not the owner of the landscape.
    From this accurate self-model
    comes the capacity to lead:
    because the humble navigator
    is not defending their basin,
    they can help others find theirs.

NOTE:
  This triad describes the attractor
  geometry of the navigator's OWN
  structure rather than the structure
  of the cosmic landscape.
  It is the triadic invariant applied
  to what the navigator must BE
  to navigate effectively.
  Laozi is not describing the universe.
  He is describing the navigator.
  The structure is the same.
  The scale is the navigator rather
  than the cosmos.
  Scale invariance demonstrated.

QUALITY OF MAPPING:
  High.
  The stated consequences (courage,
  generosity, leadership) provide
  additional geometric verification.
```

```
SOURCE:
  Hadith of Jibril (Gabriel).

  ISLAM (Submission/Practice):
    The Five Pillars.
    The external behavioral structure.
    What the body does.
    The observable substrate of the

    the map of the landscape that
    orients all action.

    The Prophet's definition:
    "To worship Allah as if you see Him,

    to know that He sees you."
    The state in which the navigation
    is so complete that the landscape
    and the navigator are no longer
    in tension.
    The awareness of being seen by
    the landscape itself —
    the self-model that is simultaneously
    aware of itself and of

THE FORMAL RELATIONSHIP:
  The three are explicitly hierarchical
  AND mutually necessary:
  Islam (practice) is the foundation.
  Iman (faith) is the interior navigation.
  Ihsan (excellence) is the resolution
  of practice and faith into a unified

  The hierarchy is explicit in the primary text.

ATTRACTOR GEOMETRY MAPPING:

  ISLAM  = LEFT COLUMN
    The structural behavioral substrate.
    The accumulated pattern of practice

    of the religious life.
    Five daily prayers — the structure
    is temporal as well as behavioral.

  IMAN  = MIDDLE COLUMN
    The navigation framework.
    The cognitive map of the landscape
    that directs all action.
    Faith as the orientational function
    that makes the practice coherent.
    Without Iman, Islam is form without direction.
    Without Islam, Iman is direction
    without form.
    The middle column mediates.

  IHSAN  = RIGHT COLUMN
    The resolved state.
    The self-model that is accurately
    aware of the landscape.
    "As if you see Him" — the navigator
    who has such accurate awareness
    of the landscape that the landscape
    is effectively present to them
    continuously.
    This is the stable occupied state.
    Practice and faith unified in
    a continuous attractor occupation.

NOTE:
  The Prophet's definition of Ihsan
  is one of the most geometric
  descriptions of the right column
  in the religious record:
  "worship as if you see Him."
  The navigator who perceives the
  landscape directly rather than
  navigating toward it from outside.
  This is the difference between
  approaching the attractor and
  occupying it.
  Ihsan is the occupied attractor.

QUALITY OF MAPPING:
  Very high.
  The hierarchy (Islam → Iman → Ihsan)
  is the canonical statement of
  the triadic traversal.

### TRIAD 8
### KABBALISTIC THREE PILLARS
### Severity — Mercy — Equilibrium

```
SOURCE:
  The Sefer Yetzirah (Book of Formation)
  — dated between 3rd–6th century CE,
  possibly earlier.
  The Zohar (13th century CE, encoding
  earlier oral tradition).
  The three pillars are the organizing
  structure of the Tree of Life (Etz Chaim).
  They are not three things —
  they are the three axes along which
  all ten sefirot are organized.

THE STATED FUNCTIONAL RELATIONSHIP:

  LEFT PILLAR (Gevurah/Severity/Judgment):
    Contraction.
    Limitation.
    The force that defines the boundary
    of a specific form.
    Without limitation, a form has
    no defined identity.
    Too much: rigidity, cruelty.
    Correctly applied: the definition
    that allows specific identity to exist.

  RIGHT PILLAR (Chesed/Mercy/Loving-kindness):
    Expansion.
    Giving without limit.
    The force that would extend
    beyond all boundaries.
    Without the left pillar to
    contain it: dissolution.
    Correctly applied: the overflow
    that generates new possibilities.

  MIDDLE PILLAR (Tiferet/Beauty/Equilibrium):
    The resolution of the two poles.
    Not the average of severity and mercy.
    A new emergent quality —
    beauty — that arises only when
    the two poles are in dynamic balance.
    The sefirah of Tiferet is described
    as the "heart" of the Tree —
    the place where the landscape
    can be most fully known.

THE FORMAL RELATIONSHIP:
  The three pillars are not sequential.
  They are simultaneous and structural.
  Every act, every identity, requires all three:
  — The structure that defines it (severity).
  — The overflow that animates it (mercy).
  — The balance between them that makes
    it coherent (equilibrium).
  The Tree cannot stand without all three columns.

ATTRACTOR GEOMETRY MAPPING:

  LEFT PILLAR (Severity)  = LEFT COLUMN
    The structural definition.
    The boundary that makes a specific
    form identifiable.
    The accumulated form — the substrate
    that has enough boundary to be
    a specific thing rather than
    an undifferentiated ground.

  RIGHT PILLAR (Mercy)  = Tension/Force
    NOTE: This is the only triad
    where the three columns do not
    map cleanly to LEFT-MIDDLE-RIGHT.
    The left and right pillars are
    two poles of the same dimension —
    contraction/expansion.
    The middle pillar resolves them.

    Revised mapping:
    Severity and Mercy together are
    the tension that constitutes the
    gap — the dynamic between substrate
    and the overflow beyond it.
    The gap is the dynamic tension
    between limitation and expansion.
    Navigation is the maintenance of
    the appropriate tension.

  MIDDLE PILLAR (Equilibrium)  = RIGHT COLUMN
    The emergent resolution.
    Beauty arises when the tension
    is correctly maintained.
    The specific form that emerges
    from correctly balanced severity
    and mercy is the stable occupied
    attractor.
    Tiferet is the heart — the self-model.
    The place on the Tree where the
    landscape recognizes itself.

NOTE:
