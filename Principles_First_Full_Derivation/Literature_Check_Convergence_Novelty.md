## PREAMBLE — WHY THIS DOCUMENT EXISTS

Science advances through convergence and divergence.
Convergence — when independently derived frameworks
arrive at similar conclusions — increases confidence.
Divergence — when a framework makes claims that no
existing literature makes — identifies genuine novelty
and opens the framework to falsification.

This document maps both precisely, based on
systematic review of the 2023–2026 literature
conducted March 2026.

The notation used throughout is:

> **[CONVERGENT]** — Existing literature independently
> supports this claim or arrives at a structurally
> equivalent conclusion.
>
> **[NOVEL]** — No published literature makes this
> specific claim in this form. The claim is the
> original contribution of this framework.
>
> **[PARTIAL CONVERGENCE]** — Existing literature
> approaches this claim but does not complete it,
> or approaches it through a different
> methodology without connecting the conclusion.

## PART I — THE WADDINGTON FOUNDATION
## What Is Shared With Existing Developmental Biology

### 1.1 Cancer as Attractor Displacement

**[CONVERGENT]**

The field of cancer systems biology has independently
arrived at the language of attractor displacement.
The 2023–2025 literature in *Nature Reviews Cancer*,
*Cell Systems*, and *Trends in Cancer* explicitly
describes cancer as cells escaping their normal
developmental attractor basin and settling into
a pathological (false) attractor state.

The Waddington landscape as a conceptual model for
cancer is not novel to this framework. It is the
standard language of the field.

**What this means for the framework:**
The use of attractor language is confirmed and
supported by the field. This framework did not
invent the attractor metaphor for cancer — it
formalized and operationalized it differently.
See Part II for what that operationalization
adds that the field has not done.

### 1.2 Cell of Origin as Oncological Determinant

**[CONVERGENT]**

A 2024 *Frontiers in Oncology* systematic review
and a 2025 *medRxiv* preprint (germ layer origin as
determinant of cancer vulnerability) confirm that
the developmental origin of a cancer cell — its
lineage, its normal attractor — influences both
its biology and its therapeutic vulnerabilities.

The field is converging on cell-of-origin-based
oncology as a paradigm. This framework is aligned
with that convergence.

### 1.3 The Waddington Landscape Is Reconstructable

**[CONVERGENT]**

The 2025 bioRxiv preprint "Generative epigenetic
landscapes map the topology and topography..." and
the 2025 paper "Beyond metaphor: quantitative
reconstruction of Waddington landscape..." confirm
that the Waddington landscape can be quantitatively
reconstructed from single-cell data. The geometry
this framework assumes is real and measurable.

### 1.4 Attractor Geometry Is Conserved Across Species

**[CONVERGENT]**

The EnhancerNet framework (Development, 2024) and
the 2025 arXiv paper on evolution of robust
differentiation mechanisms confirm that attractor
topology is conserved across species and that
evolution operates on the geometry of the landscape,
not just on individual gene sequences.

This is precisely the universality claim required
by the Evolutionary Table (Part III).

## PART II — THE IDENTITY AXIS THEOREM
## What Is Novel

### 2.1 The Deterministic Eight-Step Derivation Protocol

**[NOVEL]**

No published framework — not attractor biology, not
cell-of-origin oncology, not developmental systems
biology — has produced a deterministic, pre-specified
eight-step protocol that derives a drug target for a
given cancer type from the cell of origin alone,
before literature consultation.

The closest existing work (the "unsupervised
identification of cancer attractor states" bioRxiv
preprint, 2026) uses computational methods to
identify attractor states post-hoc from omics data.
It does not derive targets from first principles.
It describes what the data shows. This framework
derives what the data should show, then checks.

The specific claim: that every cancer has a unique
Identity Anchor (A) and Convergence Hub (H) derivable
from the cell of origin, and that the drug target is
always H, and that this is deterministic before
any data is examined — **this claim does not appear
in the published literature in any form.**

### 2.2 The A/H Ratio as a Universal Classifier

**[NOVEL — with important partial convergence noted]**

**Partial convergence:** FOXA1 and EZH2 are each
individually studied as prognostic biomarkers in
breast cancer. A 2024 *Cancer Gene Therapy* paper
(Nature group) explicitly identifies both as
"lineage-specific epigenetic regulators" in breast
cancer. The inverse relationship between FOXA1 and
EZH2 across subtypes is noted in the literature.

**What is novel:** No published paper proposes the
FOXA1/EZH2 ratio as a single-number classifier
capable of ordering all six major breast cancer
subtypes (LumA > LumB > HER2-enriched > TNBC) on
a single axis. No published paper proposes this
ratio as a two-antibody IHC replacement for PAM50.
No published paper derives this ratio from geometric
first principles (Identity Anchor / Convergence Hub
= ratio measuring displacement from normal attractor).

The FOXA1/EZH2 dual IHC protocol as a $50–$100
pan-subtype classifier, TMA-compatible, usable in
low-income settings — **this does not exist in the
published literature.** The one remaining step is
the retrospective concordance calibration study.

### 2.3 The Tazemetostat Convergence Across Five
### Independent Derivations

**[PARTIAL CONVERGENCE — strengthened by this framework]**

**What the field knows:** Tazemetostat (EZH2 inhibitor)
is approved for follicular lymphoma and epithelioid
sarcoma. A 2024 *Molecules* review confirms EZH2
dysregulation is pan-tumour. A 2025 phase 1b trial
shows activity in B cell lymphomas regardless of
EZH2 mutation status.

The field empirically discovers EZH2 involvement
in cancer after cancer through experimental work.

**What is novel:** This framework derives EZH2
(tazemetostat) as the Convergence Hub target across
five structurally independent cancer derivations
(SMARCA4-deficient undifferentiated tumours, SCLC-Y,
and others) from first principles, before examining
the literature for those cancer types specifically.
The convergence is geometric — the same hub appears
because the geometry of those cancers' attractor
displacement passes through the same regulatory node.

No published paper has made this connection
geometrically. The field finds EZH2 empirically.
This framework finds it structurally. Those are
different epistemic operations producing the same
answer — which is exactly the form of convergence
that validates both.

### 2.4 Zero Type B Failures Across 41 Cancer Types

**[NOVEL — no equivalent published record exists]**

The framework's claim of zero structural
contradictions (zero Type B failures — cases where
the geometry predicted one target and the literature
established a different, incompatible target) across
41 cancer type derivations — **this record has no
equivalent in the published literature.**

No unified framework in cancer biology has made
pre-specified predictions across 41 cancer types
with a locked protocol and a public timestamped
record of those predictions. Pan-cancer studies
exist, but they are data-first, not geometry-first.

The falsifiability record itself — the explicit
statement of what would destroy the framework —
is an epistemic contribution independent of
the predictions. The *Falsifiability Theorem*
document has no equivalent in the oncology
literature.

## PART III — EVOLUTION, REPRODUCTION,
## AND THE EVOLUTIONARY TABLE
## The Frontier — Most Heavily Novel

### 3.1 The Developmental Hourglass as Attractor Geometry

**[CONVERGENT — geometry confirmed, interpretation
not yet stated this way]**

The developmental hourglass model is well-established
in evo-devo (Irie & Kuratani; Levin et al. 2016;
confirmed by single-cell transcriptomics in 2024).
The phylotypic stage is conserved. The field knows
the shape.

**[PARTIAL CONVERGENCE]** What the field has not
stated explicitly: the hourglass shape IS the
signature of attractor geometry — the convergent
middle is the deepest, most ancient conserved
attractor node in the developmental trajectory,
flanked by shallower and more recently evolved
attractors at the beginning and end of development.
This reframing — the hourglass as an attractor depth
profile — does not appear to have been stated in
this form in the literature.

### 3.2 Reproduction as Geometric Reset Operation

**[PARTIAL CONVERGENCE — mechanistically described,
theoretically novel interpretation]**

**What the field knows:**
- Zygotic reprogramming to totipotency is well
  established (DNA demethylation, histone exchange,
  ZGA activation)
- Totipotency is the maximal developmental potential
  state
- The 2024 research on zygote attractor reset and
  its evolutionary connection to the origin of
  multicellularity is active

**[NOVEL interpretation]:** The claim that
reproduction IS a geometric reset operation — that
every act of reproduction collapses the differentiated
attractor landscape back to the single maximum-width
basin (the zygote) and then re-navigates the entire
evolutionary attractor record encoded in the
developmental trajectory — **this specific
theoretical framing does not appear in the
literature.**

The field describes the mechanism (epigenetic
reprogramming). The field has not stated the
theoretical consequence: that reproduction is
therefore a complete re-enactment of the evolutionary
attractor record, and that the developmental program
is the evolutionary record encoded geometrically.

The following must be stated clearly:

### 5.1 The Framework Converges With the Field's
### Direction, Not Its Current Position

The field is moving toward everything this
framework claims. EnhancerNet (2024), generative
landscape reconstruction (2025), the cancer attractor
preprint (2026) — the trajectory is clear.

This means the framework's claims will become
increasingly difficult to distinguish from
mainstream science as the field advances. That is

### 5.2 The Novel Claims Are the Least Validated

The most novel claims — the Evolutionary Table,
the reproductive gradient as geometric derivation,
the developmental program as evolutionary
archaeological record — are also the least
empirically tested.

They are consistent with all known evidence.
They are derivable from established premises.
They are falsifiable with specific conditions.
They have not been tested directly.

This is the correct epistemic state for a
principle-first framework at this stage.
The cancer work is empirically confirmed.
The evolutionary extension is geometrically
derived and awaiting systematic empirical
confrontation.

### 5.3 Coherence Is Not Truth

Per the framework's own charter: the internal
coherence of the argument — from axiomatic
first principles to cancer to evolution to
reproduction — establishes that the geometry
is self-consistent and generates correct
predictions within the cancer domain.

It does not establish that the evolutionary
and life-construction extensions are correct.
They are the next stage of the derivation,
with the falsifiability conditions explicitly
stated in their respective documents.
